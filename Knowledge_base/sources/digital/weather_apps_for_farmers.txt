WEATHER APPLICATIONS AND FORECAST INTERPRETATION FOR GUJARAT FARMERS

1. INTRODUCTION
Weather is the most important variable in farming. A timely forecast can save a farmer from crop loss due to drought, frost, or unseasonal rain. Gujarat has a diverse climate ranging from arid in Kutch to tropical monsoon in South Gujarat. Understanding weather patterns and using digital tools to access forecasts is essential for modern farming. This guide covers the best weather apps and services for Gujarat farmers, how to interpret forecasts, and how to make farm decisions based on weather predictions.

2. IMD (INDIA METEOROLOGICAL DEPARTMENT) SERVICES

2.1 IMD Website and Apps
The India Meteorological Department is the official source for weather forecasts in India.

IMD Website (mausam.imd.gov.in):
- Current weather conditions for all Gujarat cities and districts
- 5-day weather forecasts updated twice daily
- District-level rainfall forecasts during the monsoon
- Special weather warnings for farmers (heat waves, cold waves, heavy rain, cyclone alerts)
- Satellite images and radar data for cloud monitoring

IMD Apps:
- "Mausam" app: IMD's official weather app; provides current weather, forecasts, and warnings
- "Damini" app: Lightning alert system; warns users about lightning strikes within 40 minutes and 15km radius
- "Meghdoot" app: Specifically designed for farmers; provides weather forecasts and agromet advisories in regional languages including Gujarati
- "Agromet Advisory" portal: District-level agromet advisories for farmers (imdaws.com)

2.2 IMD Agromet Advisory Service (AAS)
IMD issues agromet advisories for every district twice a week (Tuesday and Friday) during the cropping season. These advisories contain:
- Weather forecast for the next 5 days (rainfall, temperature, humidity, wind)
- Advice on irrigation, spraying, planting, harvesting, and pest management based on the forecast
- Warnings about adverse weather (heat, frost, heavy rain, strong winds)
- How to access: Visit imdaws.com, download the Agromet app, or receive SMS advisories from the district agriculture office

2.3 IMD Automatic Weather Stations (AWS) in Gujarat
IMD operates over 100 AWS in Gujarat that provide real-time data on temperature, humidity, rainfall, wind speed, and solar radiation. Farmers near these stations get highly accurate local forecasts. Major AWS locations include Ahmedabad, Vadodara, Surat, Rajkot, Bhavnagar, Jamnagar, Bhuj, Junagadh, and many taluka-level stations.

3. SKYMET WEATHER SERVICES

3.1 Skymet Weather App and Website
Skymet is a private weather company that provides highly accurate forecasts, especially for monsoon rainfall.

Features:
- Hyperlocal weather forecasts (village-level in some areas)
- Monsoon forecasts and updates (critical for Gujarat's kharif planning)
- Rainfall alerts with intensity and duration
- Temperature forecasts with heat index for Gujarat's summer
- Lightning alerts and cyclone tracking
- Satellite imagery for cloud monitoring

Skymet Agri: Specialized agricultural weather service providing:
- Crop-specific weather advisories
- Pest and disease weather indices (when weather conditions favor specific pests)
- Irrigation scheduling based on evapotranspiration data
- Frost alerts for winter crops (mustard, potato in North Gujarat)

Access: skymetweather.com or the Skymet Weather app (Android/iOS)

3.2 Monsoon Forecasting
Skymet is well-known for its monsoon forecasts. Their long-range forecast (April) and update (June) help Gujarat farmers decide:
- When to begin land preparation
- Which crops to plant based on expected rainfall
- Whether to invest in riskier high-value crops or safer drought-tolerant crops

4. OTHER USEFUL WEATHER APPS FOR GUJARAT FARMERS

4.1 AccuWeather
- Highly detailed hourly forecasts
- Severe weather alerts
- 15-day extended forecast
- Good for planning harvest and spraying operations
- Available in Gujarati (language setting)

4.2 Weather Underground
- Hyperlocal weather using personal weather station data
- Useful for farmers in areas without official AWS stations
- Historical weather data for planning

4.3 Windy.com (App and Website)
- Best for wind forecasting (critical for spraying operations)
- Shows wind speed, direction, and gusts at multiple altitudes
- Rainfall radar and cloud cover visualization
- Essential for deciding whether to spray pesticides (never spray if wind > 10 km/h)

4.4 Rain Alarm
- Simple app that alerts when rain is approaching
- Uses radar data to warn of imminent rainfall
- Useful for protecting harvested produce or postponing irrigation

4.5 Google Weather
- Integrated into Google search and Android phones
- Quick daily forecast for any location
- Good for casual checking; less detailed than IMD or Skymet

5. INTERPRETING WEATHER FORECASTS FOR FARMING DECISIONS

5.1 Rainfall Forecasts
- Light rain (<2.5mm): Not sufficient for crop needs; do not skip planned irrigation
- Moderate rain (2.5-7.5mm): May supplement irrigation; reduce irrigation by 25-50%
- Heavy rain (7.5-35mm): Postpone irrigation; check drainage; prepare for possible waterlogging
- Very heavy rain (>35mm): Ensure drainage channels are clear; protect vulnerable crops; delay spraying and harvesting
- Forecast probability: A 60% chance of rain means it is likely to rain in 60% of the forecast area, not a 60% chance at your specific location. Always have a backup plan.

5.2 Temperature Forecasts
- Maximum temperature: Above 40C in Gujarat summer causes heat stress in crops and livestock. Provide shade and extra water.
- Minimum temperature: Below 10C in North Gujarat winter can damage sensitive crops (potato, tomato, papaya). Use frost protection (fogging, smoke, or covers).
- Diurnal range (day-night difference): Large differences (e.g., 35C day, 15C night) can cause physiological disorders in tomatoes and capsicum.

5.3 Humidity Forecasts
- High humidity (>70%): Favorable for fungal diseases (powdery mildew, blight). Avoid overhead irrigation and increase ventilation in greenhouses. Schedule preventive fungicide sprays.
- Low humidity (<30%): Increases evapotranspiration; increase irrigation frequency. Mite and thrip populations increase in dry conditions.

5.4 Wind Forecasts
- Calm (<5 km/h): Ideal for spraying pesticides; droplets settle evenly
- Light breeze (5-10 km/h): Acceptable for spraying; use larger droplet size
- Moderate wind (10-20 km/h): Avoid spraying; drift risk is significant
- Strong wind (>20 km/h): Do not spray; risk of phytotoxicity in neighboring crops and health hazards
- Wind direction: Important for spray drift management; never spray towards neighboring crops or livestock sheds

6. MAKING FARM DECISIONS BASED ON WEATHER

6.1 Planting Decisions
- Kharif planting: If the forecast shows a good monsoon onset (June 15-30), plant long-duration crops (cotton, paddy). If delayed or weak monsoon is forecasted, switch to short-duration crops (moong, cowpea, sesame).
- Rabi planting: If the forecast predicts a warm winter, plant wheat. If cold winter is expected, protect against frost or switch to more cold-tolerant crops (barley, gram).
- Vegetable planting: Use 5-day forecasts to time transplanting after a predicted rainfall, reducing transplant shock.

6.2 Irrigation Scheduling
- Check the 3-day forecast before scheduling irrigation. If rain is predicted, postpone irrigation by 2-3 days.
- During monsoon, use weather forecasts to reduce irrigation frequency by 30-50%.
- In winter, if frost is predicted, irrigate 1-2 days before the frost event; moist soil holds more heat than dry soil.

6.3 Pesticide and Fungicide Spraying
- Check wind speed and rainfall forecast before spraying
- Ideal conditions: Wind 3-8 km/h, no rain forecast for 6 hours, temperature below 30C, humidity 60-70%
- Avoid spraying if rain is predicted within 4 hours; most pesticides need 4-6 hours without rain to be effective
- For fungicides, spray before a predicted rainfall if the disease pressure is high; the rain will help distribute the fungicide

6.4 Harvesting Decisions
- Harvest grains before a predicted rainfall to prevent germination and mold
- Harvest cotton before the monsoon ends; unseasonal rain damages cotton quality
- For perishables (vegetables, fruits), harvest before extreme heat events or before transport delays due to rain
- Monitor the 7-day forecast during harvest season to plan labor and transport

7. GUJARAT-SPECIFIC WEATHER RISKS AND PREPAREDNESS

7.1 Cyclones (June-November)
Gujarat's coastal districts (Kutch, Jamnagar, Porbandar, Junagadh, Bhavnagar, Amreli, Surat, Valsad) are vulnerable to cyclones from the Arabian Sea.
- Monitor IMD cyclone warnings (issued 3-5 days in advance)
- Secure loose structures, greenhouses, and shade nets
- Harvest mature crops before the cyclone arrives
- Drain excess water from fields to prevent lodging
- Protect livestock in sturdy shelters

7.2 Heat Waves (April-June)
Gujarat regularly experiences temperatures above 45C.
- Provide shade and extra drinking water for livestock
- Irrigate sensitive crops (vegetables, flowers) in the evening to reduce heat stress
- Avoid spraying pesticides during peak heat; phytotoxicity risk is high
- Plant heat-tolerant varieties in areas prone to heat waves

7.3 Unseasonal Rain (October-March)
- Protect harvested produce in covered storage
- Cover standing crops with plastic sheets if hail is predicted
- For wheat and gram, unseasonal rain at flowering causes severe disease (rust, blight); apply preventive fungicides if rain is forecasted

7.4 Frost (December-January, North Gujarat)
- Monitor minimum temperature forecasts for North Gujarat and Kutch
- Use smudge pots or smoke generators in orchards
- Irrigate before frost events
- Cover sensitive crops with straw or plastic

8. WEATHER-BASED INSURANCE (WBCIS)
The Weather Based Crop Insurance Scheme (WBCIS) uses automatic weather station data to trigger insurance payouts based on rainfall deficit, temperature extremes, or humidity conditions. Gujarat farmers enrolled in WBCIS receive automatic compensation if weather parameters deviate from the trigger levels, without needing to prove yield loss. Contact your district agriculture office or insurance company (AIC, HDFC Ergo, etc.) to enroll before the season starts.

9. DIGITAL LITERACY TIPS FOR USING WEATHER APPS
- Install 2-3 apps (IMD Meghdoot, Skymet, and Windy) for cross-checking forecasts
- Learn to read radar images: Green patches indicate light rain; yellow and red indicate moderate to heavy rain
- Set up alerts for severe weather (rain, heat, wind) on your phone
- Share forecasts with fellow farmers via WhatsApp groups
- Attend training sessions at KVKs on using weather apps and interpreting forecasts

10. SUMMARY: BEST WEATHER RESOURCES FOR GUJARAT FARMERS
- Primary: IMD Meghdoot app and imdaws.com for official agromet advisories
- Monsoon: Skymet for detailed rainfall forecasts and updates
- Spraying: Windy.com for wind and rainfall timing
- Lightning: Damini app for lightning alerts
- Daily: AccuWeather or Google Weather for quick checks
- Advisory: District agriculture office SMS service for local weather advisories in Gujarati
