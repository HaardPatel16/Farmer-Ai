Sprayer maintenance guide Gujarat: Types of sprayers commonly used in Gujarat: hand-operated knapsack sprayer (16-20 litre), battery-operated backpack sprayer (16-20 litre), tractor-mounted boom sprayer (200-500 litre), power sprayer (knapsack engine-type). Daily maintenance after use: empty tank completely (never store chemicals in tank); wash tank 2-3 times with clean water; spray clean water through nozzle and all hoses to flush chemical residue; leave all parts open to dry. Nozzle care: nozzles are the most critical precision part — check for wear by measuring output (compare with new nozzle output); worn nozzle gives 20% or more excess chemical wastage and causes uneven coverage. Never clean nozzle orifice with wire or pin — use soft bristle brush or compressed air only. Replace nozzles when output varies more than 10% from specification. Flat fan nozzles: standard for most Gujarat pesticide application at 40-60 PSI. Hollow cone nozzles: for fungicide and insecticide requiring all-around leaf coverage. Weekly: check diaphragm (hand pump) or piston cup seal for cracks — replace when suction is poor; inspect rubber hose for cracks or hardening. Filter: clean all inline filters after every use — blocked filters cause uneven spray and pump damage. Battery sprayer: charge battery after every use; do not let battery discharge completely; check terminal contacts monthly. Calibration: always calibrate sprayer volume output before first use of season — fill measured water and spray over 100 square metre area and measure output; calibration ensures correct pesticide dose and avoids over or under application.
