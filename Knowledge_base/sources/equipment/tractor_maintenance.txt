Tractor maintenance guide for Gujarat farmers: Daily checks before starting: engine oil level (check dipstick — should be between min and max marks), coolant level (radiator — check when engine is cold), fuel level, tyre pressure (front 20-22 PSI, rear 14-16 PSI for standard tractors — check tractor manual), all lights and horn working, battery terminals clean and tight. Weekly maintenance: clean air filter (tap dust out of dry element; if wet-type, wash and re-oil), check fan belt tension (should have 1cm slack — tight belt damages alternator, loose belt causes overheating), grease all grease nipples (PTO shaft, front axle, lift arm pins — typically 12-15 points on standard tractor), check hydraulic oil level, inspect all tyre condition for cuts or uneven wear. Monthly maintenance: change engine oil if running above 250 hours since last change or per manufacturer interval (most common: every 250 hours); clean fuel filter; check battery electrolyte level and top up with distilled water if low; inspect brake pedals and linkage; check clutch free play adjustment; clean and inspect spark plugs (petrol tractors) or injector nozzles (diesel). Annual servicing: full engine tune-up, gearbox oil change, rear axle oil, fuel injection pump calibration, cooling system flush. Gujarat common issues: overheating in summer — ensure radiator fins are clear of dust and chaff; check water pump impeller annually. After heavy monsoon field work: clean all electrical connections with contact spray; dry out wet areas to prevent rust. Government workshop support: ATMA workshops and KVK agriculture machinery training available in most districts. SMAM scheme subsidises tractor servicing cost for small farmers.
