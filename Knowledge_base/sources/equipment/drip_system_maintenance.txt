Drip irrigation system maintenance guide Gujarat: Daily operation checks: check main filter (sand filter and screen filter) pressure differential — if pressure drop exceeds 5 metres head, backwash sand filter and clean screen. Check if all laterals (drip lines) are getting water — walk field and check emitter drip at multiple points. Check injection pump/venturi if fertigation is running — ensure fertilizer solution is drawing in. Weekly: backwash sand filter thoroughly (open backwash valve for 3-5 minutes); clean screen filter (remove and wash with clean water); flush all sub-main pipes by opening end caps for 5 minutes; check dripper output at 5-10 random points in field — blocked drippers mean chemical or root blockage. Monthly: acid treatment to prevent chemical clogging — inject phosphoric acid 2.5 ml/litre through system for 15 minutes, then flush with clean water for 30 minutes (kills algae and mineral deposits); chlorination — inject sodium hypochlorite solution 0.5g/litre through system for 20 minutes monthly to prevent algal and bacterial growth in pipes (Gujarat's warm water promotes rapid algae growth in drip lines). Annual: pressure test entire system; replace any cracked or leaking laterals; replace all emitters that show greater than 15% output variation; inspect and clean venturi injector or fertilizer tank connection. Monsoon shutdown: flush entire system thoroughly; store injection equipment in dry place; cover sub-mains and laterals if surface lines. Gujarat subsidy: micro-irrigation system maintenance training and subsidy for repairs available through GGRC (Gujarat Green Revolution Company) — contact district agriculture office.
