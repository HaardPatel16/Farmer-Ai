WATER-CONSERVING CROPS FOR GUJARAT

1. INTRODUCTION
Water scarcity is the defining challenge of Gujarat agriculture. With over 70% of the state's net sown area dependent on rainfall and groundwater, choosing crops that match water availability is the single most important decision a farmer makes. This guide presents low-water-requirement crops suitable for Gujarat's different agro-climatic zones and explains how crop substitution can maintain or even increase farm income while reducing water use.

2. GUJARAT'S AGRO-CLIMATIC ZONES AND WATER AVAILABILITY

Zone 1: South Gujarat (Heavy Rainfall, >1500mm)
- Crops: Rice, sugarcane, banana, mango, vegetables
- Water availability: Generally sufficient; focus on drainage rather than conservation
- Substitution potential: Replace some rice with low-water cereals or pulses

Zone 2: Central Gujarat (Moderate Rainfall, 800-1200mm)
- Crops: Cotton, tobacco, castor, bajra, groundnut
- Water availability: Moderate; canal irrigation supplements rainfall
- Substitution potential: Replace cotton with castor or sesame in water-scarce years

Zone 3: North Gujarat (Low Rainfall, 500-800mm; Groundwater-dependent)
- Crops: Cotton, wheat, cumin, mustard, bajra, castor
- Water availability: Severe groundwater depletion; most water-stressed zone
- Substitution potential: High priority for water-saving crops

Zone 4: Saurashtra (Low-Moderate Rainfall, 500-1000mm; Groundwater and canal)
- Crops: Groundnut, cotton, bajra, sesame, cumin, onion
- Water availability: Improving with Narmada canal; still water-scarce in many areas
- Substitution potential: Replace cotton with groundnut or castor in dry years

Zone 5: Kutch (Very Low Rainfall, <400mm; Extreme scarcity)
- Crops: Bajra, guar, moth bean, castor, date palm (in some areas)
- Water availability: Extremely limited; every drop counts
- Substitution potential: Focus entirely on drought-tolerant crops and agroforestry

3. LOW WATER REQUIREMENT CROPS FOR KHARIF (MONSOON SEASON)

3.1 Pearl Millet (Bajra) - Water Requirement: 250-350mm
- The most drought-tolerant cereal; thrives in Gujarat's hot, dry climate
- Grows well in all zones, especially Kutch, Saurashtra, and North Gujarat
- Yields: 15-25 quintals per hectare under rainfed conditions; 30-40 quintals with one irrigation
- Suitable varieties: GHB 558, GHB 732, GHB 744 (short-duration, drought-tolerant)
- Marketing: Strong demand for fodder and grain; Gujarat is a major bajra producer
- Water-saving tip: Sow with the first rains; avoid pre-sowing irrigation

3.2 Sorghum (Jowar) - Water Requirement: 300-400mm
- Dual-purpose grain and fodder crop; excellent for mixed farming systems
- Drought-tolerant varieties available for Gujarat's conditions
- Yields: 20-30 quintals grain per hectare; 100-150 quintals fodder
- Suitable for Kutch, Saurashtra, and North Gujarat
- Water-saving tip: Grows on residual moisture; one irrigation at flowering can significantly boost yield

3.3 Green Gram (Moong) - Water Requirement: 250-300mm
- Short-duration (60-70 days) pulse; fits between monsoon showers
- Fixes atmospheric nitrogen; improves soil fertility for the next crop
- Yields: 8-12 quintals per hectare
- Price advantage: Premium market price due to protein demand; good for export
- Water-saving tip: Can be grown entirely on rainfall in most of Gujarat; no irrigation needed

3.4 Black Gram (Urad) - Water Requirement: 300-350mm
- Another short-duration pulse; 70-80 days duration
- Good for intercropping with cotton or groundnut
- Yields: 8-10 quintals per hectare
- Water-saving tip: Sow with the monsoon; requires irrigation only if dry spell exceeds 3 weeks

3.5 Guar (Cluster Bean) - Water Requirement: 250-350mm
- Native to Gujarat and Rajasthan; extremely drought-tolerant
- Industrial demand for guar gum (used in oil drilling, food, and pharmaceuticals)
- Yields: 10-15 quintals per hectare (grain); 100-150 quintals fodder
- Water-saving tip: No irrigation needed in most Gujarat areas; the most water-efficient cash crop

3.6 Castor - Water Requirement: 350-500mm
- Gujarat is India's largest castor producer; Saurashtra is the castor belt
- Deep-rooted crop that can extract water from lower soil layers
- Yields: 15-25 quintals per hectare
- Water-saving tip: One irrigation at flowering and pod development is sufficient; avoid vegetative-stage irrigation

3.7 Sesame (Til) - Water Requirement: 300-400mm
- Short-duration oilseed; 80-90 days
- Excellent for water-scarce years when groundnut or cotton cannot be grown
- Yields: 5-8 quintals per hectare; high oil content (45-50%)
- Water-saving tip: Can be grown entirely on residual moisture after early monsoon rains

3.8 Moth Bean (Matki) - Water Requirement: 200-300mm
- The most drought-tolerant pulse; survives on 200mm rainfall
- Native to Gujarat and Rajasthan; grows in sandy soils
- Yields: 5-10 quintals per hectare
- Water-saving tip: Requires no irrigation; the ultimate water-saving crop for Kutch and arid areas

4. LOW WATER REQUIREMENT CROPS FOR RABI (WINTER SEASON)

4.1 Chickpea (Gram) - Water Requirement: 250-350mm
- Most important rabi pulse of Gujarat; grown in North Gujarat and Saurashtra
- Grows mostly on residual soil moisture; one irrigation may be needed in dry winters
- Yields: 15-25 quintals per hectare
- Suitable varieties: GJG 1, GJG 3, GNG 1581 (wilt-resistant)
- Water-saving tip: Avoid excessive irrigation; chickpea is sensitive to waterlogging

4.2 Mustard - Water Requirement: 250-350mm
- Important oilseed for North Gujarat and Saurashtra
- Cold-tolerant and drought-tolerant; thrives on residual moisture
- Yields: 12-18 quintals per hectare
- Water-saving tip: Irrigate only at flowering and siliqua formation if winter is dry

4.3 Safflower - Water Requirement: 200-300mm
- Most drought-tolerant oilseed; deep-rooted system extracts subsoil moisture
- Yields: 10-15 quintals per hectare; also produces good quality fodder
- Water-saving tip: No irrigation needed in most Gujarat conditions; the best rabi crop for water-scarce years

4.4 Barley - Water Requirement: 250-350mm
- Drought-tolerant cereal; can replace wheat in very dry years
- Yields: 30-40 quintals per hectare; good for fodder and grain
- Water-saving tip: Requires 30-40% less water than wheat; good for saline areas too

4.5 Cumin - Water Requirement: 300-400mm
- High-value spice crop for Gujarat (North Gujarat and Kutch are major producers)
- Needs 3-4 light irrigations; cannot tolerate heavy irrigation
- Yields: 5-8 quintals per hectare; market price is very high (Rs. 15,000-25,000 per quintal)
- Water-saving tip: Use sprinkler or drip irrigation to avoid overwatering and fungal diseases

5. CROP SUBSTITUTION STRATEGIES

5.1 High-Water to Low-Water Crop Substitutions
- Replace sugarcane (1500-2000mm) with sweet sorghum or fodder sorghum (400-500mm)
- Replace rice (1200-1500mm) with pearl millet or sorghum (300-400mm)
- Replace cotton (800-1000mm) with castor (350-500mm) or sesame (300-400mm)
- Replace wheat (500-600mm) with barley (250-350mm) or chickpea (250-350mm)
- Replace groundnut (500-700mm) with sesame (300-400mm) or guar (250-350mm)

5.2 Income-Neutral Substitution
Many farmers fear that low-water crops are less profitable. However, the math often favors substitution when water costs and electricity costs are included:
- Cumin (5 quintals x Rs. 20,000 = Rs. 1,00,000/ha, 350mm water) vs. Wheat (40 quintals x Rs. 2,000 = Rs. 80,000/ha, 550mm water): Cumin uses less water and earns more
- Guar (12 quintals x Rs. 5,000 = Rs. 60,000/ha, 300mm water) vs. Cotton (15 quintals x Rs. 6,000 = Rs. 90,000/ha but with 5x more water cost): Net profit may be comparable when water and electricity are accounted for

5.3 Contingency Planning Based on Monsoon Progress
- If monsoon is delayed beyond July 15: Switch from cotton/groundnut to short-duration moong, cowpea, or sesame
- If rainfall is 50% below normal by August: Switch to guar, moth bean, or horse gram
- If soil moisture is depleted after kharif: Replace wheat with chickpea or safflower in rabi
- If groundwater is critically low (water table below 60m): Consider shifting from annual crops to agroforestry (neem, prosopis, acacia) or permanent pasture

6. AGROFORESTRY AS WATER-CONSERVING ALTERNATIVE
- Multipurpose trees (neem, acacia, prosopis) require minimal water once established
- Silvopasture (trees + grass + livestock) is highly water-efficient
- Fruit trees like ber, aonla, and custard apple are drought-tolerant and profitable
- Gujarat's Saurashtra and Kutch have successful agroforestry models with minimum irrigation

7. INTERCROPPING AND MIXED CROPPING FOR WATER EFFICIENCY
- Cotton + black gram: The legume fixes nitrogen and provides early income while using less water
- Castor + green gram: Short-duration pulse harvested before castor needs water
- Bajra + cowpea: Dual-purpose grain and fodder system with efficient water use
- Coconut + cocoa (South Gujarat): Multi-story cropping uses vertical space and rainfall efficiently

8. GOVERNMENT SCHEMES SUPPORTING CROP DIVERSIFICATION
- NMNF (National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture): Supports crop diversification and alternative farming systems
- Gujarat State Agriculture Policy: Incentives for shifting from water-intensive crops to pulses and oilseeds in over-exploited talukas
- PM-AASHA: Price support for pulses and oilseeds ensures minimum prices for water-saving crops
- MSP for bajra, moong, urad, and other low-water crops provides income security
