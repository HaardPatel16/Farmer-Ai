1. INTRODUCTION
Drip irrigation is the most efficient water delivery method, saving 40-60% water while increasing yields by 20-40%. Gujarat has over 15 lakh hectares under micro-irrigation.

2. COMPONENTS OF DRIP SYSTEM
Water Source:
- Well, borewell, canal, farm pond
- Water quality testing essential
- Filtration requirement based on source

Pump and Head Unit:
- Pump: Centrifugal/submersible
- Filtration: Screen/sand/media filters
- Fertilizer tank: Venturi/injection system
- Control valves: Air release, non-return, pressure relief

Mainline and Sub-main:
- PVC/HDPE pipes
- Diameter: 63-110 mm mainline, 32-75 mm sub-main
- Pressure rating: 4-6 kg/cm2

Laterals:
- 12-20 mm diameter
- Laid along crop rows
- Dripper spacing: matches plant spacing

Drippers:
- Types: Online, inline, pressure-compensating
- Discharge: 2, 4, 8 LPH
- Selection based on crop and soil

3. SYSTEM DESIGN STEPS
Step 1: Survey
- Field dimensions and shape
- Crop type and spacing
- Water source and quality
- Topography

Step 2: Water Requirement Calculation
- Peak crop water demand (mm/day)
- Operating hours per day
- System capacity calculation

Step 3: Hydraulic Design
- Pipe sizing
- Pressure requirements
- Flow rate calculations
- Head loss calculations

Step 4: Component Selection
- Filter type and size
- Dripper type and spacing
- Pipe diameters
- Fittings and accessories

4. INSTALLATION GUIDE
Pre-installation:
- Mark layout on field
- Excavate trenches for mainline
- Install pump house
- Connect electrical supply

Installation Sequence:
1. Lay and join mainline
2. Install filter station
3. Lay sub-mains with control valves
4. Install laterals with drippers
5. Flush system thoroughly
6. Pressure test all lines
7. Calibrate dripper discharge

5. COST ESTIMATES
Small farm (<1 ha): Rs. 75,000-1,25,000/ha
Medium farm (1-5 ha): Rs. 60,000-90,000/ha
Large farm (>5 ha): Rs. 45,000-70,000/ha

Subsidy Available:
- PMKSY: 55% for small farmers, 45% for others
- Gujarat state: Additional 10% for SC/ST
- Total subsidy: Up to 65% for eligible farmers

6. OPERATION AND MAINTENANCE
Daily:
- Check filter pressure differential
- Flush filters if needed
- Check system pressure
- Monitor for leaks

Weekly:
- Flush lateral ends (2-3 minutes)
- Check dripper flow uniformity
- Clean fertilizer tank
- Inspect for physical damage

Monthly:
- Acid flush for clogging prevention
- Check pump performance
- Inspect pipe joints
- Calibrate system

Seasonal (Pre-season):
- Complete system flush
- Replace damaged components
- Service pump and filters
- Pressure test

7. FERTIGATION
Benefits:
- 25-30% fertilizer saving
- Uniform application
- Spoon-feeding possible
- Labor saving

Compatible Fertilizers:
- Urea (completely soluble)
- DAP (di-ammonium phosphate)
- Potassium nitrate
- Water-soluble NPK grades
- Micronutrient chelates

Application Schedule:
- Divide total dose into 8-10 applications
- Apply during irrigation middle phase
- Flush with plain water after fertigation
- Avoid mixing incompatible fertilizers

8. TROUBLESHOOTING
Clogging:
- Physical: Screen filter maintenance
- Chemical: Acid flushing (HCl/citric acid)
- Biological: Chlorination (10 ppm)

Uneven Watering:
- Check pressure at lateral ends
- Clean clogged drippers
- Ensure level field

Leakages:
- Repair with couplers
- Replace damaged sections
- Secure connections

9. CROP-SPECIFIC RECOMMENDATIONS
Vegetables:
- Dripper spacing: 20-30 cm
- Discharge: 2-4 LPH
- Scheduling: Daily

Orchards:
- 2-4 drippers per tree
- Discharge: 4-8 LPH
- Scheduling: Alternate days

Field Crops:
- Inline laterals
- Dripper spacing: 30-60 cm
- Discharge: 2-4 LPH

10. BENEFITS IN GUJARAT CONTEXT
Water Saving: 40-60% compared to flood irrigation
Yield Increase: 20-40% due to optimal moisture
Quality Improvement: Uniform fruit size, better color
Weed Reduction: Water only to crop root zone
Labor Saving: Reduced irrigation labor by 70%
Energy Saving: Less water pumping needed
