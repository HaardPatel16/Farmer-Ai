FARM POND CONSTRUCTION GUIDE FOR GUJARAT FARMERS

1. INTRODUCTION
Farm ponds (khet talavadi) are the most cost-effective water harvesting structure for individual farmers in Gujarat. A well-designed farm pond can store 10-50 lakh liters of rainwater, providing critical irrigation during dry spells, protecting against drought, and even supporting fish culture. Under various government schemes, Gujarat farmers can receive 50-70% subsidy for farm pond construction. This guide covers design, construction, lining, cost estimation, and maintenance.

2. FARM POND DESIGN PRINCIPLES

2.1 Site Selection
- Choose the lowest point in the farm to capture maximum natural runoff
- The catchment area should be at least 5-10 times the pond area to ensure adequate inflow
- Ensure the pond is not too close to the farm boundary (minimum 5m) to prevent encroachment or undermining of neighbor's land
- Avoid sites with heavy clay that is difficult to excavate unless machinery is available
- Avoid areas with high water table (can cause seepage problems) or rocky strata (expensive excavation)
- The site should be accessible for excavation equipment and for future desilting

2.2 Size Calculation
The size of the farm pond depends on:
- Farm area: Larger farms need larger ponds
- Rainfall pattern: Low and erratic rainfall requires larger storage relative to catchment
- Crop water requirement: Higher-value crops (vegetables, horticulture) need more reliable water supply

Standard sizes for Gujarat conditions:
- Small farm (1-2 hectares): 15m x 15m x 2.5m = 562 cubic meters (5.6 lakh liters)
- Medium farm (3-5 hectares): 20m x 20m x 3m = 1,200 cubic meters (12 lakh liters)
- Large farm (5-10 hectares): 25m x 25m x 3.5m = 2,187 cubic meters (22 lakh liters)
- Orchard/vegetable farm (2-3 hectares): 20m x 15m x 3m = 900 cubic meters (9 lakh liters)

Design formula: Storage volume = Catchment area x Runoff coefficient x Design rainfall
For Gujarat: Runoff coefficient = 0.15-0.25 for flat land, 0.25-0.35 for sloping land
Design rainfall = 50% of average monsoon rainfall (for drought-year security)

Example: A 5-hectare farm in Saurashtra (average rainfall 600mm) on flat land:
Storage needed = 50,000 sqm x 0.20 x 0.30m = 3,000 cubic meters (30 lakh liters)
This would require a pond of approximately 25m x 25m x 4.8m, or two smaller ponds.

2.3 Design Specifications
- Side slopes: 1:1 (45 degrees) for clayey soils; 1.5:1 for sandy soils; 2:1 for loose soils
- Freeboard: 0.5m above the maximum water level to prevent overtopping during intense rain
- Bottom slope: 1:500 to 1:1000 towards the outlet to facilitate drainage
- Inlet: Stone-pitched channel with a silt trap at the pond entry
- Outlet: Sluice pipe or valve for controlled water release; 150-200mm diameter HDPE or RCC pipe
- Overflow: Stone masonry or RCC weir on the downstream side; sized to pass peak flood flow
- Embankment: If the pond is excavated with side casting, the excavated soil can form a ring bund (embankment) increasing capacity by 20-30%

3. CONSTRUCTION METHODS

3.1 Excavation
- Equipment: JCB/backhoe for most sizes; for large ponds (above 2,000 cubic meters), use an excavator or dozer
- Depth: Excavate in 0.5m lifts; do not dig the full depth in one pass to prevent slope collapse
- Soil disposal: Use excavated soil for:
  a) Forming ring bund around the pond
  b) Leveling low spots in the farm
  c) Filling borrow pits
  d) Making raised beds for vegetable cultivation
- Slope finishing: Trim slopes to the designed angle and compact with a plate compactor or rammer
- Bottom preparation: Remove loose soil, roots, and organic matter; scarify and compact the bottom 0.3m

3.2 Inlet and Silt Trap Construction
- Silt trap dimensions: 2m x 2m x 1.5m deep, located before the main pond
- Silt trap is separated from the main pond by a permeable wall (stone or brick with gaps) that allows water but traps sediment
- Clean the silt trap after every monsoon or when it is half full
- Inlet channel: Stone-pitched or concrete-lined to prevent erosion; gradient 1:100 to 1:200
- Install a coarse mesh screen at the inlet to prevent debris and animals from entering

3.3 Outlet and Overflow
- Install a sluice valve at the deepest point for complete drainage
- The outlet pipe should have a filter screen to prevent fish or debris from clogging the delivery system
- Overflow weir: Constructed in stone masonry or RCC; crest level at the designed full supply level
- Provide a stone-pitched apron downstream of the overflow to prevent scour

4. POND LINING

4.1 When to Line
- If the soil has high permeability (sandy loam, sandy soil, gravelly soil): Lining is essential
- If the pond is for storing water for long periods (months): Lining reduces seepage losses by 80-90%
- If the pond is in a black cotton soil area: Natural clay may provide sufficient sealing, but lining is still recommended for long-term storage
- If fish culture is planned: Lining prevents nutrient loss and maintains water quality

4.2 Lining Options and Costs

Option 1: HDPE Geomembrane (Most Popular)
- Thickness: 0.3mm for small ponds, 0.5mm for medium, 0.75-1.0mm for large or rocky-bottom ponds
- Cost: Rs. 40-80 per sqm depending on thickness (including installation)
- Installation: Place on a smooth, stone-free surface with a 100mm sand base; anchor in trenches at the top of the embankment; weld sheets together
- Life: 10-15 years with proper care
- Advantages: Excellent impermeability, relatively low cost, easy to repair

Option 2: PVC Geomembrane
- Thickness: 0.5-1.0mm
- Cost: Rs. 50-100 per sqm
- More flexible than HDPE but less resistant to UV and puncture
- Good for complex shapes but requires more careful installation

Option 3: Concrete Lining (RCC or PCC)
- Thickness: 75-100mm for PCC, 100-150mm for RCC
- Cost: Rs. 300-500 per sqm (expensive but very durable)
- Best for small ponds or pond sections where mechanical damage is likely
- Life: 20-30 years with proper construction

Option 4: Puddle Clay (Traditional)
- Material: Local clay soil with at least 30% clay content
- Thickness: 300-500mm compacted in layers
- Cost: Rs. 50-100 per sqm (mostly labor cost)
- Best for areas with good clay availability and low-cost labor
- Life: 5-10 years; requires periodic repair

Option 5: Soil-Cement Lining
- Mix: 8-10% cement with local soil, compacted in 100mm layers
- Cost: Rs. 100-150 per sqm
- Good balance between cost and durability for medium-sized ponds

5. COST ESTIMATION (2024 rates, Gujarat)

Example: 20m x 20m x 3m pond (1,200 cubic meters) with HDPE lining
- Excavation (1,200 cubic meters @ Rs. 100/cubic meter): Rs. 1,20,000
- HDPE lining (0.5mm, 600 sqm @ Rs. 60/sqm): Rs. 36,000
- Inlet/silt trap (stone masonry): Rs. 15,000
- Outlet pipe and valve: Rs. 8,000
- Overflow weir: Rs. 10,000
- Labor for finishing and installation: Rs. 20,000
- Total cost: Rs. 2,09,000
- After 50% subsidy: Rs. 1,04,500
- After 70% subsidy (small farmer): Rs. 62,700

Cost per cubic meter of storage: Rs. 170-200 (with lining), which is highly cost-effective compared to borewell drilling.

6. FISH CULTURE IN FARM PONDS
Adding fish culture to farm ponds provides additional income and helps control aquatic weeds and mosquitoes.

Suitable species for Gujarat:
- Indian major carps: Rohu (Labeo rohita), Catla (Catla catla), Mrigal (Cirrhinus mrigala)
- Exotic carps: Common carp, silver carp, grass carp (for weed control)
- Tilapia: Hardy and fast-growing; suitable for small ponds
- Catfish: Magur and singhi are hardy but require careful management

Stocking density: 5,000-10,000 fingerlings per hectare of pond area (water spread)

Feeding: Supplementary feed with rice bran, groundnut cake, and commercial fish feed

Expected production: 1,500-3,000 kg per hectare per year with proper feeding and management

7. MAINTENANCE AND MANAGEMENT

7.1 Pre-Monsoon Maintenance (May-June)
- Inspect the embankment and lining for damage; repair cracks and holes
- Clean the silt trap and inlet channel
- Check and grease the outlet valve
- Ensure the overflow is clear and functional
- Remove aquatic weeds from the pond bottom (if dry) or use grass carp (if filled)

7.2 During Monsoon
- Monitor inflow and water level; if the pond is filling too fast, check that the overflow is working
- Remove floating debris from the inlet screen daily during heavy rain
- After the first heavy rain, check for erosion on the embankment and repair immediately

7.3 Post-Monsoon and Dry Season
- Record the maximum water level reached; this helps in redesign if the pond is undersized or oversized
- Use water judiciously; prioritize critical irrigation for high-value crops or sensitive growth stages
- If the pond is for drinking water, maintain water quality by preventing livestock entry and contamination
- Desilt the pond every 3-5 years or when silt accumulation reduces capacity by more than 20%

8. GOVERNMENT SCHEMES AND SUBSIDIES

8.1 PMKSY (Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana)
- Component: Watershed Development (WDC)
- Subsidy: 50% for general farmers, 60% for SC/ST/small farmers
- Maximum subsidy: Rs. 1.5 lakh per pond
- Application: Through the District Watershed Development Office or Gram Panchayat

8.2 Sujalam Sufalam Jal Sanchay Abhiyan (Gujarat State)
- Focus on community and farm-level water harvesting
- Additional state subsidy beyond PMKSY in some districts
- Contact: District Development Officer (Rural Development) or Taluka Development Officer

8.3 MGNREGA
- Labor component for farm pond excavation can be paid through MGNREGA
- The material component (lining, pipes, valves) is paid by the farmer or other schemes
- Minimum 100 days of labor guaranteed; useful for labor-intensive clay lining work

9. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
- Building the pond on the highest point of the farm: It will not capture any runoff
- Insufficient side slopes: Steep slopes collapse during the monsoon, filling the pond with soil
- No overflow provision: Results in embankment breach and flooding downstream
- No silt trap: The pond fills with sediment within 2-3 years, losing capacity
- Poor lining installation: HDPE liner with wrinkles or insufficient anchoring will tear and leak
- Using the pond as a drainage dump: Never dispose of pesticide containers, chemical waste, or sewage into the farm pond
- Neglecting maintenance: A pond that is not maintained will fail within 5 years; a maintained pond lasts 15-20 years
