================================================================================
CENTRAL SAURASHTRA AGRO-CLIMATIC ZONE — COMPLETE REGIONAL PROFILE
================================================================================

1. REGION OVERVIEW & LOCATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Central Saurashtra is one of the most agriculturally significant yet water-stressed
agro-climatic zones of Gujarat. It encompasses the districts of Surendranagar,
Botad, Morbi, and parts of Rajkot district. Geographically, this zone lies in the
heart of the Saurashtra peninsula, characterised by gently undulating terrain with
scattered hillocks and extensive plains. It serves as a transitional zone between
the more humid coastal Saurashtra and the arid western Saurashtra regions. The
zone is heavily dependent on rainfed agriculture, with limited access to major
river systems, making water management the central challenge for farmers.

2. CLIMATE & RAINFALL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Rainfall Pattern: The zone receives low to moderate rainfall ranging from 500mm to
650mm annually, with the majority concentrated during the south-west monsoon
(June to September). The coefficient of rainfall variability is high, often exceeding
30%, which leads to frequent meteorological droughts.

Temperature: Summer temperatures (April–June) range between 38°C and 44°C, with
extreme peaks touching 46°C during heatwaves. Winter temperatures (December–February)
are mild, ranging from 10°C to 28°C. The high summer temperatures accelerate
evapotranspiration, further stressing water resources.

Humidity: Relative humidity is low (25–40%) during the hot summer months, rising
to 60–80% during the monsoon. The dry summer winds (locally called "loo") cause
significant moisture stress in standing crops.

Drought/Flood Risk: Drought is the dominant climatic risk. The zone experiences
moderate to severe drought once every 3–4 years. Flooding is rare but localized
waterlogging can occur in low-lying areas after intense monsoon showers. IMD
advisories recommend kharif sowing only after assured rainfall of 80mm within a
week.

3. SOIL CHARACTERISTICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The dominant soil type is medium black cotton soil (vertisol), with patches of
shallow black soil and rocky outcrops. The soil is clay-rich (40–50% clay content),
which imparts high water-holding capacity but also causes poor drainage and
waterlogging in depressions. Soil depth varies from 30cm to 80cm. Fertility status:
moderate in potassium, low to moderate in available phosphorus, and generally low
in organic matter (0.3–0.6%). Zinc deficiency is widespread. Soil crusting after
heavy rains can impede seedling emergence in groundnut.

4. MAJOR CROPS & CROP ROTATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Kharif Crops:
  • Groundnut — the most important crop; Gujarat-1, GG-7, GG-20, and ICGV-91114
    are recommended varieties. Sowing window: June 20 to July 15.
  • Cotton — Bt cotton dominates on medium-heavy soils; popular hybrids include
    RCH-2, Bunny, and various BG-II varieties. Sowing: June 15 to July 10.
  • Bajra (Pearl Millet) — GHB-538, GHB-732 on lighter soils; drought escape crop.
  • Sesame (Til) — GT-1, GT-2 as mixed or intercrop with groundnut.
  • Castor — GCH-4, GCH-7 on deeper soils with residual moisture.

Rabi Crops (limited, on residual moisture or irrigation):
  • Wheat — GW-273, GW-322 on check-dam irrigated pockets.
  • Chickpea — GG-1, GG-2 on medium black soils.
  • Cumin — high-value spice crop in irrigated areas.

Recommended Rotations:
  • Groundnut – Wheat (irrigated)
  • Cotton – Chickpea
  • Bajra – Gram (on lighter soils)
  • Groundnut + Castor (intercropping 6:2 ratio)

5. WATER RESOURCES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Groundwater is the primary source of irrigation, but water tables have declined
by 2–4 metres over the past two decades due to over-extraction. The zone has no
major river canal command except minor tributaries. Check dams, farm ponds, and
percolation tanks are the backbone of water harvesting. The Sujalam Sufalam and
Sauni Yojana schemes have improved canal and pipeline connectivity in some talukas.
Lift irrigation from seasonal rivers (Bhogavo, Demi) is practiced in limited areas.

6. AGRICULTURAL PRACTICES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sowing Windows: Kharif sowing is strictly tied to monsoon onset — typically
June 20 to July 15. Delayed sowing beyond July 20 significantly reduces groundnut
yields.

Irrigation Methods: Drip irrigation is strongly promoted for cotton and groundnut
under the PM-KISAN and GGRC (Gujarat Green Revolution Company) subsidy schemes.
Sprinkler irrigation is used for wheat and fodder. Micro-irrigation coverage has
expanded significantly in the last decade.

Intercropping: Groundnut + Castor (6:2) and Groundnut + Cotton (1:1 in some
areas) are common. Mixed cropping of bajra + pigeon pea is practiced in marginal
areas.

Soil & Water Conservation: Contour bunding, graded bunding, and farm pond construction
are essential. Laser land levelling is gaining popularity for water-efficient
irrigation.

7. COMMON CHALLENGES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  • Pests: Spodoptera in cotton, leaf miner in groundnut, white grub (Holotrichia)
    in groundnut during monsoon.
  • Diseases: Tikka leaf spot and collar rot in groundnut; wilt and bollworm
    (despite Bt) in cotton.
  • Weather: Delayed monsoon, mid-season dry spells (July–August breaks), and
    terminal drought during pod-filling in groundnut.
  • Soil: Zinc deficiency, iron chlorosis in calcareous patches, soil crusting
    and poor drainage in black cotton soils.
  • Groundwater: Salinity ingress in coastal fringe talukas; fluoride contamination
    in some parts of Surendranagar.

8. GOVERNMENT SCHEMES APPLICABLE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  • Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY) — micro-irrigation subsidies
  • Mukhya Mantri Sujalam Sufalam Jal Sanchay Yojana — water harvesting structures
  • Soil Health Card Scheme — free soil testing and fertilizer recommendations
  • National Food Security Mission (NFSM) — pulses and oilseeds
  • Gujarat State Agricultural Marketing Board (GSAMB) — APMC infrastructure
  • Kisan Call Centre (KCC) — 1800-180-1551 for crop advisory
  • Crop Insurance: PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana) — mandatory for
    loanee farmers, optional for others.

9. DISTRICTS INCLUDED IN THIS ZONE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  • Surendranagar (entire district)
  • Botad (entire district)
  • Morbi (entire district)
  • Rajkot district (partially — Dhoraji, Upleta, Jetpur talukas predominantly;
    Rajkot city and surrounding areas fall under Middle Gujarat zone influence)
