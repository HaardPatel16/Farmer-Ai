1. INTRODUCTION
Dairy farming is Gujarat's economic backbone. Amul - the world's largest dairy cooperative - originated here. Gujarat produces over 15 million tonnes of milk annually.

2. SETTING UP A DAIRY FARM
Location Selection:
- Near milk collection center
- Good road connectivity
- Reliable water source
- Electricity availability
- Away from pollution sources

Herd Size Planning:
Small: 2-5 animals (family income)
Medium: 10-25 animals (commercial)
Large: 50+ animals (organized farm)

Infrastructure Required:
- Cattle shed (open housing system)
- Fodder storage
- Feed preparation area
- Milking parlor or area
- Manure pit
- Water storage
- Office/record room

3. HOUSING DESIGN
Open Housing (Recommended for Gujarat):
- Orientation: East-West length
- Floor: Concrete, 1:60 slope
- Manger: 60 cm wide, 45 cm height
- Water trough: 60 cm wide per 10 animals
- Space per animal:
  * Adult cow: 3.5 x 2.0 m
  * Calf: 1.5 x 1.0 m
  * Heifer: 2.5 x 1.5 m
- Roof: 3.5-4.5 m height, asbestos/GI sheet
- Overhang: 1 m on all sides
- Ventilation: 1.5 sq m per 10 cows

Fencing:
- Boundary: Barbed wire/concrete wall
- Internal: Pipe/rails
- Gates: 3 m wide for tractor access

4. HERD COMPOSITION
Ideal Ratio:
- Milking cows: 50%
- Dry cows: 15%
- Pregnant heifers: 15%
- Calves/heifers: 15%
- Bulls: 5% (or AI use)

5. DAILY ROUTINE
Morning (5:00 AM - 9:00 AM):
- Shed cleaning
- Feeding green fodder
- Milking
- Feed concentrate
- Record keeping

Midday (9:00 AM - 3:00 PM):
- Water availability
- Heat detection
- Fodder cutting
- Maintenance

Evening (3:00 PM - 8:00 PM):
- Feeding green fodder
- Milking
- Concentrate feeding
- Shed locking

6. FEEDING MANAGEMENT
Green Fodder (40-50% of diet):
- Lucerne/berseem in winter
- Cowpea/jowar bajra in summer
- Hybrid napier year-round
- Quantity: 30-40 kg/animal/day

Dry Fodder (20-30%):
- Jowar/bajra stover
- Wheat/paddy straw
- Quantity: 5-10 kg/animal/day

Concentrate (20-30%):
- Maize, groundnut cake, wheat bran
- Mineral mixture
- Salt
- Quantity: Based on milk production
  * Maintenance: 1-2 kg
  * Per liter milk: 400-500 g

Water:
- Clean, fresh water always
- Quantity: 60-100 liters/animal/day
- Double in summer

7. MILKING MANAGEMENT
Milking Frequency:
- 2 times: Morning and evening
- 3 times: For high yielders (>20 L)

Milking Procedure:
1. Wash udder with warm water
2. Dry with clean cloth
3. Forestrip (first milk discard)
4. Attach machine or hand milk
5. Post-dip teats
6. Record yield

Milking Hygiene:
- Milker: Clean clothes, trimmed nails
- Equipment: Wash, rinse, sanitize
- Storage: Immediate chilling to 4C
- Transport: Within 2 hours

8. ECONOMICS
Investment (per cow):
- Shed construction: Rs. 25,000-40,000
- Purchase: Rs. 30,000-80,000
- Equipment: Rs. 5,000-10,000

Operating Cost (monthly per cow):
- Feed: Rs. 3,000-5,000
- Labor: Rs. 500-1,000
- Veterinary: Rs. 300-500
- Miscellaneous: Rs. 200-500

Income (monthly per cow):
- Milk: 10 L/day x Rs. 30/L x 30 = Rs. 9,000
- Manure: Rs. 300-500
- Calf value: Rs. 500/month

Net Profit: Rs. 3,000-5,000/cow/month

9. SUCCESS FACTORS
- Quality animals with good genetics
- Proper nutrition and feeding
- Clean milk production
- Regular health care
- Record keeping
- Market linkage (cooperative/private)
- Continuous learning

10. IMPORTANT CONTACTS
- Amul Dairy: Various districts
- Sumul, Banas, Sabar dairies
- Gujarat Dairy Development Board
- State Animal Husbandry Department
- Veterinary colleges
- KVKs with dairy programs
