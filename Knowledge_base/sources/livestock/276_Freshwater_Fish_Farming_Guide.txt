1. INTRODUCTION
Fish farming (aquaculture) is a growing sector in Gujarat. With 160 km coastline and numerous inland water bodies, Gujarat has significant potential for fish production.

2. FISH SPECIES FOR GUJARAT
Indian Major Carps (IMC):
- Catla (Catla catla): Surface feeder, fast growth
- Rohu (Labeo rohita): Column feeder, popular
- Mrigal (Cirrhinus mrigala): Bottom feeder

Exotic Carps:
- Silver carp: Phytoplankton feeder
- Grass carp: Vegetation feeder
- Common carp: Omnivorous, hardy

Other Species:
- Tilapia (Oreochromis niloticus): Hardy, fast breeding
- Pangasius (Pangasianodon hypophthalmus): Air-breathing
- Singh/Magur (Clarias batrachus): Air-breathing, high value

3. POND CONSTRUCTION
Site Selection:
- Water availability year-round
- Soil with good water holding (clay/loam)
- Gentle slope for drainage
- Away from flood-prone areas
- Road access for transport
- Electricity for aeration

Pond Design:
- Size: 0.5-2.0 hectares (ideal)
- Depth: 1.5-2.5 m (deeper in center)
- Shape: Rectangular (3:2 ratio)
- Dykes: 1.5-2 m wide at top
- Inlet/outlet: Properly designed
- Overflow pipe: Emergency drainage

Construction Steps:
1. Clear site of vegetation
2. Mark boundaries
3. Excavate to desired depth
4. Build dykes with excavated soil
5. Install inlet/outlet pipes
6. Compact dykes
7. Lime and fertilize
8. Fill with water
9. Check for leaks
10. Stock fish

4. POND PREPARATION
Pre-stocking:
- Drain and dry for 2-4 weeks
- Remove unwanted fish (mahua oil cake: 2500 kg/ha)
- Lime application: 500-1000 kg/ha (pH correction)
- Fertilize: Organic (cow dung 5000 kg/ha) + Inorganic (urea 50 kg, SSP 50 kg)
- Wait 7-10 days for plankton development

5. STOCKING DENSITY
Composite Culture (recommended):
- Catla: 30% (surface)
- Rohu: 30% (column)
- Mrigal: 20% (bottom)
- Grass carp/Silver carp: 20%
- Total: 8000-10,000 fingerlings/ha

Mono Culture:
- Tilapia: 20,000-30,000/ha
- Pangasius: 30,000-50,000/ha
- Singh: 50,000-80,000/ha

Fingerling Size: 8-10 cm (50-100 g)
Stocking Time: Morning/evening (cool hours)

6. FEEDING MANAGEMENT
Natural Food:
- Plankton (phytoplankton, zooplankton)
- Benthos (bottom organisms)
- Aquatic insects
- Enhanced through fertilization

Supplementary Feed:
- Rice bran: 40%
- Groundnut cake: 30%
- Wheat bran: 20%
- Fish meal: 5%
- Mineral-vitamin mix: 5%

Feeding Rate:
- 5% body weight initially
- Reduce to 2-3% as fish grow
- Feed twice daily (morning and evening)
- Use feeding tray to monitor consumption

7. WATER QUALITY MANAGEMENT
Parameters:
- Dissolved oxygen: >5 ppm
- pH: 7.0-8.5
- Temperature: 25-32C
- Transparency: 30-40 cm
- Ammonia: <0.5 ppm
- Nitrite: <0.1 ppm

Management:
- Aeration: Paddle wheel (4-6 hours/day)
- Water exchange: 10-20% monthly
- Lime: Monthly application (100-200 kg/ha)
- Fertilization: Monthly (maintain plankton)
- Avoid overfeeding (causes pollution)

8. HARVESTING
Signs of Maturity:
- Catla/Rohu: 1-1.5 kg (8-10 months)
- Mrigal: 0.8-1.0 kg (10-12 months)
- Tilapia: 250-500 g (5-6 months)
- Pangasius: 1-2 kg (8-10 months)

Harvesting Methods:
- Drag net (seine net)
- Gill net
- Cast net
- Complete draining

Post-harvest:
- Keep alive in water till sale
- Icing for transport
- Clean and grade
- Record weights

9. DISEASE MANAGEMENT
Preventive Measures:
- Quality seed from certified hatcheries
- Pond disinfection
- Balanced feeding
- Water quality maintenance
- Quarantine new stock
- Avoid overcrowding

Common Diseases:
- Bacterial: Aeromonas, Pseudomonas
- Fungal: Saprolegniasis
- Parasitic: Argulus (fish lice), Lernea (anchor worm)
- Viral: WSSV (in shrimp)

Treatment:
- Salt bath: 2-3% for 5-10 minutes
- Lime: 100-200 kg/ha
- Potassium permanganate: 2-4 ppm
- Antibiotics (under veterinary guidance)

10. ECONOMICS
Investment (1 hectare pond):
- Pond construction: Rs. 2-5 lakhs
- Fingerlings: Rs. 30,000-50,000
- Feed: Rs. 1,00,000-1,50,000
- Labor: Rs. 50,000-75,000
- Miscellaneous: Rs. 30,000-50,000

Production (1 hectare):
- Fish yield: 4-6 tonnes/ha/year
- Price: Rs. 100-200/kg
- Gross income: Rs. 4-12 lakhs
- Net profit: Rs. 1.5-4 lakhs/ha/year

11. GOVERNMENT SCHEMES

- Subsidy on fish seed and feed
- Training programs
- Market support
