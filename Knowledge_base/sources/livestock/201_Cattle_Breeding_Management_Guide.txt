1. INTRODUCTION
Cattle breeding management is fundamental to Gujarat's dairy industry. With 8+ million cattle, Gujarat ranks among India's top milk-producing states.

2. INDIGENOUS BREEDS OF GUJARAT
Gir:
- Origin: Gir forests (Junagadh, Amreli)
- Color: Red to spotted red/white
- Milk yield: 8-12 liters/day
- Special: Heat tolerant, disease resistant
- Bull: Used for draft and breeding

Kankrej:
- Origin: Kutch, Banaskantha
- Color: Silver to iron gray
- Milk yield: 5-8 liters/day
- Special: Fastest draft breed, drought tolerant
- Also known as Wadad or Waged

Dangi:
- Origin: Dangs, Valsad, Navsari
- Color: Red/white spotted
- Milk yield: 4-6 liters/day
- Special: Hill breed, sure-footed

Krishna Valley:
- Origin: Karnataka (adapted in Gujarat)
- Color: Gray/black
- Milk yield: 6-9 liters/day
- Special: Large size, good draught

3. EXOTIC AND CROSSBRED CATTLE
Holstein Friesian (HF):
- Color: Black and white
- Milk yield: 20-40 liters/day
- Requires: Good management, cooling
- Popular in organized dairy farms

Jersey:
- Color: Fawn/cream
- Milk yield: 15-25 liters/day
- Fat: 5% (higher than HF)
- Suitable: Small farms, low input

Crossbreds (HF x Gir, Jersey x Gir):
- Milk yield: 10-20 liters/day
- Adapted to Gujarat climate
- Popular among farmers

4. BREEDING PROGRAMS
Selection Criteria for Bulls:
- Pedigree: 3 generation records
- Progeny testing: Milk yield of daughters
- Conformation: Body structure, udder
- Health: Disease free, fertile
- Temperament: Docile

Selection Criteria for Cows:
- Milk yield: Minimum 3000 liters/lactation
- Fat content: >3.5%
- Age at first calving: 30-36 months
- Calving interval: 12-14 months
- Udder health: No mastitis history

5. ARTIFICIAL INSEMINATION (AI)
Benefits:
- Access to superior genetics
- Disease control
- Cost-effective
- Record keeping

AI Schedule:
- First service: 60-90 days post-calving
- Heat detection: 2-3 times daily
- Insemination: 12-18 hours after heat onset
- Second service: If not pregnant, next heat

6. ESTRUS DETECTION
Signs of Heat:
- Mounting other cows
- Clear mucus discharge
- Restlessness, bellowing
- Reduced feed intake
- Swollen vulva

Detection Methods:
- Visual observation (morning/evening)
- Tail painting
- Pedometers/activity monitors
- Heat detection patches
- Teaser bulls (vasectomized)

7. PREGNANCY DIAGNOSIS
Methods:
- Rectal palpulation: 45-60 days
- Ultrasound: 28-35 days
- Blood test (P4): 21-24 days
- Milk progesterone test

8. CALVING MANAGEMENT
Pre-calving Preparation:
- Separate 2 weeks before expected date
- Monitor udder development
- Ensure clean, dry calving area
- Keep emergency kit ready

Normal Calving Signs:
- Udder engorgement
- Vulval relaxation
- Restlessness
- Stringy mucus discharge
- Water bag appears

Assistance:
- Normal: Allow natural calving
- Difficult: Gentle assistance
- Professional help for malpresentation
- Aftercare: Ensure calf nurses colostrum

9. RECORD KEEPING
Essential Records:
- Breeding dates and bull used
- Calving dates and sex of calf
- Milk production daily
- Health treatments
- Vaccination history
- Growth records of young stock

10. GOVERNMENT SCHEMES
National Dairy Development:
- AI services at subsidized rates
- Sex-sorted semen program
- Embryo transfer technology
- Herd improvement programs

Gujarat Schemes:
- Gopalan department services
- Mobile veterinary units
- Kisan credit for livestock
- Insurance schemes
