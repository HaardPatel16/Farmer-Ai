  • Scientific Name: Grandparent, parent stock
  • Primary Uses: Breeding stock, day-old chick supply
  • Suitable Regions: All zones, biosecure facility

                           3. SOIL / SUBSTRATE REQUIREMENTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Ideal Substrate/Soil Characteristics:
  • Type: Well-drained loamy to sandy loam (for soil-based crops)
  • pH: 6.0-7.5 (slightly acidic to neutral)
  • Organic Matter: 1.5-3.0%
  • Depth: 30-60 cm minimum (for rooted crops)
  • Drainage: Excellent (critical for root health)
  • Water Table: >2 meters below surface

Substrate Preparation (for non-soil enterprises):
  Step 1: Select appropriate substrate (straw, compost, cocopeat, etc.)
  Step 2: Sterilize or pasteurize to eliminate contaminants
  Step 3: Adjust moisture content to 60-70%
  Step 4: Mix with supplements (bran, gypsum, lime) as needed
  Step 5: Check pH and adjust to optimal range
  Step 6: Fill containers or prepare beds

Soil Testing Recommendations:
  • Test before planting for N, P, K, micronutrients
  • pH adjustment with lime or sulfur as needed
  • EC should be <2.0 dS/m for most crops
  • Heavy metal testing for export-oriented products

                           8. PEST, DISEASE & BIOSECURITY MANAGEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. INTEGRATED MANAGEMENT STRATEGY:

Preventive Measures:
  • Quarantine: New stock isolation before introduction
  • Sanitation: Regular cleaning, disinfection protocols
  • Biosecurity: Restricted entry, foot baths, protective clothing
  • Monitoring: Daily inspection, record keeping, early detection
  • Vaccination: Where applicable and recommended

Cultural Control:
  • Proper spacing / density to reduce stress
  • Optimal nutrition to boost immunity
  • Environmental control to discourage pathogens
  • Crop / stock rotation where possible
  • Removal of infected / dead material promptly

Biological Control:
  • Beneficial organisms: Predators, parasites, competitors
  • Microbial agents: Bacillus, Trichoderma, Pseudomonas
  • Probiotics: For gut health in livestock/aquaculture
  • Competitive exclusion: Beneficial microbes

Chemical / Medical Control (Last Resort):
  • Use only approved products
  • Follow withdrawal periods strictly
  • Rotate active ingredients to prevent resistance
  • Maintain treatment records
  • Use protective equipment

                           9. HARVEST / COLLECTION & POST-PROCESSING
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. HARVEST / COLLECTION:

Optimal Timing:
  • For fresh market: At peak quality, maturity
  • For processing: At specified stage
  • For seeds/breeding: At full maturity
  • Time: Early morning (6-9 AM) or cool period

Harvesting Techniques:
  • Manual: Hand picking, cutting, netting
  • Mechanical: Where applicable and economical
  • Frequency: Multiple collections for continuous production
  • Care: Gentle handling, avoid damage, maintain quality

B. POST-HARVEST / POST-COLLECTION HANDLING:

Pre-Cooling / Conditioning:
  • Immediate cooling to appropriate temperature
  • Hydrocooling or air cooling as needed
  • Reduces respiration, extends shelf life

Grading & Sorting:
  • Size, color, weight, shape, defect-free
  • Manual or mechanical grading
  • Separate into grades A, B, C, D

Packaging:
  • Ventilated containers (preferred for fresh)
  • Airtight containers (for dried/preserved)
  • Insulated containers (for temperature-sensitive)
  • Label with date, grade, origin, batch

Storage:
  • Cold storage: 0-10°C, 85-95% RH for fresh
  • Dry storage: Cool, dark, ventilated for dried
  • Controlled atmosphere: For long-term storage
  • Inventory rotation: FIFO (First In, First Out)

C. VALUE ADDITION OPPORTUNITIES:
  • Drying: Solar, mechanical, freeze-drying
  • Processing: Extraction, concentration, formulation
  • Packaging: Consumer packs, branded products
  • Direct marketing: Online, farmers markets, contracts

                           14. TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Problem: Poor establishment / low survival rate
  → Check: Quality of stock/seed, environmental conditions, handling stress
  → Solution: Source certified stock, optimize environment, gentle handling

Problem: Slow growth / poor production
  → Check: Nutrition adequacy, environmental stress, disease, overcrowding
  → Solution: Balanced feeding, optimize temperature/humidity, health check, reduce density

Problem: Disease outbreak
  → Check: Biosecurity breach, stress factors, contamination source
  → Solution: Isolate affected, sanitize, treat appropriately, review biosecurity

Problem: Poor quality product
  → Check: Harvest timing, handling, storage, nutrition
  → Solution: Harvest at optimal stage, gentle handling, proper storage, balanced nutrition

Problem: High mortality
  → Check: Water quality, disease, nutrition, temperature extremes, toxins
  → Solution: Test water, veterinary consultation, diet review, climate control

Problem: Low market price
  → Check: Oversupply, poor quality, wrong market, lack of grading/branding
  → Solution: Diversify products, improve quality, direct marketing, value addition

Problem: High input costs
  → Check: Feed efficiency, energy use, labor productivity, waste
  → Solution: Optimize feed conversion, energy audit, mechanization, reduce waste

Problem: Regulatory compliance issues
  → Check: Certification status, record keeping, inspection readiness
  → Solution: Maintain records, follow SOPs, seek extension support, pre-audit
