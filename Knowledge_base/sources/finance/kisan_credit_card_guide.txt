Kisan Credit Card (KCC) practical guide for Gujarat farmers: KCC is a revolving credit facility that allows farmers to buy agricultural inputs (seeds, fertilizers, pesticides, fuel) without cash. Works like a debit card against an approved credit limit. Eligibility: any farmer, sharecropper, tenant farmer, or SHG member engaged in crop production in Gujarat. Limit calculation: based on scale of finance (crop-wise cost of cultivation as per district-level committee) × area cultivated + 10% post-harvest expenses + 20% maintenance + insurance. Example: if groundnut SoF for Rajkot district is Rs 55,000/hectare and you farm 2 hectares, your KCC limit ≈ Rs 1,40,000-1,60,000. Interest rate: 7% per annum; government interest subvention reduces effective rate to 4% for prompt repayment within 1 year. Renewal: KCC is renewed annually; keep account active. Where to apply: any nationalised bank (SBI, Bank of Baroda, Bank of India), cooperative banks (district cooperative banks in Gujarat), NABARD-affiliated RRBs, cooperative credit societies (PACS). Documents needed: land records (7-12 utara), identity proof (Aadhar), photograph, bank account, soil health card (if available). Process: visit bank with documents; bank officer assesses and approves within 7-14 days for amounts below Rs 3 lakh (no collateral needed); above Rs 3 lakh — land mortgage required. Repayment: after harvest; typically one crop cycle. ATM/debit card: bank issues KCC-linked Rupay card; withdraw cash or pay at input dealers using card. Common problem: many Gujarat farmers do not know they can get KCC without full land ownership — tenant farmers and sharecroppers are also eligible under 2020 RBI guidelines; use Kisan Sahay app to apply.
