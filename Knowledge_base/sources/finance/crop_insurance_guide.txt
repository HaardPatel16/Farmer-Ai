Crop insurance claim guide Gujarat — PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana): Enrolment: enrol at bank (if crop loan taken, enrolment is automatic); or enrol independently through CSC (Common Service Centre) or at PMFBY.gov.in or mParivahan app before cut-off date (varies by crop and state — check with district agriculture office). Premium: 2% of sum insured for Kharif food/oilseed crops; 1.5% for Rabi crops; 5% for annual horticultural/commercial crops. Government pays remaining premium. Sum insured = average district yield × MSP. How claims work: yield-based claims triggered by district-level crop cutting experiments (CCE) conducted by agriculture department; if district-average yield falls below threshold yield, all insured farmers in that block/district get automatic payout — you do not need to file individual claim for this type. Individual calamity claims (mid-season adversity): if your specific field is damaged by localised flooding, hailstorm, or cyclone, submit claim within 72 hours to your bank branch or directly to insurance company through mobile app. Documents: submit crop damage photo with GPS location, Khasra/7-12 copy, bank passbook photocopy, Aadhar. Claim payment: directly credited to your bank account within 30-45 days of settlement. Complaints: if claim is rejected or delayed, file grievance at agri-insurance.gov.in or call 14447 (PMFBY helpline). WBCIS (Weather-Based Crop Insurance Scheme): automatic payout based on IMD weather station readings (rainfall, temperature) — no crop cutting needed; faster settlement. Available for specific crops and districts in Gujarat — check at PMFBY portal.
