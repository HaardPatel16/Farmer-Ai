Warehouse receipt loan guide Gujarat — pledge loan against stored produce: Warehouse receipt loan allows farmers to pledge stored crops at a certified warehouse and get a loan (75-80% of market value) to meet immediate financial needs — without selling the crop at low harvest-time prices. How it works: (1) Store produce at WDRA (Warehousing Development and Regulatory Authority) registered warehouse in your area. (2) Warehouse issues an electronic Negotiable Warehouse Receipt (e-NWR) in your name. (3) Pledge this receipt at any bank and get a loan at 7% interest (interest subvention available under government scheme). (4) When market price is good (typically 2-3 months later), sell produce and repay loan. Benefit: avoid distress sale at harvest when prices are lowest; sell when prices are highest; interest cost is usually much less than price gain from waiting. Eligible crops and Gujarat warehouses: groundnut (Gondal, Rajkot, Junagadh warehouses), castor, soybean, wheat, cumin (Unjha), coriander. Where to find registered warehouses: wdra.gov.in warehouse directory; or ask at district APMC. Maximum loan amount: usually Rs 75-80 per Rs 100 of current market value. Repayment: when you sell the crop, proceeds go to repay loan; remaining amount to your bank account. Caution: monitor warehouse charges (handling + storage fee); calculate total cost before pledging for long periods; typical storage + interest cost should be less than expected price gain. Farmer Producer Organisations (FPOs) often negotiate better storage rates for members — ask your FPO.
