Farmer Producer Organisation (FPO) formation and benefits guide Gujarat: An FPO (also called FPC — Farmer Producer Company) is a registered company owned by farmers. Minimum 10 farmers can form one; government target is 300+ FPOs in Gujarat under 10,000 FPO scheme. Benefits of joining or forming FPO: collective input purchase (bulk fertilizer, seed, pesticide at 10-20% lower cost); collective selling (better price negotiation at APMC, direct corporate buyer access, export possibility); access to credit as institution (bank loans at lower rate than individual); government scheme priority (many schemes give priority or exclusive access to FPOs). How to register FPO in Gujarat: minimum 10 farmers; register as Farmer Producer Company under Companies Act 2013 at MCA21.gov.in (Ministry of Corporate Affairs); or register as cooperative society under Gujarat Cooperative Societies Act (simpler, at district registrar office). Required documents: MCA registration form, PAN of all members, Aadhar of all members, bank account details, memorandum and articles of association. Support organisations: NABARD, SFAC (Small Farmers Agribusiness Consortium), and state government provide Cluster Based Business Organisations (CBBOs) who give free technical support for FPO registration and operations. Find your local CBBO at sfacindia.com. Government financial support: newly registered FPOs get Rs 15 lakh equity grant under central scheme (10,000 FPOs scheme); plus credit guarantee coverage for loans. Examples of successful Gujarat FPOs: Saurashtra Groundnut Farmers FPO (Amreli), Banaskantha Potato FPO, Kutch Dates FPO (dates export to UAE). Contact district agriculture officer or KVK for nearest FPO or CBBO support.
