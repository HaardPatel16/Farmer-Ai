BEEKEEPING WITH APIS MELLIFERA (EUROPEAN HONEYBEE) IN GUJARAT
===============================================================

OVERVIEW
Apis mellifera ligustica (Italian bee) is the most commercially kept bee in Gujarat 
for large-scale honey production. Migratory beekeepers from Punjab, Haryana, and 
Rajasthan regularly bring mellifera colonies into Gujarat during Rabi season for 
mustard and other crop pollination and honey production.

CHARACTERISTICS OF APIS MELLIFERA
- Large colony size: 40,000-60,000 bees
- High honey production: 25-50 kg/colony/year
- Docile temperament; easier to manage than Apis cerana
- Not native to India; more susceptible to Varroa mite
- Less prone to absconding; stays in hive through lean periods
- Ideal for commercial and migratory beekeeping

HIVE SYSTEM — LANGSTROTH (STANDARD)
- Brood box (Deep super): 50 × 41 × 24 cm; 10 frames
- Honey supers (Shallow): added above brood box during honey flow
- Bottom board, inner cover, telescoping outer cover
- Standard across India; frames and parts interchangeable
- Cost per hive: ₹1,200-1,800 (wooden); ₹2,500-3,500 (plastic)

ESTABLISHING COLONIES
- Purchase nucleus colony (NUC) from established beekeeper
- NUC contains: marked queen, 3-4 frames brood, 1-2 frames honey, nurse bees
- Cost: ₹1,800-2,500 per NUC colony
- Transfer NUC to full Langstroth hive at location
- Feed 1:1 sugar syrup for 2 weeks after transfer to establish

SEASONAL MANAGEMENT IN GUJARAT

Prime Honey Flow (November-March)
- Mustard bloom (Banaskantha, Patan, Mehsana): November-January
- Cumin and fennel (North Gujarat, Saurashtra): November-February
- Coriander: November-January; yields 15-20 kg/colony
- Sunflower: varies by district; good nectar flow
This is the main earning period for Gujarat beekeepers.

Monsoon (June-September)
- Move colonies to areas with wild flowers (Gir, Dangs forests)
- Colonies build strength on diverse flora
- Honey not extracted (bees need stores); focus on colony strength

Summer (March-May)
- Scarce nectar; reduce colony to 1 super + brood box
- Feed sugar syrup (1:1) to prevent starvation
- Relocate to cooler zones or forest areas
- Inspect for Varroa mite and treat if needed

MIGRATORY BEEKEEPING IN GUJARAT
Gujarat is a key destination for north Indian migratory beekeepers:
- Colonies arrive from Punjab/Haryana in October-November
- Follow mustard bloom: Banaskantha → Patan → Mehsana → Sabarkantha
- Move to Rabi spice crops (cumin, fennel) in January-February
- Return north before monsoon (May-June)
Migratory route yields 30-50 kg honey/colony/season.

HONEY EXTRACTION (COMMERCIAL)
- Use radial or tangential extractor (stainless steel)
- Uncap frames with heated knife or cold uncapping fork
- Extract when frames are 80%+ capped (moisture <18%)
- Strain through 200 micron and 400 micron filters
- Fill in food-grade containers; label with flora type and date
- Varieties: mustard honey, multiflora honey, forest honey (highest value)

VARROA MITE MANAGEMENT (CRITICAL FOR MELLIFERA)
- Most serious pest; can destroy colony within months if unmanaged
- Monitor: sticky bottom board; count mite fall
- Treatment options:
  * Oxalic acid 3.2% solution: 5 ml/seam (brood-free period)
  * Formic acid strips (MAQS): place in hive during honey flow
  * Apivar (amitraz strips): effective but not during honey extraction
- Integrated Pest Management: rotate treatments; never use banned chemicals

QUEEN MANAGEMENT
- Replace queens every 1-2 years for peak production
- New queens available from queen-rearing operations in Punjab/Haryana
- Mark queens with paint pen (year colour code) for easy identification
- Signs of queenlessness: no eggs, agitated bees, multiple eggs/cell (laying workers)

HONEY QUALITY AND CERTIFICATION
- FSSAI license required for commercial honey sales
- Agmark certification: national quality grading system
- Organic honey: requires 3-year chemical-free record; APEDA certification
- Adulteration testing: NMR test at CFTRI Mysore for premium buyers

ECONOMICS (50 COLONIES, COMMERCIAL SETUP)
- Setup cost: ₹3-5 lakh (hives, extractor, transport vehicle, protective gear)
- Annual honey production: 50 colonies × 35 kg avg = 1,750 kg
- Revenue (₹200/kg wholesale or ₹400/kg retail): ₹3.5-7.0 lakh
- Pollination income: ₹500-1,500/colony/season: additional ₹25,000-75,000
- Net profit (year 2 onwards): ₹1.5-3.0 lakh/year

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
- National Bee Mission (NBM): subsidy on hives, extractors, beeswax equipment
- NABARD: term loan for commercial apiaries at 4% interest
- Honey testing labs at KVIC centres (subsidised testing)
- Export support through APEDA for certified honey
