AQUACULTURE IN KHEDA DISTRICT — GUJARAT
=========================================

OVERVIEW
Kheda district sits between the Mahi and Sabarmati rivers with flat alluvial plains 
ideal for pond construction. The district has growing interest in fish farming as a 
supplement to vegetable and tobacco cultivation.

WATER RESOURCES
- Mahi River (west boundary), Vatrak River (north)
- Canal network from Mahi and Dharoi projects
- Seasonal low-lying fields suitable for conversion to fish ponds
- Average rainfall: 750-900 mm (sufficient for monsoon pond filling)

SUITABLE SPECIES FOR KHEDA
Same composite culture as Middle Gujarat:
- Rohu, Catla, Mrigal, Common Carp (standard polyculture)
- Magur (Walking Catfish): High local demand; tolerates low oxygen
- Tilapia: Fast-growing, disease-resistant; good for seasonal ponds

SEASONAL POND CULTURE (LOW INVESTMENT OPTION)
Many Kheda farmers have seasonal waterlogged fields post-monsoon.
These can be stocked with fast-growing species for 4-6 month culture:
- Stock Tilapia or Common Carp @ 5,000-6,000/ha in October
- Harvest in March-April before summer drying
- Minimal input needed; monsoon-filled ponds are naturally fertile
- Net return: ₹40,000–70,000/ha/season with low investment

PRAWN CULTURE POTENTIAL
- Freshwater prawn (Macrobrachium rosenbergii) suited to Kheda's warm waters
- Stocking: 30,000-40,000 post-larvae/ha
- Grow-out: 6-8 months to 30-50 g size
- Market price: ₹300-500/kg (significantly higher than fish)
- Requires better water management and aeration than fish

POND CONSTRUCTION GUIDELINES
- Minimum area: 0.2 ha for viable unit
- Depth: 1.5-2.0 m (deeper for summer retention)
- Inlet and outlet pipes with screens to prevent wild fish entry
- Embankment slope: 1.5:1 (horizontal:vertical) for stability
- Cost of new pond construction: ₹2.5-4.0 lakh/ha (subsidy available)

MARKETING IN KHEDA
- Nadiad fish market: main wholesale point
- Anand and Kheda town retail markets
- Direct sale to restaurants in Anand-Nadiad corridor
- Local demand from Muslim and coastal communities strong year-round

ECONOMICS
- Composite fish culture (1 ha): Net profit ₹80,000-1.2 lakh/year
- Freshwater prawn (1 ha): Net profit ₹1.5-2.5 lakh/season
- Seasonal pond (0.5 ha): Net profit ₹20,000-35,000/season

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
- PMMSY: pond lining subsidy (HDPE liner) for water conservation
- Fisheries Dept: free training at Fisheries Training Centre, Navsari
- NABARD: term loans for pond construction at 4% interest (with subsidy)
