AQUACULTURE IN VALSAD DISTRICT — GUJARAT
=========================================

OVERVIEW
Valsad is Gujarat's southernmost district, bordering Maharashtra, with heavy rainfall 
(2,000+ mm), rivers (Damanganga, Kolak, Auranga), and a coastline suited to both 
freshwater and marine aquaculture. The district has tribal communities who traditionally 
practise inland fishing, and there is growing commercial aquaculture activity.

WATER RESOURCES
- Damanganga, Kolak, and Auranga rivers provide year-round freshwater
- Coastal belt around Umargam, Valsad town, and Tithal
- Ukai reservoir backwaters in Vyara-Valsad border (shared with Surat/Tapi)
- Annual rainfall: 1,900-2,200 mm — highest water availability in Gujarat

FRESHWATER POND AQUACULTURE
Valsad's warm, humid climate supports excellent fish growth:
- Rohu + Catla + Mrigal polyculture: 4,500-5,500 kg/ha/year
- Pangasius (Basa): Fast-growing catfish; 1 kg in 6 months; growing demand
- Magur (Walking Catfish): Very high local demand; drought-tolerant
- Stocking density: 8,000-12,000/ha depending on aeration

INLAND CAGE CULTURE IN RESERVOIRS
Valsad and border districts have reservoir cage culture potential:
- Floating cages (4m × 4m × 2m) in Ukai backwaters
- Species: Rohu, Catla, Pangasius
- Stocking: 200-300 fish/cage
- Yield: 500-800 kg/cage/crop (6-8 months)
- Lower land investment; good for resource-poor farmers

COASTAL AND BACKWATER OPPORTUNITIES

Brackishwater Fish Culture (Mullet, Milkfish)
- Tidal flats near Umargam suitable for mullet/milkfish culture
- Low-input culture using natural productivity
- Market: local coastal communities and Valsad town market

Ornamental Fish Culture
- Valsad's clean water and climate ideal for ornamental fish breeding
- Guppy, Molly, Goldfish, Koi for urban pet markets
- Low space requirement; can be done on 500-1000 sq m
- Income: ₹1-3 lakh/year from small unit

TRIBAL COMMUNITY AQUACULTURE (Dungri, Vansda, Dharmpur talukas)
- Traditional Kokna and Dhodia tribal communities practise fishing
- Tribal sub-plan provides enhanced subsidies (75-80% under PMMSY)
- Community pond formation through SHGs and FPOs
- TRIFED and State Tribal Development Corporation provide support

FISH SEED HATCHERY
- Private hatcheries near Navsari supply fingerlings for Valsad
- Government hatchery at Tithal (Valsad) produces Rohu, Catla, Mrigal seed
- Staggered availability: April-September for Kharif ponds

MARKETING
- Valsad town daily fish market
- Surat wholesale market (major hub, 50-60 km north)
- Mumbai direct supply (viable due to proximity — 150 km)
- Export-grade shrimp to processing plants in Surat and Vapi

ECONOMICS — FRESHWATER POND (1 HA)
- Investment: ₹1.5-2.5 lakh
- Income: ₹3.0-4.5 lakh
- Net profit: ₹1.0-2.0 lakh/year

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
- PMMSY: enhanced tribal area subsidy (75%)
- Tribal Sub-Plan for Aquaculture: separate budget allocation
- Gujarat Fisheries Dept: fingerlings, training, equipment support
- NABARD Rural Infrastructure Development Fund for pond development
