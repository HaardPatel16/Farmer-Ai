AQUACULTURE IN NAVSARI DISTRICT — GUJARAT
==========================================

OVERVIEW
Navsari district in South Gujarat has an established aquaculture tradition due to its 
coastal location, rivers (Purna, Ambika), and proximity to the Arabian Sea. Both 
freshwater and brackishwater (coastal) aquaculture are practised, making Navsari one 
of Gujarat's most productive fisheries districts.

WATER RESOURCES
- Purna and Ambika rivers provide freshwater throughout the year
- Coastal belt (Dandi, Vansda) suitable for brackishwater shrimp farming
- Navsari receives 1,800-2,200 mm annual rainfall (highest in Gujarat)
- Natural ponds, low-lying paddy fields, and tidal flats

FRESHWATER AQUACULTURE
Species and stocking (same as Middle Gujarat):
- Rohu, Catla, Mrigal, Common Carp polyculture @ 8,000-10,000/ha
- Expected yield: 4,000-5,000 kg/ha/year (higher due to better climate)
- Scampi (Macrobrachium rosenbergii): ₹300-500/kg, high demand

BRACKISHWATER SHRIMP FARMING (COASTAL NAVSARI)
Navsari's coastal areas near Dandi are suitable for Vannamei shrimp farming.

Species: Pacific White Shrimp (Litopenaeus vannamei)
- Most farmed species in India; fast growth, disease-resistant
- Stocking density: 30-60 post-larvae (PL)/m² for semi-intensive
- Grow-out period: 90-110 days to 18-22 g size
- Expected yield: 4,000-8,000 kg/ha/crop (2 crops/year possible)
- Market price: ₹200-350/kg farm gate

Pond Requirements for Shrimp:
- Salinity: 10-25 ppt (can be managed with sea water mixing)
- Aeration: paddle wheel aerators compulsory (2-4 HP/ha)
- Water exchange: 20-30% daily during culture
- Feed: commercial pellet feed (Shrimp feed available in Navsari town)

FISH-PADDY INTEGRATED FARMING
Unique to South Gujarat's paddy-growing areas:
- Stock common carp or rohu in paddy fields during monsoon crop
- Fish feed on weeds, insects, and fertiliser runoff
- Paddy reduces need for pesticides; fish improve soil fertility
- Additional income: ₹15,000-25,000/ha from fish per paddy season
- Compatible paddy varieties: tall, flood-tolerant types

NAVSARI FISHERIES TRAINING CENTRE
- Located at Navsari town
- Offers 3-month and 1-year certificate courses in aquaculture
- Free training for SC/ST and small farmers under PMMSY
- Practical ponds on-site for hands-on training
- Contact: 02637-283427

MARKETING
- Navsari town fish market: daily, high-volume
- Surat wholesale market (30 km): major hub for export-grade shrimp
- Direct sale to processing plants in Surat for export
- Local retail: ₹150-250/kg for fresh fish; ₹200-350/kg fresh shrimp

ECONOMICS — SHRIMP FARMING (1 HA / CROP)
- Investment: ₹3.0-5.0 lakh (pond prep, PL, feed, aeration, labour)
- Income (6,000 kg @ ₹250/kg avg): ₹15 lakh per hectare per 2 crops
- Net profit: ₹4-8 lakh/ha/year (highly variable; disease risk exists)

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
- Coastal Aquaculture Authority (CAA) license required for shrimp farming
- PMMSY: subsidy on aerators, hatchery-reared PL, feed
- Marine Products Export Development Authority (MPEDA): export support
- Apply through: ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in or MPEDA regional office, Surat
