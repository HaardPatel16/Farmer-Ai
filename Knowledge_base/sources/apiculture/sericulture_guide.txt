SERICULTURE (SILK FARMING) GUIDE FOR GUJARAT
=============================================

OVERVIEW
Sericulture — the cultivation of silkworms for silk production — is a growing 
activity in Gujarat, particularly in tribal districts (Chhota Udaipur, Dahod, 
Panchmahal) and parts of South Gujarat. The Central Silk Board (CSB) and Gujarat 
government actively promote sericulture as a livelihood for small and marginal farmers 
through subsidised training, planting material, and equipment.

SILK VARIETIES SUITED TO GUJARAT
1. Mulberry Silk (Bombyx mori) — Primary variety
   - Silkworm feeds exclusively on mulberry leaves
   - Produces the finest, most valuable silk
   - Suitable in: Middle Gujarat, South Gujarat, tribal belt
   - 2-3 crops per year possible in Gujarat climate

2. Eri Silk (Philosamia ricini) — For tribal areas
   - Silkworm feeds on castor leaves (abundant in North Gujarat)
   - Produces coarser but warm silk fabric
   - Suitable for: Dahod, Panchmahal, Sabarkantha, Chhota Udaipur
   - Open-ended cocoon; easy processing without special reeling equipment

3. Tasar Silk (Antheraea mylitta) — Forest-based
   - Silkworm feeds on Asan, Arjun, Saja trees in forests
   - Tribal communities in Dang, Valsad collect wild tasar
   - Lower yield but high value; demand from handloom weavers

MULBERRY CULTIVATION — FOUNDATION OF SERICULTURE

Site Selection
- Well-drained loamy or sandy loam soil
- pH: 6.2-6.8
- Irrigation facility essential (mulberry is irrigated crop)
- Not suitable on waterlogged or saline soils

Varieties for Gujarat
- V1 (S-13) and M-5: Early maturing; suitable for Gujarat climate
- S-36: High leaf yield; drought-tolerant; recommended for Gujarat
- Vishwa: High yield; multiple cuts per year

Planting and Management
- Plant cuttings at 90 × 90 cm spacing (12,000 plants/ha)
- Apply FYM 25 tonnes/ha at planting
- Fertiliser: NPK 150:75:75 kg/ha/year in 3-4 splits
- Irrigate every 7-10 days in summer; 15-20 days in winter
- Pruning: 3-4 times/year to manage canopy and stimulate leaf growth
- First harvest (leaf): 3-4 months after planting
- Leaf yield: 15,000-20,000 kg/ha/year (3-4 crops)

SILKWORM REARING (BOMBYX MORI)

Rearing House Requirements
- Minimum 12 × 6 m room per 100 dfls (disease-free layings)
- Temperature: 24-28°C; Humidity: 70-85%
- Good ventilation; avoid direct sunlight
- Wooden rearing stands/trays (60 × 90 cm)

Silkworm Rearing Process
1. Procure dfls (egg cards) from Central Silk Board or state sericulture dept
2. Incubate eggs at 25°C until hatching (10-12 days)
3. First instar (I) to Fifth instar (V) takes 22-24 days total
4. Feed freshly harvested mulberry leaves 4-5 times daily
5. Leaf quantity increases dramatically: 100 dfls need ~600 kg leaves total
6. Fifth instar worms eat 85% of total leaf consumption
7. Mature worms (spinning stage): place on mountages (bamboo frame)
8. Worms spin cocoons in 3-4 days

Cocoon Harvesting
- Harvest cocoons 7-8 days after spinning begins
- Sort: good cocoons vs double/stained/dead
- Sell fresh to reeling units within 5-7 days (or sun-dry to kill pupae)

COCOON YIELD AND INCOME
- 100 dfls → 80-100 kg cocoons (with good management)
- Gujarat target: 60-70 kg/100 dfls (for new farmers)
- Cocoon price: ₹250-400/kg depending on quality and market
- Income per crop (100 dfls): ₹15,000-40,000
- Duration per crop: 28-30 days
- 4-5 crops/year possible in Gujarat = ₹60,000-2,00,000/year

ERI SILKWORM REARING (CASTOR LEAF BASED)
- Uses castor leaves (Ricinus communis) — widely available in Gujarat
- Open-ended cocoon; spun silk cannot be reeled but is spun like wool
- Eri fabric is warm, coarse, and called "Ahimsa silk" (no worm killed)
- Rearing temperature: 26-28°C; humidity 70-80%
- Crop duration: 28-32 days
- Yield: 30-40 kg cocoons/100 dfls; price ₹120-180/kg

POST-COCOON PROCESSING
- Fresh cocoons: sell to reeling units (silk reeling factories)
- Dry cocoons: sell to handloom weavers and yarn spinners
- Value addition: some FPOs in Gujarat process yarn and sell raw silk
- Silk reeling units in Surat district process Gujarat cocoons

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
- Central Silk Board (CSB): free training, subsidised dfls, equipment
- Silk Samagra Scheme (CSB): 75% subsidy on rearing house, equipment, mulberry
- Gujarat Tribal Development: enhanced support in scheduled areas
- NABARD: term loan for sericulture infrastructure
