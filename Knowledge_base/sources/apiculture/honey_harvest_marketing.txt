HONEY HARVEST AND MARKETING IN GUJARAT
========================================

OVERVIEW
Gujarat produces significant quantities of mustard, multiflora, and forest honey 
annually. North Gujarat (Banaskantha, Patan, Mehsana) is the primary mustard honey 
belt, while the Gir forest and tribal belt (Dangs, Valsad) produce premium forest honey. 
Proper harvest, processing, and marketing determine the final income from beekeeping.

WHEN TO HARVEST
Honey is ready to harvest when:
- At least 80% of cells on a frame are capped (wax-sealed)
- Honey moisture is below 18% (prevents fermentation)
- Do NOT harvest uncapped (unripe) honey — it will ferment in storage
- Quick test: hold frame horizontally and shake; if honey falls out, too moist

TOOLS REQUIRED FOR HARVEST
- Bee brush or feather (to move bees off frames)
- Uncapping knife (heated electric or serrated manual)
- Uncapping tray (to collect wax and drips)
- Honey extractor (centrifugal — 2-frame manual or motorised)
- Strainer/filter (200-400 micron stainless mesh)
- Food-grade settling tank (50-200 litre)
- Food-grade storage containers (glass jars, HDPE buckets)
- Refractometer (to measure honey moisture %)

EXTRACTION PROCESS
1. Remove supers (honey boxes) from hive; brush bees back
2. Uncap frames with heated knife in a warm room
3. Load frames into extractor (2-6 frames at a time)
4. Spin extractor at low speed first, then increase
5. Drain extracted honey into strainer over settling tank
6. Allow to settle for 24-48 hours (wax and debris float)
7. Bottle from bottom of settling tank
8. Label clearly: flora type, date, district of origin

HONEY VARIETIES AND PRICES IN GUJARAT

Mustard Honey (November-January, North Gujarat)
- Light yellow; granulates quickly (crystallises within weeks)
- Flavour: mild, slightly pungent
- Farm gate: ₹100-150/kg | Retail: ₹250-400/kg
- High demand from health stores and direct consumers

Multiflora Honey (Monsoon/Rabi, Mixed crop areas)
- Golden amber; slow to crystallise
- Flavour: rich, complex
- Farm gate: ₹120-180/kg | Retail: ₹300-500/kg

Forest Honey (Gir, Dangs, Valsad tribal belt)
- Dark amber to dark brown; high antioxidant content
- Flavour: strong, complex, woody
- Farm gate: ₹200-350/kg | Retail: ₹600-1,200/kg (premium organic)
- Highest value honey produced in Gujarat

Cumin/Fennel Honey (North Gujarat, Saurashtra — Jan-Feb)
- Light golden; distinctive spice aroma
- Niche market; premium pricing
- Retail: ₹400-600/kg

STORAGE GUIDELINES
- Store in airtight, food-grade containers
- Keep away from direct sunlight (UV degrades honey)
- Ideal storage temperature: 18-24°C
- Crystallised honey is NOT spoiled — gently warm to re-liquefy
- Never heat above 40°C (damages enzymes and quality)
- Shelf life: 2+ years if moisture <18% and properly sealed

LEGAL AND QUALITY REQUIREMENTS
- FSSAI Basic License required to sell honey commercially
  (Annual turnover <12 lakh: ₹100/year registration)
- Food label must include: net weight, batch no., date, ingredients, FSSAI number
- Agmark Grade I or Grade II: optional but increases buyer confidence
- For export: APEDA registration and NMR testing for adulteration

MARKETING CHANNELS

1. Direct Consumer Sales (Highest Margin)
- Weekend haats, organic markets (DESI brand outlets, Ahmedabad/Surat)
- Own brand with QR code linking to farm video and story
- Social media (WhatsApp groups, Instagram) for direct orders
- Margins: retain 100% of retail price

2. FPO / Cooperative Marketing
- Pool honey with other beekeepers through Farmer Producer Organisation
- FPO negotiates better prices with bulk buyers
- Gujarat Cooperative Milk Marketing Federation (GCMMF/Amul) has honey range
- Forest Honey: Tribal Cooperative societies (VANBANDHU scheme)

3. Wholesale to Traders (Bulk, Lower Margin)
- Honey traders in Mehsana, Unjha, Rajkot aggregate large quantities
- Typical wholesale: ₹100-180/kg depending on flora and moisture
- Quick cash but lowest margin

4. Export Market
- India exports significant honey to USA, Europe, Middle East
- NMR-tested, pesticide-residue-free honey commands ₹200-300/kg export price
- Exporters in Ahmedabad, Surat, Mumbai source from verified beekeepers
- APEDA (Agricultural and Processed Food Products Export Development Authority) supports

5. Institutional Buyers
- Hospitals and wellness centres for raw/forest honey
- Cosmetics companies (beeswax + honey combo)
- KVIC honey brand (supply to KVIC outlets)

VALUE-ADDED PRODUCTS
- Beeswax: sell at ₹200-400/kg; used in cosmetics, candles, wood polish
- Propolis: ₹500-2,000/kg to pharma and herbal product companies
- Royal jelly: ₹3,000-8,000/kg; requires specialised harvesting
- Infused honey: Ginger honey, Tulsi honey, Turmeric honey (retail ₹600-1,000/250g jar)
