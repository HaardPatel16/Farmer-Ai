BEEKEEPING WITH APIS CERANA (INDIAN HONEYBEE) IN GUJARAT
==========================================================

OVERVIEW
Apis cerana indica, the Indian honeybee, is a native bee species well-adapted to 
Gujarat's climate and flora. It is smaller than the European honeybee (Apis mellifera) 
but more resistant to local diseases and pests, making it the preferred choice for 
small and tribal farmers new to beekeeping.

CHARACTERISTICS OF APIS CERANA
- Smaller colony size: 10,000-20,000 bees (vs 40,000-60,000 for mellifera)
- Well-adapted to Indian diseases, pests, and climate fluctuations
- More tolerant of Varroa mite and other local pathogens
- Prone to swarming and absconding (leaving hive entirely) — needs management
- Honey production: 8-12 kg/colony/year (lower than mellifera's 25-40 kg)
- Excellent pollinator for most Gujarat crops

HIVE TYPES FOR APIS CERANA
Newton's hive (standard for cerana in India):
- Smaller than Langstroth hive used for mellifera
- Brood box: 25 × 20 × 20 cm; 8 frames
- Super box (honey storage): added on top during honey flow
- Made of light wood (white cedar, pine, or locally available wood)
- Cost: ₹600-900 per hive (vs ₹1,200-1,800 for mellifera hive)

ESTABLISHING A COLONY
Method 1 — Catching a Swarm
- Most common and free method in rural Gujarat
- Swarm season: February-April (pre-monsoon) and September-October
- Place empty hive smeared inside with beeswax near fruiting trees
- Swarms naturally settle in hives or can be manually transferred

Method 2 — Splitting an Existing Colony
- Once a colony is strong (7-8 frames covered), divide into two
- Ensure each split has eggs, larvae, honey, and nurse bees
- One split gets the old queen; other raises a new queen

SEASONAL MANAGEMENT IN GUJARAT

Kharif Season (July-October)
- Rich in floral diversity; major honey flow from wild flowers and crops
- Active colony expansion; add supers when 80% of brood box is full
- Watch for swarming — colony may abscond if overcrowded

Rabi/Winter (November-February)
- Mustard and other Rabi crops provide good nectar flow (Banaskantha)
- Cumin and fennel flowering in November-January: major honey source
- Supplement with sugar syrup during late winter (January-February) if needed

Summer (March-June)
- Scarce nectar; colonies reduce in size naturally
- Provide water feeder near hive (bees need water in summer)
- Move hives to mango orchards or teak forests if possible
- Minimise hive inspections during peak heat (March-May)

HONEY EXTRACTION
- Extract honey only after frames are 80%+ capped (sealed)
- Use uncapping knife to remove wax caps
- Cerana honey is thicker; centrifugal extractor or crush-and-strain method
- Filter through double muslin cloth
- Target moisture content: <18% (prevents fermentation)
- Yield per extraction: 1-3 kg/hive for cerana

PEST AND DISEASE MANAGEMENT
- Varroa mite: less severe in cerana; use oxalic acid treatment if needed
- Wax moth: keep colonies strong; remove weak hives; clean debris
- Tropilaelaps mite: common in cerana; rotate brood breaks to disrupt life cycle
- Absconding: add propolis strips, shade hive, avoid disturbance

SITING HIVES IN GUJARAT
- Orient hive entrance facing east or southeast (morning sun)
- Keep under tree shade or thatched cover (avoid direct afternoon sun)
- Place 1-2 m above ground on stands (prevents ants)
- Maintain 2-3 m spacing between hives
- Locate near water source within 500 m
- Avoid proximity to chemical-sprayed fields during bloom

CROP POLLINATION SERVICES
Cerana is an excellent pollinator for:
- Groundnut, sunflower, cotton (Kharif)
- Mustard, fennel, cumin, coriander (Rabi)
- Mango, chiku, guava (Fruit crops)
- Farmers can charge ₹500-1,500/colony/season for pollination rental

ECONOMICS (10 COLONIES)
- Setup cost: ₹8,000-12,000 (hives, protective gear, tools)
- Annual income: ₹15,000-25,000 from honey + ₹10,000-15,000 from pollination
- Net profit (year 2 onwards): ₹20,000-35,000/year from 10 colonies

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
- National Bee Mission (NBM): subsidy on hives, honey extractors, protective gear
- Gujarat Horticulture Mission: beekeeping training at KVKs
- Tribal beekeeping: Tribal Sub-Plan support for forest honey collection
- Apply: ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in under horticulture schemes

CONTACT
- Khadi and Village Industries Commission (KVIC) for training: 1800-3000-0034
- Gujarat Agro Industries Corporation (GAIC): equipment supply
- Local KVK for demonstration colonies and training
