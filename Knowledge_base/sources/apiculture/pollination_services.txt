POLLINATION SERVICES FOR GUJARAT FARMERS
==========================================

OVERVIEW
Managed pollination — placing beehives in crop fields during flowering — significantly 
increases yield and quality in many Gujarat crops. As wild bee populations decline due 
to pesticide use and habitat loss, commercial pollination services are becoming 
economically important for fruit, vegetable, and oilseed farmers.

WHY POLLINATION MATTERS
- Cross-pollination increases fruit set, seed set, and fruit size
- Increases yield by 15-50% for bee-pollinated crops
- Improves uniformity and marketability of produce
- Reduces the need for growth regulators in some crops

CROPS IN GUJARAT THAT BENEFIT FROM MANAGED POLLINATION

High Response (30-60% yield increase with managed bees):
- Sunflower (Kharif): 40-60% increase; very bee-dependent
- Mustard and rapeseed: 20-40% increase
- Fennel (Saunf): 25-35% increase; quality and seed weight improve
- Coriander: 20-30% increase in seed yield
- Cumin: 15-25% increase (wind + bee pollinated)
- Onion seed production: 30-50% increase (critical for hybrid seed)

Moderate Response (15-30% yield increase):
- Groundnut: primarily self-pollinated but bees increase pod fill
- Cotton: some benefit from bee visits
- Castor: wind-pollinated but bees assist in female flower pollination
- Mango: bee pollination during bloom improves fruit set
- Guava: 15-20% increase in fruit set with managed pollination

Vegetable Crops (require bee pollination for seed production):
- Tomato, brinjal, capsicum (for seed): need buzz pollination (bumblebees ideal)
- Cucumber, bitter gourd, bottle gourd: honeybees essential
- Watermelon, muskmelon: honeybees required for fruit set

RECOMMENDED BEE PLACEMENT
- Place hives at field edge (not inside dense crop)
- Hive entrance should face crop
- Place 2-4 hives/ha for most crops
- Place 4-8 hives/ha for cross-pollinated seed crops (onion, fennel)
- Install hives at 10-20% bloom stage (not before flowering starts)
- Remove hives after 80% bloom is over

TIMING REQUIREMENTS IN GUJARAT

Crop → Bloom Period → Placement Timing:
- Mustard: November-January → place at bud stage (October-November)
- Cumin: November-February → place at first 10% flowering
- Fennel: November-March → place at bud stage
- Sunflower: June-August (Kharif) → place at 5-10% flower opening
- Mango: February-March → place just before 30% blossom open
- Watermelon: February-April → place at first female flower stage

PESTICIDE SAFETY DURING POLLINATION SEASON
This is CRITICAL — pesticide misuse kills bees and destroys pollination service value.

Rules When Hives Are in Field:
- Do NOT spray any insecticide during active bee foraging (7 AM – 5 PM)
- If spraying is necessary, apply after 5 PM or before 6 AM
- Do NOT use neonicotinoids (imidacloprid, thiamethoxam, clothianidin) during bloom
- Neonicotinoids are systemic and persist in pollen and nectar
- If unavoidable, use short-residue insecticides (malathion, spinosad)
- Notify beekeeper 48 hours before any spray so hives can be relocated

Signs of Bee Poisoning in Field:
- Dead bees at hive entrance or under flowers
- Bees behaving erratically, unable to fly straight
- Colony suddenly weakened after spray event

BEE RENTAL SERVICE — HOW IT WORKS
Commercial beekeepers provide pollination services in Gujarat:
- Farmer pays per hive per season: ₹500-1,500/hive depending on crop
- Beekeeper brings colonies, places them, manages for duration
- Arrangement: 2-3 week placement per crop bloom
- Written agreement recommended specifying pesticide restrictions

ECONOMICS FOR FARMERS
Sunflower example (1 ha, 4 hives):
- Hive rental cost: 4 × ₹1,000 = ₹4,000
- Yield increase with managed bees: ~40% (from 1,500 to 2,100 kg/ha)
- Additional income at ₹45/kg: ₹600 kg × ₹45 = ₹27,000
- Net gain from pollination: ₹27,000 − ₹4,000 = ₹23,000 per hectare

Fennel seed example (1 ha, 4 hives):
- Hive rental: ₹4,000
- Yield gain: 25% (from 1,200 to 1,500 kg/ha)
- Additional income at ₹100/kg: ₹30,000
- Net gain: ₹26,000 per hectare

ECONOMICS FOR BEEKEEPERS
Providing pollination services as a beekeeper:
- 50 colonies × ₹1,000/colony × 3 seasonal placements = ₹1.5 lakh/season
- In addition, colonies fed on diverse nectar produce better quality honey
- Good for colony strength and brood rearing during Rabi season
