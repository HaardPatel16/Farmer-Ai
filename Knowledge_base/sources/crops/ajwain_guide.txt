AJWAIN (CAROM SEED) CULTIVATION GUIDE — GUJARAT
=================================================

OVERVIEW
Ajwain (Trachyspermum ammi) is an important spice crop grown mainly in North Gujarat 
(Mehsana, Banaskantha, Patan) and parts of Saurashtra. Gujarat is one of India's top 
ajwain-producing states. The crop is sown in October-November and harvested in 
February-March, fitting well in the Rabi season calendar.

CLIMATE AND SOIL
- Cool, dry climate during crop growth (Rabi season)
- Optimal temperature: 15-25°C during growth; cool nights improve oil content
- Soil: well-drained sandy loam to loam; pH 6.5-7.5
- Cannot tolerate waterlogging or heavy clay soils
- Annual rainfall zone: 400-700 mm (rain-fed or limited irrigation)

VARIETIES
- AA-1 (GAU release): most popular in Gujarat; 120-130 days; yield 700-900 kg/ha
- AA-2: slightly later; tolerant to drought; good for Saurashtra
- Local/Desi: widely grown; lower yield but adapted to local conditions

SOWING
- Season: October 15 – November 15 (Rabi)
- Method: Line sowing in rows 30-45 cm apart; seed rate 3-4 kg/ha
- Or broadcast sowing: 5-6 kg/ha (less preferred; harder to weed)
- Seed treatment: thiram 3 g/kg seed before sowing
- Germination: 7-10 days; thin to 15-20 cm within row at 20 DAS

FERTILISER REQUIREMENT
- FYM/compost: 5-8 tonnes/ha applied before last ploughing
- N:P:K = 30:20:20 kg/ha
- Apply full P and K + half N as basal dose at sowing
- Remaining N: top dress at 30 DAS

IRRIGATION
- Mostly grown on conserved soil moisture in North Gujarat
- 2-3 light irrigations if needed: at establishment (10-15 DAS), 
  branching stage, and seed formation
- Avoid irrigation near harvest — increases disease risk
- Excess moisture causes stem rot; ensure good drainage

INTERCULTURAL OPERATIONS
- Weeding: hand weed or shallow hoeing at 20-25 DAS
- Thinning: thin crowded seedlings to 15 cm within rows at 3 weeks
- Apply pendimethalin 1.5 kg/ha as pre-emergence herbicide to reduce weeding cost

PEST AND DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Aphid (Myzus persicae)
- Sucks sap from tender shoots and flower clusters
- Control: spray imidacloprid 0.3 ml/litre or dimethoate 1.5 ml/litre

Powdery Mildew
- White powdery coating on leaves; common in humid periods
- Control: spray sulphur 2.5 g/litre or hexaconazole 1 ml/litre

Stem Gall (Phytoplasma disease)
- Causes distorted, bunchy growth; transmitted by leafhoppers
- No chemical cure; rogue out infected plants; control leafhopper vectors

Blight (Alternaria)
- Brown spots with concentric rings on leaves
- Control: mancozeb 2.5 g/litre at 10-day intervals

HARVEST
- Ready when 70-80% of umbels turn brown/grey-green
- Cut plants close to ground; bundle and sun-dry for 3-5 days
- Thresh by beating bundles on hard surface or using thresher
- Yield: 600-900 kg/ha under good management
- Clean and dry seeds to <10% moisture before storage

POST-HARVEST AND MARKETING
- Grade into uniform size for premium price
- Main market: Unjha APMC (world's largest spice market for cumin/ajwain)
- Also sold at: Mehsana, Patan, Vadnagar APMCs
- Price range (2024-25): ₹12,000-22,000/quintal depending on quality
- Export demand: Middle East, USA, UK (Indian diaspora markets)

ECONOMICS (PER HECTARE)
- Cost of cultivation: ₹18,000-25,000
- Yield: 700-800 kg
- Income at ₹160/kg: ₹1.12-1.28 lakh
- Net profit: ₹80,000-1.0 lakh/ha (good season)
