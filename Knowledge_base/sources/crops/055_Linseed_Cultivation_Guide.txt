3.0  SOIL REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

IDEAL SOIL CHARACTERISTICS:
+------------------------+-------------------+----------------------+
| Parameter              | Ideal Range       | Management Required  |
+------------------------+-------------------+----------------------+
| Soil Type              | Well-drained Loam | Add organic matter   |
| pH Level               | 6.0 - 8.0         | Lime if pH <6.0      |
| Salinity (EC)          | <4 dS/m           | Leaching for saline  |
| Drainage               | Good-Excellent    | Raised beds if poor  |
| Organic Carbon         | >0.8%             | Regular FYM addition |
| Depth                  | >60 cm            | Avoid shallow soils  |
+------------------------+-------------------+----------------------+

SUITABLE SOILS IN GUJARAT:
- Medium Black Cotton Soil (Regur) - Most common in Gujarat
- Alluvial Loamy Soil - River valleys
- Sandy Loam with good drainage - Coastal and Kutch
- Red Loamy Soil - Eastern Gujarat hills

LAND PREPARATION:
1. Deep ploughing (25-30 cm depth)
2. 2-3 harrowings for fine tilth
3. Leveling with laser leveler
4. Incorporate FYM during final ploughing
5. Form ridges/furrows or beds as needed

5.0  SOWING/PLANTING
================================================================================

SPACING: 30cm x 5cm
PLANTS PER HECTARE: 6,66,000

PLANTING TIME BY ZONE:
- South Gujarat: June-July (Kharif), October-November (Rabi)
- Middle Gujarat: June-July, October-November
- North Gujarat: June-July (with irrigation), October-November
- Saurashtra: June-July (with monsoon), October-November
- Kutch: July (with first good rain)

PLANTING METHOD:
1. Prepare field to fine tilth
2. Sow/plant at recommended spacing and depth
3. Apply basal fertilizer at planting
4. Irrigate immediately after planting
5. Ensure good soil-seed contact

================================================================================
6.0  NUTRIENT MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

RECOMMENDED FERTILIZER: FYM 5t/ha, NPK 40:20:20 kg/ha

GENERAL GUIDELINES:
- Apply full phosphorus and 1/3 nitrogen as basal
- Top dress remaining nitrogen in 2-3 splits
- Apply potash in splits as needed
- Include micronutrients based on soil test/deficiency symptoms

ORGANIC OPTIONS:
- FYM: 5-25 tonnes/ha (crop dependent)
- Vermicompost: 2-5 tonnes/ha
- Neem cake: 200-500 kg/ha
- Biofertilizers: Azotobacter, PSB, KSB, Rhizobium

FOLIAR NUTRITION:
- Pre-flowering: Micronutrient spray (Zn, B, Fe)
- Flowering: Urea 2% + DAP 2%
- Fruiting/Pod filling: Potassium sulfate 1%

================================================================================
7.0  IRRIGATION MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

METHOD: Drip irrigation recommended for water use efficiency

CRITICAL IRRIGATION STAGES:
1. Germination/Establishment (0-30 days)
2. Vegetative growth (30-60 days)
3. Flowering/Reproductive phase
4. Fruit/Pod/Grain development
5. Grain filling/Maturity

SEASONAL SCHEDULE:
- Summer: Every 5-7 days
- Winter: Every 10-15 days
- Monsoon: As needed based on rainfall

WATER CONSERVATION:
- Mulching: 5-10 cm organic mulch reduces evaporation by 40%
- Drip irrigation: 40-60% water saving
- Morning irrigation reduces evaporation losses

================================================================================
8.0  WEED MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

CRITICAL PERIOD: First 45-60 days after planting

METHODS:
- Pre-emergence herbicide: Pendimethalin 1.0 kg/ha
- Post-emergence: Crop-specific herbicides as recommended
- Hand weeding: At 20 and 45 days after sowing
- Mechanical: Interculture with hoe/cultivator
- Mulching: Organic mulch suppresses weed growth

================================================================================
9.0  PEST MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

MAJOR PESTS: Aphids, Bud fly, Cutworm, Semi-looper

INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM):
- Monitor with pheromone traps (5-10 per hectare)
- Yellow/blue sticky traps for sucking pests
- Biological control: Trichogramma, Chrysoperla, ladybird beetles
- Need-based chemical application only
- Follow economic threshold levels (ETL)
- Use safe insecticides at recommended doses
- Follow pre-harvest waiting periods

COMMON INSECTICIDES (USE AS PER LABEL):
- Imidacloprid: Sucking pests (aphids, jassids, whitefly)
- Spinosad: Caterpillars (bollworm, fruit borer)
- Chlorpyriphos: Soil pests (white grub, termites)
- Always follow recommended doses and safety precautions

================================================================================
10.0  DISEASE MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

MAJOR DISEASES: Rust, Wilt, Powdery mildew, Alternaria blight

PREVENTIVE MEASURES:
- Use certified, disease-free seed/planting material
- Crop rotation (3-4 years with non-host crops)
- Field sanitation - remove infected plant debris
- Proper drainage - avoid waterlogging
- Balanced nutrition - avoid excess nitrogen

CURATIVE MEASURES:
- Fungicides: Mancozeb, Carbendazim, Copper oxychloride
- Bactericides: Streptocycline for bacterial diseases
- Follow spray schedule based on disease forecasting
- Apply preventive sprays before disease onset

================================================================================
11.0  HARVESTING & POST-HARVEST
================================================================================

HARVEST TIME: March-April (130-150 days)

HARVEST INDICATORS:
- Physiological maturity signs (color change, drying)
- Days to maturity from sowing/planting
- Moisture content reduction
- Size and quality parameters

HARVEST METHOD:
- Use sharp tools (sickles, knives, secateurs)
- Harvest during cool hours (morning/evening)
- Handle carefully to avoid bruising and damage
- Clean harvested produce before storage

POST-HARVEST HANDLING:
1. Sorting and grading by size/quality
2. Cleaning to remove dirt and debris
3. Proper packaging (CF boxes, gunny bags, crates)
4. Storage in cool, ventilated, dry place
5. Cold storage for perishable items

VALUE ADDITION OPPORTUNITIES:
- Primary processing: Cleaning, grading, drying
- Secondary processing: Flour, oil, juice, pickles
- Packaging: Branded retail packs
- Direct marketing: Farmers markets, online
