ALOE VERA CULTIVATION GUIDE — GUJARAT
======================================

OVERVIEW
Aloe vera (Aloe barbadensis miller) is a high-value succulent crop suited to Gujarat's 
semi-arid climate, especially in Kutch, North Gujarat, and Saurashtra. It requires 
minimal water, tolerates poor soils, and has strong industrial demand from cosmetics, 
pharma, and food processing industries. Contract farming arrangements are available 
through several processors.

CLIMATE AND SOIL
- Hot, dry climate ideal; tolerates temperatures from 10-45°C
- Requires 8-9 months frost-free period
- Soil: light sandy loam to loam; well-drained; pH 6.5-8.0
- Does NOT tolerate waterlogged or heavy clay soils
- Kutch, Surendranagar, Rajkot, and Banaskantha are ideal zones
- Can grow on marginal, degraded, or saline-tolerant soils

VARIETIES
- IC-111271 (CIMAP, Lucknow): most popular commercial variety; high gel content
- IC-111269: high dry matter content; preferred by pharma industry
- Local varieties: slightly lower gel yield but more drought-adapted

PLANTING MATERIAL
- Use root suckers (side shoots/pups) from established plants
- Each mature plant produces 5-8 pups per year
- Pup size: 15-20 cm height before transplanting
- Do NOT use leaf cuttings (poor root establishment)
- Cost of planting material: ₹1-3/pup from nurseries or other farmers

LAND PREPARATION AND PLANTING
- Deep plough 2-3 times; make raised beds or ridges
- Add FYM 10-15 tonnes/ha before last ploughing
- Planting spacing: 60 × 60 cm or 45 × 45 cm (27,000-44,000 plants/ha)
- Plant pups at 5-8 cm depth; firm soil around base
- Best planting time: February-March (pre-summer) or July-August (post-monsoon)
- Irrigate immediately after planting

IRRIGATION
- Very drought-tolerant once established (3-4 months)
- Young crop: irrigate every 10-15 days
- Established crop: every 20-30 days; reduce in winter
- Drip irrigation: highly recommended; saves 60-70% water
- Zero irrigation needed during monsoon if well-drained
- Do NOT over-irrigate — causes root rot

FERTILISER
- FYM/compost: 10-15 tonnes/ha at planting
- N:P:K = 50:50:50 kg/ha per year
- Apply in 2 splits (at planting + after 6 months)
- Micronutrients: zinc and boron deficiency common in Kutch soils

INTERCULTURAL MANAGEMENT
- Weed twice in first 3 months (before canopy closes)
- After establishment, dense canopy suppresses weeds
- Remove dead leaves and old pups periodically
- Pup management: remove excess pups to increase leaf size and gel yield

PEST AND DISEASE MANAGEMENT
- Relatively pest-free crop (gel is bitter and repellent)
- Mealy bug: on roots; drench with chlorpyrifos 2 ml/litre if severe
- Root rot (Phytophthora): avoid waterlogging; improve drainage
- Leaf spot (Alternaria): spray mancozeb 2.5 g/litre during humid weather
- Aphids: occasional; spray imidacloprid 0.3 ml/litre

HARVESTING
- First harvest: 12-18 months after planting
- Harvest mature outer leaves (4-5 leaves/plant/harvest)
- Leaves should be thick, firm, and fleshy (not shrivelled)
- Harvesting frequency: every 2-3 months after establishment
- Leave at least 4-5 inner leaves and growing tip intact
- Leaf yield: 40-50 tonnes/ha/year (fresh leaves) from 2nd year
- Plant life: 4-5 years of productive harvesting per planting

POST-HARVEST AND MARKETING
- Fresh leaf sale to processors: ₹3-8/kg (varies widely by buyer)
- Gel extraction: cut leaf, remove rind, extract clear gel
- Gel yield: 65-75% of leaf weight
- Processing units in Gujarat: Ahmedabad, Mehsana, Rajkot
- Contract farming: Patanjali, Dabur, Marico, and smaller processors
- Value-added products: Aloe vera juice, gel, powder, cosmetics

CONTRACT FARMING OPTION
Many Gujarat farmers enter into contract arrangements:
- Processor provides planting material and buyback guarantee
- Assured price: ₹3-6/kg fresh leaf; ₹60-120/kg gel
- Reduces market risk significantly
- Contact APMC or District Horticulture Officer for registered buyers

ECONOMICS (PER HECTARE, YEAR 2 ONWARDS)
- Cost of cultivation (inputs, labour, irrigation): ₹40,000-60,000/year
- Fresh leaf yield: 40-50 tonnes/ha/year
- Income at ₹5/kg: ₹2.0-2.5 lakh
- Net profit: ₹1.4-1.9 lakh/ha/year (stable from year 2-5)

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
- National Medicinal Plants Board (NMPB): subsidised planting material
- Gujarat Horticulture Mission: plantation establishment support
- Krishi Mahotsav distribution: aloe pups at subsidised rates periodically
- Apply at ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in
