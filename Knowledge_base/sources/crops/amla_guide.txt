AMLA (INDIAN GOOSEBERRY) CULTIVATION GUIDE — GUJARAT
======================================================

OVERVIEW
Amla (Phyllanthus emblica / Emblica officinalis) is a hardy, drought-tolerant fruit 
tree suited to Gujarat's semi-arid and dry conditions. It is grown commercially in 
Mehsana, Banaskantha, Sabarkantha, and Vadodara districts. Amla has strong demand 
from Ayurvedic pharma companies, health food brands, and the pickle/murabba industry.

CLIMATE AND SOIL
- Tropical and subtropical climate; tolerates 0-45°C
- Annual rainfall: 500-1,500 mm (wide range; rain-fed or irrigated)
- Drought-tolerant once established (2-3 years)
- Soil: wide range — light loam to clay loam; pH 5.0-8.0
- Can grow on marginal and degraded soils
- Does NOT tolerate waterlogging

VARIETIES FOR GUJARAT
- Chakaiya: most popular; medium fruit (25-30 g); prolific bearer
- Kanchan: large fruit (35-40 g); good for processing
- NA-6 (Neelam): high yielding; suited to irrigated conditions
- NA-7: large fruit; good shelf life; popular for fresh market
- BSR-1 (Banarsi): popular in Vadodara belt; sweet and large
- Francis: traditional variety; good for murabba processing

PLANTING
- Propagation: budding or grafting on seedling rootstock
- Grafted plants: available from government nurseries and KVKs
- Planting time: July-August (monsoon) or February-March with irrigation
- Spacing: 6 × 6 m or 8 × 8 m (220-275 plants/ha)
- Dig 60 × 60 × 60 cm pits; fill with FYM 20 kg + soil + superphosphate 500 g
- Plant grafted saplings in prepared pits

FERTILISER SCHEDULE (PER TREE PER YEAR)
Year 1-2: FYM 10 kg + N 100 g + P 50 g + K 50 g
Year 3-5: FYM 20 kg + N 200 g + P 100 g + K 100 g
Year 6+: FYM 40 kg + N 500 g + P 200 g + K 200 g
- Apply in 2 splits: May-June (pre-monsoon) and September-October (post-monsoon)
- Micronutrients: zinc sulphate 50 g/tree + borax 50 g/tree every 2 years

IRRIGATION
- Young trees (1-3 years): irrigate every 7-10 days in summer
- Bearing trees: 2-3 irrigations critical — flowering (Jan-Feb), fruit set (Mar-Apr), 
  and fruit development (May-June)
- Withhold irrigation during November-January to stimulate flowering
- Drip irrigation: recommended for water efficiency in Gujarat's dry zones

TRAINING AND PRUNING
- Year 1-3: train to main framework (3-4 primary branches at 70-90 cm height)
- Annual pruning: remove dead, crossing, and inward-growing branches (November)
- Avoid heavy pruning of bearing wood in mature trees
- Ringed branches or bark scratching (girdling) stimulates flowering

FLOWERING AND FRUIT DEVELOPMENT
- Flowering: February-March (small, yellowish-green flowers)
- Fruit set: March-April
- Fruit development: April-September
- Harvest maturity: October-November (fruit turns from green to yellowish-green)

PEST AND DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Bark Eating Caterpillar (Indarbela tetraonis)
- Most serious pest; bores into stem/branches
- Control: clean frass from tunnel; insert cotton wool soaked in monocrotophos 
  in tunnel; seal with mud

Mealy Bug (Drosicha mangiferae)
- Attacks during January-February; clusters on new growth
- Control: apply sticky bands around trunk; spray dimethoate 2 ml/litre

Rust (Ravenelia emblicae)
- Yellow-orange pustules on leaves
- Control: spray mancozeb 2.5 g/litre or propiconazole 1 ml/litre

Fruit Drop (Physiological)
- Common in May-June; reduce by calcium nitrate spray (1%) at pea-size stage

HARVESTING
- Grafted plants: first bearing in 3-4 years
- Full production from year 6-7 onwards
- Mature fruit indicators: yellowish-green colour; hard; slightly ribbed
- Harvest by hand-picking or tree shaking with tarpaulin below
- DO NOT let fruit over-ripen on tree (drops and bruises easily)
- Yield: Year 4-5: 20-40 kg/tree; Year 8+: 80-120 kg/tree
- Per hectare (mature orchard): 15,000-25,000 kg

POST-HARVEST AND MARKETING
- Fresh market: sold as is to traders; ₹12-30/kg depending on grade
- Processing market: sold to Ayurvedic companies at ₹8-15/kg
- Major buyers: Patanjali, Dabur, Himalaya, local halwais (murabba)
- Value added: dried amla, amla powder, amla candy, murabba, juice

ECONOMICS (PER HECTARE, MATURE ORCHARD — YEAR 8+)
- Annual maintenance cost: ₹40,000-60,000
- Yield: 20,000 kg fresh amla
- Income at ₹15/kg average: ₹3.0 lakh
- Net profit: ₹2.2-2.5 lakh/ha/year

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
- National Horticulture Mission (NHM): grafted plant subsidy
- Gujarat Horticulture Mission: orchard establishment subsidy
- MIDH portal: apply for plantation and drip irrigation support
- Apply: ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in
