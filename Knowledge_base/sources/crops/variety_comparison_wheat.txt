=== GEOGRAPHICAL INDICATION (GI): BHALIA DURUM WHEAT PROFILE ===
Target Location: Low-lying Bhal Tract (Spanning Ahmedabad, Bhavnagar, Anand, and Surendranagar districts)

1. THE CONSERVED MOISTURE ECOSYSTEM
- Cultivated entirely without artificial irrigation or groundwater pumping. The heavy clayey black alluvial soils of the Bhal tract naturally store immense water volumes from the monsoon rains. As surface water recedes in October, the soil profile retains a deep moisture reservoir.

2. AGRONOMIC CONSTRAINTS AND SOWING SCRIPT
- Sowing Window: October 15 to November 10.
- Deep Sowing Matrix: Sown deeper than normal (7 to 10 cm) using a specialized local two-row drill ('Dantedar') to place seeds directly into the subsurface moisture zone. Row spacing is set at 30 cm. Seed rate is elevated to 120-130 kg/ha.
- Fertilizer Restriction: Zero chemical fertilizers are applied during growth. Applying synthetic nitrogen without flush irrigation can scorch root hairs in rainfed environments.

3. GI SPECIFIC TRAITS
- Variety Lineages: Arnej-206, GW-1, GW-2 (Durum / Triticum durum).
- Quality Metrics: Grains are rich in protein (>13.5-14.5%) and high in carotene content. These hard macaroni wheats are highly sought after by premium semolina, pasta, and spaghetti manufacturers across India.
