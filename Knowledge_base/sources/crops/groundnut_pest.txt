=== GROUNDNUT ENTOMOLOGICAL EMERGENCY DEFENCE ===
Target Insects: White Grub (Holotrichia consanguinea) and Sucking Pest Vectors

1. WHITE GRUB (LOCAL NAME: LOLO / MUNDRA) MANAGEMENT
- Damage Manifestation: Larvae chew through primary root architecture, causing sudden, patch-wise wilting and drying of plants across fields during July and August.
- Night Collection Routine: Adult beetles emerge from the soil and settle on host trees (Neem, Acacia, Ber) immediately following the first monsoon showers. Shake branches between 8:00 PM and 11:00 PM to drop and mechanically destroy beetles in kerosene-water solutions.
- Soil Chemical Remediation: Apply Phorate 10G @ 10 kg/ha or Fipronil 0.3% G @ 20 kg/ha into the soil prior to sowing. For standing crop outbreaks, drench root lines with Chlorpyrifos 20% EC @ 4.0 liters/ha.

2. SUCKING PEST MANAGEMENT
- Vector Risks: Jassids and Thrips act as vectors for Peanut Stem Necrosis Disease.
- Chemical Control: Spray Imidacloprid 17.8% SL @ 3.5 ml or Thiamethoxam 25% WG @ 3 grams per 10 liters of water.
