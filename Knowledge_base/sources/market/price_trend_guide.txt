Crop price trends and market intelligence for Gujarat farmers: Where to check prices: Agmarknet.nic.in — official government site showing daily APMC arrivals and prices for all crops and all Gujarat mandis; check before selling to know today's market price. kisan.gov.in shows MSP and market news. Commodity exchanges: NCDEX (National Commodity and Derivatives Exchange) lists castor oil, guar, cumin, coriander, soybean, chilli — prices on NCDEX reflect national demand and export trends; Gujarat farmers can check NCDEX for price direction even without trading. Price patterns: Kharif crops (groundnut, cotton, castor, soybean) — prices are typically lowest October-December (harvest glut) and highest April-June (supply tight). Rabi crops (cumin, coriander, wheat) — prices lowest February-April (harvest) and highest September-November. Key price drivers: export demand — Gujarat cumin exports to Middle East, Europe directly affect Unjha prices; China demand affects castor; US import policy affects cotton. MSP as floor: Minimum Support Price (MSP) announced by Government of India every year; state procurement at MSP for eligible crops if market price falls below MSP. Check msp_kharif_2026.txt and msp_rabi_2026.txt for current MSP. Gujarat-specific market intelligence: Gondal mandi price reflects Saurashtra groundnut market sentiment; Unjha cumin price is international benchmark; Anand tobacco cooperative sets base price for bidi tobacco in Kheda-Anand belt. Price strategies: stagger selling — do not sell entire crop at harvest (lowest price); store 30-40% and sell in lean months; take warehouse receipt loan from WDRA-approved warehouse and repay when you sell.
