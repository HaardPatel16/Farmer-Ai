Minimum Support Price (MSP) complete guide for Gujarat farmers: MSP is the government-guaranteed minimum price for specific crops — if market price falls below MSP, government procures at MSP through designated agencies. How MSP is set: Cabinet Committee on Economic Affairs (CCEA) announces MSP before sowing season; based on A2+FL cost (actual paid-out cost + family labour) + 50% margin; MSP is increased annually. Kharif MSP crops (2026 key rates): check msp_kharif_2026.txt for current rates — key crops include paddy, groundnut, soybean, cotton, bajra, maize, sesame, moong, tur, urad. Rabi MSP crops: check msp_rabi_2026.txt — wheat, chana, mustard, safflower, masoor lentil. MSP procurement in Gujarat: NAFED (National Cooperative Exports), state cooperative (GUJCOMASOL), and sometimes FCI (Food Corporation of India) conduct procurement. Gujarat state usually procures groundnut, bajra, moong, and cotton at MSP when prices fall. How to access MSP procurement: register on PM-AASHA portal or at your district NAFED/state agency office before procurement begins; bring land records, Aadhar, bank details; bring clean, graded produce meeting procurement specifications (moisture, grade). Procurement schedule: varies by crop and district — district agriculture office announces when MSP centres open. Important: MSP procurement is NOT guaranteed at all times; it is activated when market price falls significantly below MSP in a district. Market price above MSP: if market is above MSP, sell in open market — MSP is a floor, not ceiling. Most years, groundnut market in Saurashtra trades above MSP.
