Where and how to sell crops in Gujarat: APMC mandis: Gujarat has 17 principal APMCs (Agricultural Produce Market Committees) and many sub-markets. Farmers must bring produce to their taluka or district APMC. Key APMCs by crop type: Unjha — world's largest cumin, coriander, fennel, isabgol market (North Gujarat); Gondal — groundnut, cotton, castor; Rajkot — groundnut, castor, cotton; Junagadh — groundnut, sesame; Amreli — groundnut; Bhavnagar — cotton, sesame; Anand — tobacco, vegetables; Surat/Navsari — sugarcane, vegetables; Jamnagar — wheat, bajra; Palanpur (Banaskantha) — potatoes, cumin. Process at APMC: register with APMC ID card (free for farmers, apply at district agriculture office); bring produce in bags or loose; produce is weighed, tested for quality (moisture, grade); auction or negotiated sale; payment through cheque or RTGS within 2 working days (post e-NAM). e-NAM (Electronic National Agriculture Market): all Gujarat APMCs are connected to enam.gov.in; farmers can register and sell online without being physically present; buyers from across India can bid; ensures transparent price discovery; payment direct to bank. FPO (Farmer Producer Organisation) marketing: collective selling through FPO gets better price (5-15% above individual farmer price); APMC fees are same but better negotiation power; reduce transportation cost by pooling; find your taluka FPO at ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in. Direct selling: contract farming with companies (e.g. Gujarat Co-operative Milk Marketing Federation, McCain Foods for potato, ITC for crops) directly; no commission, stable price; register at respective company offices. Vegetables: sell at local sabji mandi, taluka market, or APMC vegetable yard.
