Export market guide for Gujarat agricultural commodities: Gujarat is one of India's top agricultural export states. Key export crops and their export markets: Groundnut (Mungfali): Gujarat is India's largest groundnut exporter; major export destinations Indonesia, Vietnam, Malaysia, China, Philippines (as oil), and Europe, USA, Australia (as confectionery groundnut). Quality requirements for export: aflatoxin below 4 ppb (European standard) — dry groundnut properly (below 9% moisture), avoid mould in storage; bold grade (50+ seeds per 100g); export through Kandla port and Mumbai from Gujarat. Price premium: aflatoxin-free certified groundnut fetches Rs 200-500 per quintal premium. Cumin (Jeera): Unjha is the world's largest cumin market; exports to Middle East, USA, UK, Germany, Australia; total export value Rs 8,000-10,000 crore annually from Gujarat. Quality: cleaned, graded cumin (1mm minimum), moisture below 10%, essential oil content above 2.5%. Castor oil: Kandla Port exports castor oil to 70+ countries; primary export crop for industrial lubricant and chemical use; demand from US, EU, China; Gujarat produces 85% of world castor. Sesame: Gujarat sesame exported as hulled/natural sesame to Japan, Korea, Middle East; quality — white/natural, sorted, 99.95% purity, moisture 5-6%. Cotton: not exported as fibre from Gujarat (processed domestically) but garments and yarn are exported. Spices (coriander, fennel, fenugreek): exported from Unjha and Rajkot to Middle East, USA; growing demand for organic certified spices from Europe. To access export market: register at APEDA (Agricultural and Processed Food Products Export Development Authority) at apeda.gov.in; join FPO for aggregation and quality standardisation; contact FIEO (Federation of Indian Export Organisations) Gujarat chapter for buyer linkage.
