Contract farming guide for Gujarat farmers: Contract farming means a company or buyer gives you a guaranteed price before you plant. You grow the crop as per their specifications; they buy at the agreed price. Gujarat examples: Potato — McCain Foods, Frito-Lay procure processing potato (Chipsona, FL-1533 varieties) from Banaskantha, Kutch farmers; company provides seed and technical support; guaranteed price. Maize seed — Mahyco, Syngenta, Pioneer engage maize seed multiplication with contract in Kheda, Anand, Vadodara; higher price than commodity maize. Cotton — some textile companies have direct procurement contracts avoiding mandi commission. Spices — ITC Spices has contracts for cumin, coriander in North Gujarat. Gherkin/cucumber — for export, companies like Pradeep Crops in South Gujarat do contract farming. Tomato — processed tomato (puree/ketchup) companies have contracts in Saurashtra. What to look for in a contract: written agreement (always insist on signed paper); clear price formula (fixed price or MSP+premium); buyback guarantee (what happens if company rejects quality); input supply terms (who supplies seed, chemicals and at what price); dispute resolution mechanism. Red flags: verbal-only agreements, no written contract, price "subject to quality" without clear parameters. Legal protection: Model Contract Farming Act 2018 protects farmers; register contract at APMC for legal validity. Where to find contracts: local agri input dealers often know which companies are recruiting contract farmers; district agriculture officer has list of registered contract companies. Risk: single-crop contract without exit option can leave farmer with unsold produce if company defaults — diversify.
