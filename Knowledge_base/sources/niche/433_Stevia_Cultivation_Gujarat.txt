                           1. INTRODUCTION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Stevia Cultivation Gujarat is an important medicinal plants crop/enterprise for Gujarat farmers.

Key Highlights:
  • Scientific Name: Stevia rebaudiana
  • Primary Uses: Natural sweetener, zero calorie
  • Suitable Regions: All zones, protected cultivation
  • Economic Importance: High value addition potential for Gujarat farmers
  • Climate Adaptability: Well-suited to Gujarat's agro-climatic conditions

Gujarat Context:
  Gujarat's diverse agro-climatic zones (Kutch, North Gujarat, Saurashtra, 
  Central Gujarat, and South Gujarat) offer unique opportunities for stevia cultivation gujarat.
  With government support under MIDH, NHB, and state schemes, farmers can 
  achieve significant income enhancement through this enterprise.

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                           3. SOIL REQUIREMENTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Ideal Soil Characteristics:
  • Type: Well-drained loamy to sandy loam
  • pH: 6.0-7.5 (slightly acidic to neutral)
  • Organic Matter: 1.5-3.0%
  • Depth: 30-60 cm minimum
  • Drainage: Excellent (critical for root health)
  • Water Table: >2 meters below surface

Soil Preparation (15-20 days before planting):
  Step 1: Deep ploughing (25-35 cm) to break hard pan
  Step 2: Apply 10-20 tonnes FYM per acre
  Step 3: Add 200-500 kg vermicompost per acre
  Step 4: Mix appropriate basal fertilizers (crop-specific)
  Step 5: Level the field and make raised beds if needed
  Step 6: Install drip irrigation if available

Soil Testing Recommendations:
  • Test before planting for N, P, K, micronutrients
  • pH adjustment with lime (if acidic) or sulfur (if alkaline)
  • EC should be <2.0 dS/m for most crops
  • Heavy metal testing for export-oriented crops

                           5. PLANTING METHODS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. Direct Sowing/Planting (Open Field):
  • Land Preparation: Fine tilth, weed-free, moisture adequate
  • Method: Line sowing, dibbling, or transplanting
  • Depth: 2-5 cm (seeds), root ball level (seedlings)
  • Spacing: As per variety and purpose
  • Irrigation: Light immediately after planting

B. Nursery Raising (if applicable):
  • Bed Preparation: 1m wide, 15cm high, well-drained
  • Media: FYM + soil + sand (1:1:1) or cocopeat
  • Sowing: Broadcast or line sowing
  • Care: Shade, watering, disease prevention
  • Transplanting: 25-45 days old seedlings

C. Protected Cultivation (if applicable):
  • Polyhouse/Greenhouse: Climate control, extended season
  • Shade Net: 50-75% shade for sensitive crops
  • Low Tunnel: Season extension, pest protection
  • Drip Irrigation: Precision water and nutrient delivery

                           6. NUTRIENT MANAGEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. Basal Application (Before Planting):

┌──────────────────┬──────────┬──────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Fertilizer       │ Quantity │ Application Method                   │
├──────────────────┼──────────┼──────────────────────────────────────┤
│ FYM/Compost      │ 10-15 t  │ Broadcast & mix in soil              │
│ Vermicompost     │ 2-3 t    │ Mix with FYM                         │
│ Neem Cake        │ 200-500 kg│ Soil application, pest repellent    │
│ SSP (P)         │ 100-200 kg│ Basal, based on soil test           │
│ MOP (K)         │ 50-100 kg │ Basal, based on soil test           │
│ Micronutrient Mix│ 10-25 kg│ Deficiency-based application         │
└──────────────────┴──────────┴──────────────────────────────────────┘

B. Top Dressing / Fertigation Schedule:
  • Split applications at critical growth stages
  • Vegetative stage: Higher nitrogen
  • Flowering/fruiting: Higher phosphorus and potassium
  • Fertigation: Through drip at 2-3 day intervals

C. Organic Nutrition Options:
  • Panchagavya: 3% solution foliar spray every 15 days
  • Jeevamrut: 500 L/acre soil drench monthly
  • Vermiwash: 1:10 dilution foliar spray weekly
  • Biofertilizers: Azotobacter, PSB, KMB, Rhizobium
  • Green Manuring: Dhaincha, sunnhemp, cowpea

D. Micronutrient Management:
  • Zinc: 0.5% ZnSO4 foliar spray
  • Iron: 0.2% FeSO4 + 0.1% citric acid
  • Boron: 0.2% borax at pre-flowering
  • Calcium: Calcium nitrate for fruit quality

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                           7. IRRIGATION MANAGEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. Water Requirements:
  • Total seasonal: 300-1500 mm (crop dependent)
  • Critical stages: Establishment, flowering, fruit development
  • Deficit irrigation: Tolerable during vegetative stage

B. Irrigation Methods:
  • Drip Irrigation: Most efficient, 30-40% water saving
  • Sprinkler: For closely spaced crops, overhead
  • Furrow: Traditional, suitable for row crops
  • Basin: For orchards and wide-spaced crops

C. Irrigation Schedule (Drip - Example):
  ┌──────────────────┬─────────────┬─────────────────────────────┐
  │ Growth Stage     │ Frequency   │ Duration/Quantity           │
  ├──────────────────┼─────────────┼─────────────────────────────┤
  │ Establishment    │ Daily       │ 30-45 min (2-4 L/plant)     │
  │ Vegetative       │ 2-3 days    │ 45-60 min (4-6 L/plant)     │
  │ Flowering        │ Daily       │ 30-45 min (3-5 L/plant)     │
  │ Harvest          │ 2-3 days    │ 30 min (reduce before harvest)│
  └──────────────────┴─────────────┴─────────────────────────────┘

D. Water Quality:
  • EC: <2.0 dS/m (lower is better)
  • pH: 6.5-7.5
  • Bicarbonate: <2 meq/L
  • Sodium: <3 meq/L
  • Chloride: <4 meq/L

E. Rainwater Harvesting:
  • Farm ponds for supplemental irrigation
  • Check dams for groundwater recharge
  • Percolation tanks for watershed management

                           8. PEST & DISEASE MANAGEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM) STRATEGY:

Cultural Control:
  • Crop rotation with non-host crops
  • Deep summer ploughing to expose pupae
  • Timely sowing to avoid pest peak periods
  • Destruction of crop residues and weeds
  • Use of pest-resistant varieties

Biological Control:
  • Trichoderma harzianum: Soil-borne disease control
  • Pseudomonas fluorescens: Bacterial antagonist
  • Bacillus thuringiensis: Caterpillar control
  • Beauveria bassiana: Whitefly, thrips control
  • Verticillium lecanii: Aphid, mealybug control
  • Predatory mites, ladybird beetles, parasitoids

Mechanical Control:
  • Yellow/blue sticky traps for sucking pests
  • Pheromone traps for monitoring and mass trapping
  • Light traps for nocturnal moths
  • Hand picking of visible pests and egg masses

Chemical Control (Last Resort):
  • Use only recommended pesticides
  • Follow pre-harvest interval (PHI) strictly
  • Rotate chemical groups to prevent resistance
  • Use safe application equipment (knapsack sprayer)
  • Wear protective gear during application

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
                           9. HARVESTING & POST-HARVEST
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. HARVESTING:

Optimal Harvest Stage:
  • For fresh market: Mature but not overripe
  • For processing: Fully mature or specific stage
  • For seeds: Complete maturity, dry pods
  • Time: Early morning (6-9 AM) or evening (4-6 PM)

Harvesting Techniques:
  • Manual: Hand picking, cutting with sharp tools
  • Mechanical: Where applicable and economical
  • Frequency: Multiple pickings for continuous crops
  • Care: Avoid bruising, sun exposure, contamination

B. POST-HARVEST HANDLING:

Pre-Cooling:
  • Hydrocooling or room cooling within 2 hours
  • Temperature: 4-10°C depending on crop
  • Reduces respiration, extends shelf life

Grading & Sorting:
  • Size, color, weight, shape, defect-free
  • Manual or mechanical grading
  • Separate into grades A, B, C, D

Packaging:
  • Ventilated plastic crates (preferred)
  • Corrugated boxes with cushioning
  • Baskets for local market
  • Label with date, grade, origin

Storage:
  • Cold storage: 0-10°C, 85-95% RH
  • Modified atmosphere storage for long-term
  • Ethylene-sensitive crops: Separate storage

C. VALUE ADDITION OPPORTUNITIES:
  • Drying: Solar dryers, mechanical dryers
  • Processing: Juice, pulp, oil, powder
  • Packaging: Branded consumer packs
  • Direct marketing: Online, farmers markets

                           14. TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Problem: Poor germination/establishment
  → Check: Seed quality, soil moisture, temperature, depth
  → Solution: Use certified seeds, maintain moisture, adjust depth, treat with bio-agent

Problem: Yellowing leaves (chlorosis)
  → Check: Nitrogen deficiency, iron deficiency, waterlogging, root damage
  → Solution: Soil test, foliar NPK, iron chelate, improve drainage

Problem: Stunted growth
  → Check: Nutrient deficiency, root rot, nematodes, compacted soil
  → Solution: Deep ploughing, nematicide, micronutrient spray, aeration

Problem: Flower/fruit drop
  → Check: Water stress, nutrient imbalance, pest attack, temperature extremes
  → Solution: Consistent irrigation, balanced nutrition, pest control, shade

Problem: Poor quality produce
  → Check: Harvest timing, post-harvest handling, variety selection, nutrition
  → Solution: Harvest at optimal stage, proper cooling, select premium varieties

Problem: High pest/disease incidence
  → Check: Monoculture, poor sanitation, susceptible variety, weather
  → Solution: Crop rotation, field sanitation, resistant varieties, IPM

Problem: Low market price
  → Check: Oversupply, poor quality, wrong market, lack of grading
  → Solution: Diversify crops, improve quality, direct marketing, value addition

Problem: Water scarcity
  → Check: Irrigation efficiency, water source, crop water requirement
  → Solution: Drip irrigation, mulching, drought-tolerant varieties, rainwater harvesting
