                           3. SOIL / SUBSTRATE / HOUSING REQUIREMENTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Ideal Soil/Substrate/Housing Characteristics:
  • Type: Well-drained loamy to sandy loam (for soil-based crops)
  • pH: 6.0-7.5 (slightly acidic to neutral)
  • Organic Matter: 1.5-3.0%
  • Depth: 30-60 cm minimum (for rooted crops)
  • Drainage: Excellent (critical for root health)
  • Water Table: >2 meters below surface

Housing/Substrate Preparation:
  Step 1: Site selection - elevated, well-drained, accessible
  Step 2: Structure design - ventilation, orientation, materials
  Step 3: Substrate preparation - sterilization, mixing, conditioning
  Step 4: Environmental controls - temperature, humidity, lighting
  Step 5: Biosecurity measures - fencing, foot baths, restricted entry
  Step 6: Equipment installation - feeders, waterers, monitoring systems

Soil/Substrate Testing Recommendations:
  • Test before planting for N, P, K, micronutrients
  • pH adjustment with lime or sulfur as needed
  • EC should be <2.0 dS/m for most crops
  • Heavy metal testing for export-oriented products
  • Pathogen screening for controlled environments

                           8. HEALTH, PEST & DISEASE MANAGEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. INTEGRATED HEALTH MANAGEMENT STRATEGY:

Preventive Measures:
  • Quarantine: New stock isolation for 14-30 days before introduction
  • Vaccination: As per recommended schedule for region
  • Deworming: Regular schedule, fecal examination based
  • Biosecurity: Restricted entry, foot baths, protective clothing
  • Sanitation: Regular cleaning, disinfection protocols
  • Monitoring: Daily inspection, record keeping, early detection

Cultural / Management Control:
  • Proper spacing / density to reduce stress and disease spread
  • Optimal nutrition to boost immunity and resistance
  • Environmental control to discourage pathogen growth
  • All-in-all-out system for batch management
  • Crop / stock rotation where possible
  • Removal of infected / dead material promptly, safe disposal

Biological Control:
  • Beneficial organisms: Predators, parasites, competitors
  • Microbial agents: Bacillus, Trichoderma, Pseudomonas, probiotics
  • Competitive exclusion: Beneficial microbes in gut/environment
  • Bacteriophages: For specific bacterial control

Chemical / Medical Control (Last Resort):
  • Use only approved veterinary/phytosanitary products
  • Follow withdrawal periods strictly (milk, meat, eggs)
  • Rotate active ingredients to prevent resistance
  • Maintain treatment records for traceability
  • Use protective equipment during application
  • Dispose expired medicines safely

                           9. HARVEST / COLLECTION / PROCESSING
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

A. HARVEST / COLLECTION / MILKING / SHEARING:

Optimal Timing:
  • For fresh market: At peak quality, maturity, optimal body condition
  • For processing: At specified stage, composition, yield
  • For breeding: At appropriate age, weight, health status
  • Time: Early morning (6-9 AM) or cool period for most operations

Harvesting / Collection Techniques:
  • Manual: Hand picking, cutting, milking, shearing, hand plucking
  • Mechanical: Where applicable, economical, and animal-friendly
  • Frequency: Multiple collections for continuous production
  • Care: Gentle handling, avoid bruising, stress, contamination
  • Hygiene: Clean equipment, udder washing, sanitization

B. POST-HARVEST / POST-COLLECTION HANDLING:

Pre-Cooling / Conditioning:
  • Immediate cooling to appropriate temperature
  • Milk: Chill to 4°C within 2 hours of milking
  • Meat: Chill to 0-4°C within 1 hour of slaughter
  • Eggs: Cool to 13-15°C gradually
  • Wool: Sort, grade, pack in clean bags

Grading & Sorting:
  • Size, color, weight, shape, defect-free, fiber diameter
  • Manual or mechanical grading
  • Separate into grades A, B, C, D
  • Quality standards for domestic and export markets

Packaging:
  • Ventilated containers (preferred for fresh produce)
  • Airtight containers (for dried, preserved, value-added)
  • Insulated containers (for temperature-sensitive products)
  • Food-grade materials for edible products
  • Label with date, grade, origin, batch, expiry

Storage:
  • Cold storage: 0-4°C for milk, meat, 13-15°C for eggs
  • Dry storage: Cool, dark, ventilated for grains, wool, dried products
  • Controlled atmosphere: For long-term storage of specific products
  • Inventory rotation: FIFO (First In, First Out)
  • Pest control: Regular monitoring, safe methods

C. VALUE ADDITION OPPORTUNITIES:
  • Processing: Pasteurization, homogenization, chilling
  • Manufacturing: Cheese, butter, ghee, yogurt, paneer
  • Drying: Solar, mechanical, freeze-drying
  • Extraction: Essential oils, active compounds, fiber processing
  • Packaging: Consumer packs, branded products, gift packs
  • Direct marketing: Online, farmers markets, farm shops, contracts

                           14. TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Problem: Poor growth / low weight gain
  → Check: Nutrition adequacy, parasite load, disease, genetics, stress
  → Solution: Diet review, deworming, health check, genetic improvement, stress reduction

Problem: Low reproduction / infertility
  → Check: Nutrition, body condition, disease, age, management, bull/ram fertility
  → Solution: Balanced ration, mineral supplementation, breeding soundness exam, AI, culling

Problem: High mortality / morbidity
  → Check: Disease outbreak, biosecurity, vaccination, nutrition, environment
  → Solution: Veterinary consultation, isolation, treatment, vaccination review, sanitation

Problem: Poor product quality (milk, meat, wool, eggs)
  → Check: Nutrition, breed, health, handling, storage, contamination
  → Solution: Diet optimization, breed selection, health management, gentle handling, cold chain

Problem: Low market price
  → Check: Oversupply, poor quality, wrong market, lack of grading/branding, seasonality
  → Solution: Diversify products, improve quality, direct marketing, value addition, storage

Problem: High feed cost / low feed efficiency
  → Check: Feed quality, wastage, formulation, parasite load, genetics
  → Solution: Quality feed, reduce wastage, balanced ration, deworming, genetic improvement

Problem: Heat stress in summer
  → Check: Shade, ventilation, water availability, body condition, breed
  → Solution: Shade structures, fans, sprinklers, cool drinking water, heat-tolerant breeds

Problem: Water scarcity / poor water quality
  → Check: Source, storage, contamination, salinity, bacterial load
  → Solution: Rainwater harvesting, borewell treatment, water testing, chlorination, filtration

Problem: Labor shortage / high labor cost
  → Check: Mechanization level, automation, family labor availability, wages
  → Solution: Mechanization, automation, cooperative labor sharing, contract services

Problem: Regulatory compliance / certification issues
  → Check: Record keeping, SOPs, inspection readiness, veterinary records, withdrawal periods
  → Solution: Maintain records, follow SOPs, pre-audit, veterinary consultation, extension support
