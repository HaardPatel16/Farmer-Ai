NFSM (National Food Security Mission) scheme guide Gujarat: NFSM provides financial assistance to increase production of rice, wheat, pulses, coarse cereals, oilseeds, and cotton. Main components available in Gujarat: Cluster front line demonstrations (CFLD) — free demonstration of improved varieties and technology in a cluster of 25-50 acres; farmer gets free seed, fertilizer, and training for demonstration plot; attend KVK or district agriculture office camps to participate. Seed distribution subsidy: quality certified seeds of improved varieties at 50% subsidy — wheat, rice, pulses (moong, urad, chana, tur), and coarse cereals; available at taluka seed stores; quantities limited, first-come-first-served. Soil amelioration: lime/gypsum distribution at 50% cost for acid or saline soils — relevant for tribal belt (acidic soils) and coastal Saurashtra (saline soils). Farm machinery: power weeders, zero-till drills, seed drills at 50% subsidy — apply at district agriculture office. IPM interventions: pheromone traps, biological control agents (Trichogramma cards, Bt) distributed free under NFSM IPM component — contact KVK for distribution schedule. Weed management: weedicides for wheat (clodinafop, isoproturon) distributed at 50% subsidy in selected blocks. How to apply: visit district agriculture office or taluka agriculture office with land record and Aadhar; apply online at agriculturalstatistics.nic.in or through state agriculture department portal ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in. Priority given to small and marginal farmers (below 2 hectares).
