Prakriti Krishi Yojana (Natural Farming Scheme) Gujarat: Gujarat government launched this scheme to promote chemical-free natural farming (zero budget natural farming / ZBNF) based on Subhash Palekar's methods. Key inputs: Jeevamrit (fermented mixture of cow dung 10kg, cow urine 5-10 litres, jaggery 2kg, gram flour 2kg, soil 100g in 200 litres water — ferment 3-7 days); Bijamrit (seed treatment solution: cow dung 50g, cow urine 50ml, lime 50g, soil pinch in 5 litres water). Benefits to farmers: subsidy on training camps, reduced input costs (no chemical fertilizer purchase), premium price for certified natural produce. Eligibility: any farmer with land; scheme promotes registration through Anand Agricultural University. Contact: Gujarat State Fertilizer and Chemicals (GSFC) natural farming cell or nearest KVK for demo plots and training. Natural farming shown to reduce input costs by 30-50% while maintaining 70-80% of chemical yield after 3-year transition period.
