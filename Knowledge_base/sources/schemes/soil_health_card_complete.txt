Soil Health Card scheme complete guide Gujarat: Soil Health Card provides farm-specific nutrient status and fertilizer recommendations — helps farmers apply only what their soil needs, saving money on fertilizer and improving crop health. What the card shows: levels of 12 nutrients — N, P, K (macro), S, Zn, B, Fe, Mn, Cu (secondary/micro), and pH, EC, organic carbon. Low/medium/high classification for each; recommended fertilizer doses for major crops on your field. How to get your Soil Health Card: collect soil sample from your field (0-20cm depth, 8-10 sub-samples from different corners mixed together); take to nearest soil testing laboratory — located at taluka agriculture office, KVK, APMC soil lab, or empanelled private lab; submit with your name, village, khasra number, and Aadhar. Cost: free at government labs. Report received: 15-20 working days; collected from same lab; also available at soilhealth.dac.gov.in using registration number. How to use the recommendations: the card gives crop-specific recommendations — follow the doses for your actual crops. If card shows adequate potassium, save money by not applying MOP. If card shows zinc deficiency (very common in Gujarat), apply zinc sulphate 25kg/ha. Deficiency trends in Gujarat: zinc is deficient in 60-70% of Gujarat soils tested; sulphur deficient in 40% (important for oilseeds, pulses); boron deficient in many South Gujarat lateritic soils; iron deficient in calcareous soils of North Gujarat and Kutch. Important: sample every 3 years for the same field; card validity is officially 3 years. Organic carbon: OC below 0.5% is very low — widespread in Gujarat; apply FYM, vermicompost, or crop residue incorporation to build OC over time. Nearest lab: contact district agriculture officer at 1800-180-1551 (toll-free).
