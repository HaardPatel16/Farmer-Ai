1. SCHEME NAME & FULL OFFICIAL NAME
   Gujarat State-Specific Agriculture Schemes — Comprehensive guide to Gujarat government schemes beyond central government programs, implemented by the Department of Agriculture, Farmers Welfare & Co-operation, Government of Gujarat.

2. WHAT IS IT
   Gujarat has several state-specific agriculture schemes that complement central government programs. These schemes are tailored to Gujarat's specific agro-climatic conditions, crop patterns, and farmer needs. The major state schemes include: Mukhyamantri Kisan Sahay Yojana (MKSY) for crop loss compensation without insurance premium; Kisan Suryodaya Yojana for daytime electricity; Gujarat Green Revolution Company (GGRC) for micro-irrigation; Krushi Mati Poshan Yojana (Soil Health Card); Agriculture Raths for mobile advisory; and the Khedut App for digital services. These schemes are funded entirely by the Gujarat state budget and are administered by the state agriculture department. Together, they form a comprehensive safety net for Gujarat farmers, covering insurance, irrigation, power, soil health, and market information.

3. WHO IS ELIGIBLE
   • All farmers with land records in Gujarat
   • Specific eligibility varies by scheme:
     - MKSY: All farmers with Kharif crops (automatic enrollment)
     - Kisan Suryodaya: Farmers in 15,000+ villages with agricultural power connections
     - GGRC: All farmers with water source
     - Soil Health Card: All farmers (free)
     - Khedut App: All farmers with smartphones
   • No income limit for most state schemes
   • SC/ST and small farmers get additional benefits in most schemes
   • Exclusions: Farmers without land records in Gujarat; institutional landholders

4. BENEFITS PROVIDED
   • Mukhyamantri Kisan Sahay Yojana (MKSY): Rs 20,000/hectare for 33-60% crop loss; Rs 25,000/hectare for above 60% loss; up to 4 hectares; no premium required
   • Kisan Suryodaya Yojana: 3-phase electricity for irrigation from 5 AM to 9 PM (previously only 6-8 hours, often at night)
   • GGRC Micro-Irrigation: 80% subsidy for small farmers (below 5 acres); 55% for large farmers
   • Soil Health Card (Krushi Mati Poshan Yojana): Free soil testing every 3 years with fertilizer recommendations
   • Agriculture Raths: Mobile advisory vehicles visiting remote villages with experts
   • Khedut App: Free weather alerts, mandi prices, pest warnings, and scheme information in Gujarati
   • Ikhedut Portal: Online application for all state and central schemes with DBT payment
   • Gujarat State Fertilizer and Chemicals (GSFC) subsidies: Additional state top-up on fertilizer subsidies
   • State tractor subsidy: Additional 5-10% over central SMAM subsidy for Gujarat farmers

5. HOW TO APPLY (STEP-BY-STEP)
   For MKSY:
   Step 1: No application needed — automatic enrollment based on land records.
   Step 2: If crop loss occurs, the revenue talati conducts a survey.
   Step 3: Compensation is approved by the district collector and credited to your bank account via DBT.
   For Kisan Suryodaya:
   Step 1: No separate application — implemented by the Gujarat Electricity Board (GEB/GUVNL) based on agricultural connection records.
   Step 2: Power schedule is updated automatically in your area.
   For GGRC Micro-Irrigation:
   Step 1: Apply through iKhedut portal or GGRC office with land records and water source proof.
   Step 2: Field survey and installation by empaneled company.
   For Soil Health Card:
   Step 1: Collect soil sample from your field (0-20 cm depth, mix 8-10 sub-samples).
   Step 2: Submit at taluka agriculture office, KVK, or APMC soil lab with your details.
   Step 3: Collect the card after 15-20 working days.
   For Khedut App:
   Step 1: Download from Play Store/App Store.
   Step 2: Register with your mobile number and farmer ID.
   Documents vary by scheme: generally Aadhaar, 7/12, bank passbook, and caste certificate.

6. WHERE TO APPLY
   • iKhedut portal: ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in — for most state schemes
   • District Agriculture Office (DAO) — for MKSY grievances and GGRC applications
   • Taluka Agriculture Office — for soil health card submission and general guidance
   • KVK (Krishi Vigyan Kendra) — for soil testing and training
   • GGRC district offices — for micro-irrigation applications
   • GUVNL/GEB office — for Kisan Suryodaya electricity schedule inquiries
   • CSC (Common Service Centre) — for online application help
   • Khedut App — for information and price tracking (not for applications)

7. GUJARAT-SPECIFIC DETAILS
   Gujarat spends over Rs 5,000 crore annually on state agriculture schemes. MKSY has paid over Rs 2,000 crore in compensation to Gujarat farmers since launch. Kisan Suryodaya has covered 15,000+ villages and benefited 20+ lakh farmers. Gujarat has the highest micro-irrigation coverage in India (25+ lakh hectares). The Khedut App has 2+ million downloads and is among the top agriculture apps in India. Gujarat's Agriculture Raths cover 100% of villages on a rotating schedule. The state has digitized 100% of land records (7/12 extracts) and made them available online. Gujarat has the highest rate of DBT penetration among farmers in India. The state has launched a "Chief Minister's Agriculture Excellence Award" for farmers adopting innovative practices.

8. COMMON PROBLEMS & SOLUTIONS
   • MKSY compensation not received: Check if your village was declared a "affected area" by the district collector. If eligible but not paid, contact the taluka mamlatdar or district collector office with your 7/12 extract.
   • Kisan Suryodaya power not available: Contact the local GEB/GUVNL office with your consumer number. The scheme is implemented in phases; some areas may be pending.
   • GGRC installation delay: Contact the GGRC district office with your application reference number. If the company is delaying, GGRC can reassign.
   • Soil Health Card not ready: Check at the same lab where you submitted after 20 days. If not ready, contact the district agriculture officer.
   • Khedut App not working: Update the app; ensure internet connectivity; if issues persist, contact the Khedut support team within the app.
   • iKhedut portal issues: Clear browser cache; use Chrome/Firefox; if still not working, visit the nearest CSC.
