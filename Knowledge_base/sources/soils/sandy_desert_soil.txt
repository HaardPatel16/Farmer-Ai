Sandy desert soils (Aeolian sandy soils) Gujarat: Found in Banaskantha, northern Kutch, Patan, Mehsana districts — formed by wind-blown desert sand from Thar. Properties: pH 7.5-8.5, very low clay content (less than 10%), extremely low water-holding capacity (water drains away within hours), low organic matter, low nutrient retention, deficient in nitrogen, phosphorus, zinc, iron, boron. Advantage: excellent aeration, easy tillage, suitable for root crops (potato, carrot, garlic) and cumin, fennel which need good drainage. Challenges: drought stress even after light rain, high evaporation, wind erosion, high irrigation frequency needed. Special management: (1) Apply FYM 15-20 tonnes per hectare every year to improve organic matter and water retention — no other amendment has comparable effect on sandy soils. (2) Drip irrigation is essential — flood irrigation wastes water and nutrients rapidly. (3) Split fertilizer applications 4-5 times rather than 2 (nutrients leach before crop can absorb). (4) Wind breaks (trees) reduce erosion. (5) Gypsum has no benefit on already neutral to alkaline sandy soils. Best suited crops: cumin, fennel, garlic, mustard, castor, isabgol — all drought-tolerant and high-value.
