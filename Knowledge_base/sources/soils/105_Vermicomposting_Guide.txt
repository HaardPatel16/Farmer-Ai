1. INTRODUCTION
Vermicomposting uses earthworms to convert organic waste into nutrient-rich compost. It produces fine, humus-like material with beneficial microorganisms.

2. EARTHWORM SPECIES FOR VERMICOMPOSTING
Eisenia fetida (Red Wiggler):
- Most common species
- Adaptable to Gujarat climate
- Reproduction: 1 cocoon/week, 2-3 worms/cocoon
- Processing capacity: 2-3 times body weight/day

Eudrilus eugeniae (African Nightcrawler):
- Larger size, faster processing
- Prefers warmer conditions
- Ideal for South Gujarat

Perionyx excavatus (Blue Worm):
- Small but prolific
- Surface feeder
- Good for tropical conditions

3. VERMICOMPOSTING UNIT DESIGN
Location:
- Shaded area (under trees, shed)
- Well-drained, protected from rain
- Accessible for feeding and harvesting

Bed Preparation:
Size: 3m x 1m x 0.6m (100 kg biomass capacity)
Material: Brick/cement/concrete/wooden
Base: Sand layer for drainage

Bedding Material:
- Coconut coir, dried leaves, straw
- Pre-decomposed for 2 weeks
- Moisture: 60-70%
- pH: 6.5-7.5

4. FEEDING MATERIALS
Ideal Feed (50-60% moisture):
- Cattle dung (3-4 days old)
- Crop residues (chopped)
- Kitchen waste
- Fruit/vegetable waste
- Tea/coffee grounds

Avoid:
- Fresh manure (ammonia toxicity)
- Onion/garlic (repel worms)
- Citrus (acidity)
- Meat/dairy (odor, pests)
- Oily foods
- Pesticide-treated material

5. PROCESS MANAGEMENT
Initial Setup:
Day 1: Place 10-15 cm bedding
Day 3: Add 1 kg worms (1000-1500 adults)
Day 5: Start feeding 500g waste daily
Increase gradually to 2-3 kg/day

Daily Care:
- Check moisture (squeeze test)
- Feed in small pockets
- Cover with gunny bag/cloth
- Monitor temperature (20-30C ideal)

Monthly Maintenance:
- Turn gently for aeration
- Check worm population
- Adjust feeding rate
- Harvest excess vermicompost

6. HARVESTING
Timeline: 45-60 days for first harvest
Signs of readiness:
- Dark, granular material
- No recognizable feed
- Earthy smell
- Worms moving to fresh feed

Separation Methods:
- Heap and scrape method
- Light exposure migration
- Mesh screen sieving (3-4 mm)

7. PRODUCTION ESTIMATES
Input: 100 kg biomass + 1 kg worms
Output: 40-60 kg vermicompost + 2 kg worm biomass
Cycle: 60-75 days
Annual production (1 unit): 300-400 kg

8. QUALITY STANDARDS
NPK Content: 1.5-2.5% N, 1.0-2.0% P, 0.5-1.5% K
Micronutrients: Rich in Fe, Zn, Mn, Cu
Microbial load: 10^6-10^8 CFU/g beneficial microbes
Hormones: Auxins, gibberellins present
pH: 6.5-7.5

9. APPLICATION
Dosage: 2-5 t/ha (field crops), 5-10 kg/plant (horticulture)
Timing: At planting/sowing, top dressing
Method: Mix with soil, band application, foliar extract
Foliar spray: 5% solution filtered

10. ECONOMICS
Setup cost: Rs. 5,000-15,000 (100 kg unit)
Operating cost: Rs. 3-5/kg produced
Selling price: Rs. 15-30/kg
Income potential: Rs. 50,000-1,00,000/year (commercial unit)

11. COMMON PROBLEMS
Ant infestation: Moat of water around bed
Fruit flies: Bury food, cover properly
Bad smell: Reduce moisture, add dry material
Worms escaping: Check pH, moisture, temperature
Slow processing: Reduce feed, check worm health
