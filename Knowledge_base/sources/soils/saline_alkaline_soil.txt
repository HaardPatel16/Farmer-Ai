Saline and Alkaline (usar/reh) soils Gujarat: Found in Rann of Kutch fringe areas, coastal districts (Jamnagar, Devbhoomi Dwarka, Porbandar), and poorly drained canal command areas. Two types: (1) Saline soils (ECe above 4 dS/m) — white salt crust visible on surface, caused by high salinity; most crops fail above ECe 8. (2) Alkaline/sodic soils (pH 8.5-10, ESP above 15) — hard, impermeable surface pan, poor drainage, toxic sodium levels. Reclamation: Saline soils — install subsurface drainage, apply gypsum 5-10 tonnes per hectare (replaces sodium with calcium), flood leach with canal water 3-4 times. Alkaline soils — apply gypsum 10-15 tonnes per hectare; this is the key amendment for sodic soil reclamation in Gujarat; grow salt-tolerant cover crops like dhaincha (Sesbania) first year. Salt-tolerant crops for partially reclaimed soils: barley, sugarbeet, safflower, cotton (moderately tolerant). Economic reclamation typically takes 3-5 years. Government subsidy available under saline land reclamation scheme through district agriculture office.
