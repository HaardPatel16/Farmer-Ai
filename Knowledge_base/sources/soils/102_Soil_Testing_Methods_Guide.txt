1. INTRODUCTION
Soil testing is the scientific basis for profitable agriculture. Gujarat's Soil Health Card scheme provides free testing to all farmers.

2. IMPORTANCE OF SOIL TESTING
- Prevents wasteful fertilizer use
- Identifies hidden deficiencies
- Improves crop yields by 10-25%
- Reduces input costs by 15-20%
- Protects environment

3. SAMPLING PROCEDURE
When to Sample:
- Before cropping season
- Every 2-3 years routinely
- When problems appear
- Before starting new enterprises

Sampling Protocol:
Step 1: Remove surface debris
Step 2: Collect from 8-10 spots per hectare
Step 3: Sample 0-15 cm depth (annual crops) or 0-30 cm (perennials)
Step 4: Mix in clean plastic bucket
Step 5: Air dry, crush, sieve (2mm)
Step 6: Submit 500g composite sample

Label Information:
- Farmer name, mobile number
- Village, taluka, district, survey number
- Previous/current crop
- Date and depth of sampling

4. PARAMETERS TESTED
Basic (Free under SHC):
pH, EC, OC, Available N, P, K, S, Zn, Fe, Mn, Cu, B

Advanced (Paid):
- Mechanical analysis (texture)
- Cation exchange capacity
- Calcium carbonate
- Heavy metals
- Microbial biomass

5. INTERPRETATION GUIDE
pH Levels:
<5.5 Strongly acidic, 5.5-6.5 Slightly acidic, 6.5-7.5 Optimal, 7.5-8.5 Slightly alkaline, >8.5 Strongly alkaline

EC (Salinity):
<2 dS/m Normal, 2-4 Critical, 4-8 Saline, >8 Highly saline

Organic Carbon:
<0.4% Very low, 0.4-0.8% Low, 0.8-1.2% Medium, >1.2% High

6. LABORATORIES IN GUJARAT
Government (Free):
- District Soil Testing Labs
- Mobile testing vans
- KVKs
- Krishi Bhavans

Private (Rs. 500-2000):
- Faster turnaround (3-5 days)
- Additional parameters
- Home sampling available

7. FERTILIZER RECOMMENDATIONS
Based on Soil Test Results:
Low status: 100% recommended dose
Medium status: 75-80% dose
High status: 50-60% dose

8. COMMON MISTAKES
- Sampling unusual spots
- Using dirty containers
- Sampling after fertilizer application
- Mixing different fields
- Sending wet samples
- Incomplete labeling

9. CASE STUDIES
Rajkot Cotton Farmer:
- Before: Rs. 4,500/acre wasted on unneeded fertilizers
- After soil test: Saved Rs. 3,200/acre, yield up 12%

Mehsana Vegetable Grower:
- Problem: Poor growth despite high fertilizer
- Soil test: Zinc deficiency, high pH
- Correction: Zinc + pH management
- Result: 30% yield increase
