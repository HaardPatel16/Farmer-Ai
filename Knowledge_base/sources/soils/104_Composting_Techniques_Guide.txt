1. INTRODUCTION
Composting is the biological decomposition of organic materials into stable, humus-like product. It is essential for sustainable farming in Gujarat.

2. TYPES OF COMPOSTING
Farmyard Manure (FYM):
- Materials: Cattle dung, urine, litter, feed refuse
- Method: Pit or heap method
- Duration: 4-6 months
- Quantity: 10-20 t/ha application

Village Compost:
- Materials: Crop residues, weeds, household waste
- Method: Heap composting
- Duration: 3-4 months
- Turnover: Monthly turning

Urban Compost:
- Materials: City garbage, market waste
- Processing: Mechanical + biological
- Quality standards: Heavy metal limits

3. COMPOSTING PROCESS
Phase 1: Mesophilic (5-10 days)
- Temperature: 20-40C
- Microbes: Bacteria, fungi
- Activity: Rapid decomposition

Phase 2: Thermophilic (10-20 days)
- Temperature: 40-65C
- Pathogen kill, weed seed destruction
- Turn for aeration

Phase 3: Cooling and Maturation (15-30 days)
- Temperature returns to ambient
- Humus formation
- Earthworm colonization

Phase 4: Curing (15-30 days)
- Stabilization
- Ready for use

4. COMPOSTING METHODS
Pit Method:
- Size: 3m x 2m x 1m
- Layering: Alternate dry and wet materials
- Turn at 15, 30, 45 days
- Ready in 60-90 days

Heap Method:
- Size: 3m wide x 1.5m high, length as needed
- Aeration: Turn weekly in first month
- Water: Maintain 50-60% moisture
- Ready in 60-90 days

NADEP Composting:
- Brick tank with holes
- Layering: Biomass + soil + water
- Duration: 90-120 days
- Quality: Fine, odorless compost

Bokashi Composting:
- Fermentation method
- Effective microorganisms (EM)
- Pre-digestion before composting
- Suitable for kitchen waste

5. C:N RATIO MANAGEMENT
Ideal C:N ratio: 25-30:1
High carbon materials (C:N > 60): Straw, sawdust, wood chips
Low carbon materials (C:N < 20): Fresh manure, legumes, food waste
Balancing: Mix high and low C:N materials

6. COMPOST QUALITY PARAMETERS
Physical:
- Dark brown to black color
- Crumbly texture
- Earthy smell (no ammonia)
- Free of pathogens and weed seeds

Chemical:
- pH: 6.5-7.5
- EC: <4 dS/m
- C:N ratio: <20:1
- Moisture: 40-50%

7. APPLICATION IN GUJARAT CONDITIONS
Kharif crops: Apply 2-3 weeks before sowing
Rabi crops: Apply at land preparation
Perennial crops: Apply in basins during monsoon
Dose: 5-20 t/ha depending on crop

8. BENEFITS
- Improves soil structure
- Enhances water holding capacity
- Supplies slow-release nutrients
- Promotes microbial activity
- Reduces chemical fertilizer need
- Waste recycling

9. TROUBLESHOOTING
Problem: Bad odor
Cause: Too wet or compacted
Solution: Turn pile, add dry material

Problem: Not heating
Cause: Insufficient nitrogen or water
Solution: Add green material or water

Problem: Attracts pests
Cause: Food waste exposed
Solution: Bury food scraps, cover with soil

10. ECONOMICS
Cost of production: Rs. 2-5/kg
Value to farmer: Rs. 8-15/kg (fertilizer replacement)
Transportation: Use locally available materials
Labor: Family labor for small quantities
