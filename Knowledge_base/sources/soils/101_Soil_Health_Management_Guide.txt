1. INTRODUCTION
Soil health is the foundation of sustainable agriculture in Gujarat. With 196 lakh hectares under cultivation, maintaining soil health is critical for food security and farmer income.

2. SOIL HEALTH COMPONENTS
Physical Health:
- Soil structure and aggregation
- Water infiltration and retention
- Root penetration ease
- Compaction status

Chemical Health:
- pH balance (6.0-7.5 optimal)
- Nutrient availability (N, P, K, micronutrients)
- Cation exchange capacity
- Salinity levels (EC < 4 dS/m)

Biological Health:
- Microbial diversity and activity
- Earthworm population (>5/spade full = healthy)
- Organic matter content (>0.8% desirable)
- Mycorrhizal associations

3. SOIL HEALTH INDICATORS FOR GUJARAT FARMS
Visual Indicators:
- Dark brown color (high organic matter)
- Crumbly structure (good aggregation)
- Earthworm presence
- Rapid water infiltration
- Uniform crop growth

Laboratory Indicators:
- pH, EC, OC, N, P, K, S, micronutrients
- Microbial biomass carbon
- Soil respiration rate
- Enzyme activities

4. MAJOR SOIL TYPES AND HEALTH ISSUES
Black Cotton Soils (45% area):
- Strengths: High fertility, water retention
- Issues: Cracking, hardening, erosion
- Management: Organic matter addition, mulching

Alluvial Soils (25% area):
- Strengths: Deep, fertile, well-drained
- Issues: Waterlogging in low areas
- Management: Drainage, raised beds

Sandy Soils (15% area):
- Strengths: Quick warming, easy tillage
- Issues: Low water holding, nutrient leaching
- Management: Organic matter, frequent irrigation

Saline-Alkali Soils (10% area):
- Strengths: Can be reclaimed
- Issues: High pH, poor structure
- Management: Gypsum, leaching, tolerant crops

5. SOIL HEALTH IMPROVEMENT PRACTICES
Immediate (1 season):
- Soil testing
- Balanced fertilization
- Mulching
- Green manuring

Short-term (1-3 years):
- Compost application (5-10 t/ha/year)
- Vermicompost integration
- Biofertilizer use
- Crop rotation with legumes

Long-term (3+ years):
- Organic carbon buildup
- Reduced tillage adoption
- Agroforestry integration
- Comprehensive nutrient management

6. SOIL HEALTH CARD SCHEME IN GUJARAT
Coverage:
- Free testing for all farmers
- 12 parameters analyzed
- Testing every 2 years
- Fertilizer recommendations provided

How to Avail:
- Contact District Agriculture Office
- Submit soil sample (500g)
- Register at soilhealth.dac.gov.in
- Receive card with recommendations

7. SUCCESS STORIES
Case 1: Banaskantha Wheat Farmer
- Problem: Declining yields
- Intervention: Soil testing + balanced fertilization
- Result: 25% yield increase, Rs. 8,000/acre additional income

Case 2: Amreli Organic Transition
- Problem: Soil degradation
- Intervention: 3-year organic conversion
- Result: Organic carbon 0.4% to 0.9%
