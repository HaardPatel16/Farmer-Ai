Red laterite soil Gujarat: Found in tribal belt and hilly regions of Chhota Udaipur, Narmada, Dahod, and parts of Panchmahal districts. Formed by intense weathering and leaching of silica, leaving iron and aluminium oxides — distinctive red to yellowish-red colour. Properties: highly acidic (pH 4.5-5.5), very low in organic matter (below 0.5%), low in nitrogen, phosphorus, calcium, and most nutrients; phosphorus gets fixed by iron and aluminium. Soil is hard and compact when dry but sticky when wet. Suitable crops: cashew, rubber, pineapple, forest trees — traditional tribal area crops; also rice in valleys with heavy irrigation. Poor for most field crops without heavy amendment. Correction: lime application 2-4 tonnes per hectare raises pH and frees phosphorus; heavy organic matter addition (FYM 20-25 tonnes/ha + green manure) essential every year; micronutrient applications especially zinc, boron, molybdenum needed.
