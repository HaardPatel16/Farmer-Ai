================================================================================
FARMER SAHAYAK - BHARUCH DISTRICT AGRO-CLIMATIC PROFILE
================================================================================

1. DISTRICT OVERVIEW
--------------------
Bharuch is one of the oldest and most agriculturally fertile districts in
Gujarat, situated in the South Gujarat region along the Narmada river. The
district covers approximately 6,500 sq km with a population of about 1.5 million.
It has 9 talukas: Bharuch, Ankleshwar, Amod, Vagra, Jambusar, Hansot, Valia,
Jhagadia, and Netrang. The district is historically significant as a major
trading port and has some of the most fertile soils in Gujarat. Bharuch is
also an industrial hub with the Gujarat Industrial Development Corporation
(GIDC) and several chemical and textile industries.

2. AGRO-CLIMATIC ZONE
---------------------
Bharuch falls in the South Gujarat Agro-Climatic Zone. The climate is tropical
monsoon with humid summers and mild winters. The district receives good annual
rainfall of 900-1000 mm, well-distributed from June to September. The average
maximum temperature ranges from 30°C in January to 42°C in May, while minimum
temperatures range from 12°C in winter to 28°C in summer. Relative humidity is
high (75-85%) during the monsoon and moderate (40-55%) in winter. The proximity
to the Narmada river and the Gulf of Khambhat creates a favourable microclimate.

3. SOIL TYPES
-------------
The dominant soil types are:
• Deep black cotton soil (Vertisols) covering 60-70% of the district
• Alluvial soils along the Narmada and Kim rivers
• Sandy loam in coastal talukas (Hansot, Vagra)
• Saline soils in some low-lying coastal areas

The deep black soils are highly fertile with excellent water retention capacity.
Soil texture is clayey to clay-loam. Soil pH ranges from 7.0 to 8.5, generally
neutral to mildly alkaline. Fertility status is high due to regular alluvial
deposition. Organic carbon content is moderate to high (0.6-1.0%). The deep
black soils are ideal for cotton, paddy, and sugarcane.

4. WATER RESOURCES
------------------
Annual rainfall: 900-1000 mm (good, well-distributed)
Irrigation sources:
• Narmada main canal: Passes through the district, providing extensive
  irrigation coverage to Ankleshwar, Bharuch, Jambusar, and Amod talukas
• Kim and Karjan rivers: Provide surface water for irrigation
• Groundwater: Tubewells and borewells; water table is relatively shallow
  (10-30 metres) in most talukas
• Check dams and village ponds for supplementary irrigation

Water issues: The district is among the best-irrigated in Gujarat. However,
industrial pollution from Ankleshwar GIDC affects water quality in some areas.
Coastal talukas face minor salinity issues. The Narmada canal system ensures
reliable water supply for most of the district.

5. MAJOR CROPS
--------------
KHARIF (June-October):
• Cotton - 80,000 ha, varieties: G Cot-13, G Cot-21, Bt hybrids
• Paddy (Rice) - 60,000 ha, varieties: GR-11, GR-12, Gurjari, Pusa-44
• Tur (Pigeon pea) - 25,000 ha, varieties: GTP-82, GT-101, BDN-2
• Banana - 20,000 ha, varieties: Grand Naine, Dwarf Cavendish
• Sugarcane - 15,000 ha, varieties: Co-86032, Co-8014

RABI (November-March):
• Wheat - 50,000 ha, varieties: GW-366, GW-496, Lok-1
• Gram (Chickpea) - 20,000 ha, varieties: GG-5, GG-7
• Mustard - 10,000 ha, varieties: GJM-2, GJM-3
• Cumin - 8,000 ha in irrigated pockets
• Potato - 5,000 ha

SUMMER (March-May):
• Vegetables: Tomato, Brinjal, Okra, Chilli, Cucurbits
• Fodder crops: Hybrid Napier, Jowar, Lucerne

Productivity: Cotton 3.5-4.5 quintals/ha lint; Paddy 6.0-7.5 tonnes/ha;
Wheat 5.5-6.5 tonnes/ha; Banana 60-80 tonnes/ha; Sugarcane 800-1000 tonnes/ha.

6. HORTICULTURE
---------------
• Banana: Large-scale cultivation in Vagra, Jambusar, and Ankleshwar talukas
• Sugarcane: Important cash crop in Narmada command areas
• Vegetables: Tomato, brinjal, okra, chilli, cluster beans in irrigated belts
• Mango: Kesar and Alphonso in home gardens and small orchards
• Flowers: Marigold and rose for urban markets and religious use

7. LIVESTOCK
------------
• Cattle: Crossbred dairy cattle (Holstein-Friesian x Gir) dominate; Gir
  breed maintained for conservation
• Buffalo: Surti and Mehsani breeds; significant for milk production
• Poultry: Commercial broiler and layer farms, especially near Ankleshwar
• Goats and Sheep: Significant population in tribal areas (Netrang, Jhagadia)
• Fodder availability: Good in irrigated areas. Hybrid Napier, Jowar, and
  Lucerne are grown. Dairy cooperatives are well-developed.

9. COMMON PESTS & DISEASES
--------------------------
• Cotton: Pink bollworm, Spotted bollworm, Aphids, Whitefly (CLCuV vector)
• Paddy: Stem borer, Brown plant hopper (BPH), Leaf folder, Blast
• Tur: Pod borer (Helicoverpa), Wilt (Fusarium), Phytophthora blight
• Sugarcane: Early shoot borer, Top borer, Pyrilla (leafhopper), Red rot
• Banana: Panama wilt, Sigatoka leaf spot, Nematodes, Banana weevil
• Wheat: Rust, Karnal bunt, Aphids
• Vegetables: Fruit borer, Whitefly, Aphids, Downy mildew, Root rot

10. KEY CHALLENGES
------------------
• Industrial pollution from Ankleshwar GIDC affecting water and soil in
  some areas
• Declining water quality in Kim river due to effluent discharge
• Labour shortage during peak cotton and paddy seasons
• Soil salinity in low-lying coastal areas
• Declining soil organic matter in intensively cultivated areas
• Market access for small tribal farmers in Netrang and Jhagadia
• Climate change affecting monsoon reliability

11. GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
----------------------
• KVK: Krishi Vigyan Kendra, Bharuch (affiliated with Navsari Agricultural
  University) - provides training and demonstrations
• ATMA: Active extension programmes in all talukas
• Soil Testing Labs: District soil testing lab at Bharuch
• APMCs: Bharuch (main), Ankleshwar, Jambusar, Amod, Hansot
• Cold Storages: Cold storage units for potatoes and bananas
• Dairy Support: Amul and Sabar dairy cooperatives; veterinary services
• Narmada Canal: SSNNL provides excellent irrigation infrastructure

12. FARMER ADVISORIES
---------------------
• Adopt drip irrigation for banana, cotton, and vegetables to improve water
  use efficiency and yields by 20-30%
• Use certified seeds and practice crop rotation to reduce soil-borne diseases
• Monitor water quality if irrigating from industrial areas; test for heavy
  metals periodically
• Grow leguminous green manure (Dhaincha, Sunnhemp) during summer fallow
  to improve soil nitrogen and organic matter
• Apply soil test-based fertilizers to maintain soil fertility; address
  zinc and sulphur deficiency in intensively cropped areas
• For sugarcane, adopt trash mulching to conserve soil moisture and improve
  soil organic carbon
• Practice IPM (Integrated Pest Management) for cotton to reduce pesticide use
  and preserve natural enemies
• Maintain soil health by applying FYM (10 tonnes/ha) and vermicompost regularly
