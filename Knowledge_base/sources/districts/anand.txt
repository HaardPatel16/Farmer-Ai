FARMER SAHAYAK - ANAND DISTRICT AGRO-CLIMATIC PROFILE
================================================================================

1. DISTRICT OVERVIEW
--------------------
Anand is a historic district in the Middle Gujarat region, famous as the
birthplace of India's White Revolution and the Amul dairy cooperative
movement. The district covers approximately 3,200 sq km with a population
of about 2.1 million. It is divided into 8 talukas: Anand, Petlad, Borsad,
Umreth, Khambhat, Tarapur, Anklav, and Sojitra. The district has a highly
developed agricultural economy with excellent irrigation infrastructure and
strong dairy linkages. It is one of Gujarat's most prosperous agricultural
districts.

2. AGRO-CLIMATIC ZONE
---------------------
Anand falls in the Middle Gujarat Agro-Climatic Zone. The climate is subtropical
with a humid summer and mild winter. The district receives moderate to good
annual rainfall of 800-900 mm, well-distributed across the monsoon months
(June-September). The average maximum temperature ranges from 29°C in January
to 42°C in May, while minimum temperatures range from 11°C in winter to 28°C
in summer. Relative humidity is high during the monsoon (75-85%) and moderate
in winter (40-55%). The district benefits from the moderating influence of the
Gulf of Khambhat.

3. SOIL TYPES
-------------
The dominant soil types are:
• Goradu (sandy loam) soil - well-drained, fertile, covering 60% of the district
• Medium black soil in eastern talukas
• Alluvial soils along the Mahi river belt
• Coastal saline soils in Khambhat taluka near the Gulf of Khambhat
• Lateritic patches in some upland areas

Soil texture is predominantly sandy loam to loam. Soil pH ranges from 7.0
to 8.2, generally neutral to mildly alkaline. The Goradu soil is highly
fertile and well-suited for tobacco, banana, and vegetable cultivation.
Organic carbon content is moderate to high (0.5-0.8%). Soil fertility is
maintained well due to intensive dairy farming and organic manure application.

4. WATER RESOURCES
------------------
Annual rainfall: 800-900 mm (reliable, well-distributed)
Irrigation sources:
• Mahi canal system (Sardar Sarovar Narmada Nigam Ltd) - extensive coverage
• Groundwater: Tubewells and borewells; water table is relatively stable
  at 15-40 metres in most talukas
• Village ponds and farm ponds for supplementary irrigation
• Check dams on seasonal rivers and streams

Water issues: Khambhat taluka faces coastal salinity ingress in groundwater.
Some eastern areas have falling water tables due to intensive irrigation for
tobacco and banana. The Mahi canal system provides excellent irrigation
security for most of the district.

5. MAJOR CROPS
--------------
KHARIF (June-October):
• Paddy (Rice) - 50,000 ha, varieties: GR-11, GR-12, Gurjari
• Cotton - 60,000 ha, varieties: G Cot-13, G Cot-21, Bt hybrids
• Banana - 30,000 ha, varieties: Grand Naine, Dwarf Cavendish
• Bajra - 20,000 ha, varieties: GHB-558, GHB-732
• Groundnut - 25,000 ha, varieties: GG-20, GG-22

RABI (November-March):
• Wheat - 70,000 ha, varieties: GW-366, GW-496, Lok-1
• Tobacco - 35,000 ha, varieties: Anand-2, Anand-3, GT-7 (Virginia tobacco)
• Gram (Chickpea) - 15,000 ha, varieties: GG-5, GG-7
• Cumin - 12,000 ha in irrigated pockets
• Mustard - 10,000 ha

SUMMER (March-May):
• Vegetables - 20,000 ha: Tomato, Brinjal, Okra, Chilli, Cucurbits
• Fodder crops - Hybrid Napier, Jowar, Lucerne for dairy

Productivity: Wheat 5.5-6.5 tonnes/ha; Tobacco 2.5-3.0 tonnes/ha cured leaf;
Banana 50-70 tonnes/ha; Paddy 5.0-6.5 tonnes/ha; Cotton 3.0-4.0 quintals/ha lint.

6. HORTICULTURE
---------------
Anand is a major horticultural district:
• Banana: Large-scale cultivation in Petlad, Borsad, Anand talukas; tissue
  culture Grand Naine is dominant
• Mango: Kesar and Alphonso in garden orchards
• Vegetables: Intensive peri-urban vegetable cultivation around Anand,
  Petlad, and Borsad towns; tomato, brinjal, okra, chilli, cluster beans
• Flowers: Marigold and rose for temple and urban markets
• Spices: Turmeric and ginger in small pockets

7. LIVESTOCK
------------
Anand is Gujarat's dairy heartland:
• Cattle: Crossbred (Holstein-Friesian x Gir, Jersey x Gir) dominate dairy;
  Gir breed maintained for conservation and breeding
• Buffalo: Mehsani and Surti breeds; significant milk producers
• Poultry: Commercial broiler farms and layer units
• Dairy infrastructure: Amul (GCMMF), Sabar Dairy, multiple private dairies
• Fodder availability: Excellent. Hybrid Napier, Jowar, Maize, Lucerne, and
  cowpea are grown extensively. Most farmers reserve 20-25% of land for
  fodder to maintain 3-5 milch animals per farm.
• Milk production: Average 15-25 litres/day per crossbred cow

9. COMMON PESTS & DISEASES
--------------------------
• Tobacco: Tobacco caterpillar (Spodoptera), Aphids, Root-knot nematode,
  Mosaic virus, Black shank (Phytophthora nicotianae)
• Banana: Sigatoka leaf spot, Panama wilt (Fusarium oxysporum), Nematodes,
  Banana weevil (Cosmopolites sordidus)
• Cotton: Pink bollworm, Whitefly, Aphids, Leaf curl virus
• Paddy: Stem borer, Brown plant hopper (BPH), Leaf folder, Blast (Pyricularia)
• Wheat: Rust, Karnal bunt, Aphids
• Vegetables: Fruit borer, Whitefly, Aphids, Downy mildew, Root rot

10. KEY CHALLENGES
------------------
• Groundwater depletion in tobacco-intensive areas (Borsad, Umreth)
• Banana Panama wilt disease spreading in some areas
• Tobacco crop requires high labour input (transplanting, topping, harvesting)
• Labour shortage during peak tobacco season
• Soil salinity in Khambhat coastal belt
• Declining micronutrient levels (zinc, boron) in intensive cropping areas
• Climate change affecting monsoon reliability and increasing temperature extremes

11. GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
----------------------
• KVK: Krishi Vigyan Kendra, Anand (Gujarat Agricultural University) -
  provides training, demonstrations, and seed production
• ATMA: Very active in Anand with multiple farmer-interest groups (FIGs)
• Soil Testing Labs: District-level soil testing lab at Anand and mobile vans
• APMCs: Anand (major), Petlad, Borsad, Khambhat, Umreth
• Cold Storages: Multiple cold storage units for potatoes and bananas
• Dairy Support: Amul dairy cooperatives in every village; cattle breeding
  centres, AI (Artificial Insemination) services, veterinary hospitals

12. FARMER ADVISORIES
---------------------
• Tobacco farmers should practice crop rotation with wheat or gram; avoid
  continuous tobacco cropping to reduce nematode and soil-borne disease pressure
• Banana farmers must use tissue-culture plants and practice sanitation to
  prevent Panama wilt; do not plant banana in Fusarium-infested fields for
  at least 3-4 years
• Maintain soil health through regular organic manure application (FYM,
  vermicompost, biogas slurry) - aim for 10-15 tonnes FYM/ha/year
• Use drip irrigation for banana, tobacco, and vegetables to improve water
  use efficiency and crop yields
• Grow leguminous green manure (Sunnhemp, Dhaincha) during summer fallow
  to improve soil nitrogen and organic matter
• Dairy farmers should ensure balanced ration for crossbred cattle; use
  bypass protein feed (cottonseed cake, mustard cake) to boost milk production
• Apply zinc sulphate @ 25 kg/ha in zinc-deficient soils before Kharif sowing
• Practice paddy-straw management by composting or incorporating into soil
  rather than burning to maintain soil organic carbon
