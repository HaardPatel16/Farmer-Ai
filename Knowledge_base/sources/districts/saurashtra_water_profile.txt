Saurashtra water availability profile (Rajkot, Amreli, Bhavnagar, Junagadh, Gir Somnath, Jamnagar, Porbandar, Botad, Morbi): Saurashtra has complex water situation — coastal districts have saline water, inland districts depend on rainfall and borewell, Narmada water through Saurashtra Branch Canal is transforming agriculture. Water sources: (1) Rainfall — primary for Kharif crops; Saurashtra receives 500-800mm annually, most concentrated in July-August; groundnut, cotton, castor grown on monsoon soil moisture. (2) Borewells — typically 60-150 metres deep in interior Saurashtra; areas near coast have saline borewell water (EC 2-8 dS/m) unsuitable for sensitive crops; test water before use. (3) Narmada water — Saurashtra Branch Canal now delivers water to Rajkot, Surendranagar, Morbi, Jamnagar, Botad districts; dramatically changed cropping pattern — enables Rabi crop irrigation in formerly rainfed areas. Check dams: Saurashtra Jaldhara has built thousands of check dams across small rivers — recharging groundwater and providing summer irrigation in farm ponds. Critical point for coastal Saurashtra (Jamnagar, Porbandar, Gir Somnath, Amreli coastal): borewell water salinity is a major issue; crop selection must consider salinity tolerance; test water twice annually; apply gypsum 500kg/ha to manage sodium; grow salt-tolerant crops (barley, cumin, date palm, Ber) in saline water zones. Canal water availability: check SSNNL (Sardar Sarovar Narmada Nigam Ltd) bulletin at sardarssarovardam.gujarat.gov.in for canal release schedules in your district — water allocation varies by year and monsoon season.
