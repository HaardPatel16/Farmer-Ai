North Gujarat water availability profile (Banaskantha, Mehsana, Patan, Sabarkantha): North Gujarat faces the most serious water scarcity in the state — over-extraction of groundwater has created a crisis requiring urgent management. Current situation: water table has fallen 10-40 metres over last 20 years in most of Banaskantha and Mehsana; many borewells now reach 200-300 metres depth (deep aquifer). Irrigation water sources: (1) Borewells — primary source; electricity through Jyotigram scheme (8 hours agricultural power per day); submersible pump sets dominant; recharge rate often lower than extraction. (2) Canal water — Narmada main canal reaches some parts of North Gujarat (Banaskantha Mehsana branch); Dharoi and Dantiwada reservoir commands limited areas. (3) Rainwater harvesting — check dams (Government of Gujarat has built 14,000+ check dams across Gujarat since 2001); farm ponds under MNREGA; watershed development programme areas. District water table depth: Radhanpur (Patan) — 80-120 metres; Deesa (Banaskantha) — 40-80 metres; Mehsana town — 30-60 metres; Patan town — 60-100 metres. Saline water problem: shallow aquifer water (first 30-40 metres) in Patan, some Mehsana blocks is saline (EC above 4 dS/m) — not usable for most crops. Deeper aquifers (80m+) are often fresh. Crop recommendation for water-scarce areas: isabgol, cumin, fennel, bajra (rainfed), castor — crops with low water requirement. Avoid banana, sugarcane, papaya in severe water-scarce areas — these crops are economically destroying deep aquifers. Drip adoption: GGRC (Gujarat Green Revolution Company) provides micro-irrigation subsidy — highest utilisation of drip is in North Gujarat precisely because borewell water is precious.
