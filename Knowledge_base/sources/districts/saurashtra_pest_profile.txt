Saurashtra region (Rajkot, Amreli, Bhavnagar, Junagadh, Gir Somnath, Jamnagar, Porbandar) major pest problems: Saurashtra is Gujarat's most important agricultural region — groundnut, cotton, castor, sesame, and bajra dominate. Groundnut: late leaf spot (Phaeoisariopsis personata) and early leaf spot (Cercospora arachidicola) are endemic — begin scouting at 30 days after sowing; spray mancozeb 2.5g/litre or chlorothalonil 2g/litre at 7-10 day intervals from first symptoms. Tobacco caterpillar (Spodoptera litura) on groundnut is a major Kharif problem — cluster egg masses on leaves (yellow-green masses with brown hair); destroy masses manually; spray chlorpyrifos 2ml/litre or emamectin benzoate 0.4g/litre when early instar larvae are present. Jassids on groundnut cause "hopperburn" — spray imidacloprid 0.3ml/litre. Tikka disease (both leaf spots together) — economic threshold 3 spots per leaf. Cotton: pink bollworm (Pectinophora gossypiella) is Saurashtra's dominant cotton pest; install pheromone traps from day 30 after sowing, threshold 8 moths per trap per week triggers spray; spray emamectin benzoate 0.4g/litre. Thrips on cotton seedlings (June-July) transmit cotton leaf curl virus — spray fipronil 1ml/litre at seedling stage. Castor semi-looper (Achaea janata) causes 80% defoliation in outbreak years — spray chlorpyrifos. Sesame (til): gall midge causes abnormal growth of shoot tip — spray dimethoate 2ml/litre at 15 and 30 days. Thrips cause silver streaking on sesame leaves — spray fipronil 1ml/litre.
