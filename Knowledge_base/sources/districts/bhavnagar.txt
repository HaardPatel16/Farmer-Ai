FARMER SAHAYAK - BHAVNAGAR DISTRICT AGRO-CLIMATIC PROFILE
================================================================================

1. DISTRICT OVERVIEW
--------------------
Bhavnagar is a coastal district in the Saurashtra region of Gujarat, located
on the Gulf of Khambhat. It covers approximately 10,000 sq km with a population
of about 2.9 million. The district has 13 talukas: Bhavnagar, Botad (now
separate district), Gadhada, Gariadhar, Ghogha, Jesar, Mahuva, Palitana,
Sihor, Talaja, Umrala, Vallabhipur, and Valukad. The district is known for its
historic port city, the Palitana Jain temples, and a growing agricultural
economy. Bhavnagar is increasingly recognized as a major onion-growing belt and
has significant salt production along its coast.

2. AGRO-CLIMATIC ZONE
---------------------
Bhavnagar falls in the South Saurashtra Agro-Climatic Zone. The climate is
tropical monsoon with semi-arid tendencies. The district receives moderate
annual rainfall of 700-800 mm, concentrated in July and August. The average
maximum temperature ranges from 30°C in January to 42°C in May, while minimum
temperatures range from 10°C in winter to 27°C in summer. Relative humidity
peaks at 80-85% during the monsoon and drops to 25-30% in summer. The coastal
talukas (Ghogha, Mahuva, Talaja) experience moderated temperatures due to
maritime influence. The district is prone to cyclonic storms from the Arabian Sea.

3. SOIL TYPES
-------------
The dominant soil types are:
• Medium black soil (Vertisols) in central and eastern talukas
• Coastal alluvial sandy soils in Ghogha, Mahuva, and Talaja talukas
• Saline-alkali soils in low-lying coastal areas and Rann border regions
• Shallow rocky soils in hilly areas near Palitana

Soil texture is predominantly clayey to clay-loam in black soil areas and sandy
near the coast. Soil pH ranges from 7.5 to 9.0, with coastal areas being more
alkaline. Fertility status is moderate in black soil areas but low in coastal
sandy and saline soils. Organic carbon content is low to medium (0.3-0.6%).
Coastal areas require gypsum and organic amendments for sustainable cultivation.

4. WATER RESOURCES
------------------
Annual rainfall: 700-800 mm (moderate, variable)
Irrigation sources:
• Shetrunji dam canal system: Serves parts of Palitana, Gadhada, and
  Vallabhipur talukas
• Borewells and tubewells: Dominant source; depths 80-200 metres
• Check dams and farm ponds: Extensive construction under Sujalam Sufalam
• Village ponds (talavs) for supplementary irrigation
• Narmada water: Saurashtra Branch Canal reaches some parts of the district

Water issues: Groundwater stress is significant in central talukas. Coastal
salinity ingress affects shallow aquifers in Ghogha, Mahuva, and Talaja. The
Shetrunji canal provides limited coverage. Water table has declined 10-20 metres
in the last two decades in intensively cultivated areas.

5. MAJOR CROPS
--------------
KHARIF (June-October):
• Cotton - 70,000 ha, varieties: G Cot-13, G Cot-21, Bt hybrids
• Groundnut - 80,000 ha, varieties: GG-20, GG-22, GG-7, TMV-2
• Bajra (Pearl millet) - 40,000 ha, varieties: GHB-558, GHB-732
• Onion - 25,000 ha, varieties: Bhima Shakti, Bhima Super, N-53
• Castor - 20,000 ha, varieties: GCH-7, GCH-9

RABI (November-March):
• Wheat - 50,000 ha, varieties: GW-366, GW-496, Lok-1
• Gram (Chickpea) - 20,000 ha, varieties: GG-5, GG-7
• Cumin - 15,000 ha, varieties: GC-1, GC-4
• Onion - 15,000 ha (Rabi onion), varieties: Bhima Shakti, N-53
• Mustard - 10,000 ha, varieties: GJM-2, GJM-3

SUMMER (March-May):
• Vegetables: Tomato, Brinjal, Okra, Chilli
• Fodder: Hybrid Napier, Jowar for dairy

Productivity: Groundnut 15-20 quintals/ha pods; Cotton 2.5-3.5 quintals/ha lint;
Onion 250-350 quintals/ha; Wheat 4.5-5.5 tonnes/ha (irrigated); Cumin 4-6 quintals/ha.

6. HORTICULTURE
---------------
• Onion: Bhavnagar is a major onion-growing belt, supplying markets across India
• Mango: Kesar and Alphonso in Palitana and Gariadhar talukas
• Vegetables: Tomato, brinjal, okra, chilli in irrigated pockets
• Coconut: Coastal talukas have coconut plantations
• Fodder crops: Hybrid Napier, Jowar, Lucerne for dairy cattle

7. LIVESTOCK
------------
• Cattle: Gir breed is native to the region; crossbred dairy cattle increasing
• Buffalo: Surti and Mehsani breeds; important for milk production
• Poultry: Growing commercial broiler and layer farms
• Sheep and Goats: Significant population in semi-arid talukas
• Fodder availability: Moderate in irrigated areas; rainfed areas depend on
  crop residues. Fodder shortage is acute in summer and drought years.

9. COMMON PESTS & DISEASES
--------------------------
• Groundnut: Late leaf spot (Tikka), Early leaf spot, Tobacco caterpillar,
  Jassids, Thrips (PBNV vector)
• Cotton: Pink bollworm, Spotted bollworm, Aphids, Whitefly
• Onion: Thrips, Purple blotch (Alternaria porri), Stemphylium leaf blight,
  Downy mildew
• Wheat: Rust, Karnal bunt, Aphids
• Bajra: Downy mildew (Green ear), Ergot, Smut
• Castor: Semi-looper (Achaea janata), Capsule borer

10. KEY CHALLENGES
------------------
• Coastal salinity ingress in Ghogha, Mahuva, and Talaja talukas
• Groundwater depletion in central and western talukas
• Erratic monsoon leading to droughts or delayed sowing
• Declining soil fertility in intensively cultivated groundnut-onion areas
• Labour shortage during peak onion harvesting and transplanting
• Limited cold storage infrastructure for onion and vegetables
• Cyclone risk from Arabian Sea affecting coastal agriculture

11. GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
----------------------
• KVK: Krishi Vigyan Kendra, Bhavnagar (affiliated with Junagadh Agricultural
  University) - provides training and demonstrations
• ATMA: Active extension programmes in all talukas
• Soil Testing Labs: District soil testing lab at Bhavnagar; mobile vans
• APMCs: Bhavnagar (main), Botad, Palitana, Mahuva, Gadhada, Sihor
• Cold Storages: Limited cold storage for onions and potatoes
• Check Dam Schemes: Government has built numerous check dams under Sujalam
  Sufalam and Jaldhara programmes
• Narmada Canal: Saurashtra Branch Canal expanding coverage

12. FARMER ADVISORIES
---------------------
• Onion farmers should practice strict crop rotation (onion should not be
grown on the same field for more than 1 year in 3); rotate with groundnut,
  cotton, or bajra to reduce disease and nematode buildup
• Adopt drip irrigation for onion and cotton to save water and increase
  yields by 25-35%
• In coastal saline areas, apply gypsum @ 2 tonnes/ha and grow salt-tolerant
  crops like barley, cumin, or coconut initially
• Use seed treatment with thiram + carbendazim for groundnut to prevent
  seed rot and early blight
• For cotton, install pheromone traps from day 30 for pink bollworm monitoring;
  spray only at economic threshold levels
• Grow fodder crops (hybrid Napier, Lucerne) on 10-15% of land to support dairy
• Apply gypsum @ 500 kg/ha in groundnut at flowering stage for pod development
• Practice summer ploughing (May) to expose soil-borne pests and pathogens to
  solar heat
• Store onion in well-ventilated, dry conditions to prevent storage rot
• Use certified seeds and resistant varieties to manage purple blotch and
  downy mildew in onion
