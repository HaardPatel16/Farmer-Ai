FARMER SAHAYAK - GANDHINAGAR DISTRICT AGRO-CLIMATIC PROFILE
================================================================================

1. DISTRICT OVERVIEW
--------------------
Gandhinagar is the capital district of Gujarat, located in the north-central part
of the state. It is a relatively small district covering approximately 2,100 sq
km with a population of about 1.4 million. It comprises 4 talukas: Gandhinagar,
Kalol, Dehgam, and Mansa. The district is named after Mahatma Gandhi and serves
as the administrative capital of Gujarat. Despite its urban character, Gandhinagar
has a significant agricultural area, particularly in the peri-urban belt around
the capital city. The district is well-planned with extensive green cover and
good infrastructure.

2. AGRO-CLIMATIC ZONE
---------------------
Gandhinagar falls in the Middle Gujarat Agro-Climatic Zone. The climate is
semi-arid tropical with hot summers and mild winters. The district receives
moderate annual rainfall of 750-850 mm, with the monsoon from June to September.
The average maximum temperature ranges from 28°C in January to 43°C in May, while
minimum temperatures range from 10°C in winter to 27°C in summer. Relative humidity
peaks at 75-80% during the monsoon and drops to 25-30% in summer. The district
has a relatively favourable climate for agriculture due to good irrigation and
moderate rainfall.

3. SOIL TYPES
-------------
The dominant soil types are:
• Sandy loam (Goradu) soil covering 50% of the district
• Medium black soil (Vertisols) in eastern and southern talukas
• Alluvial soils along the Sabarmati river belt
• Saline-alkali patches in some low-lying areas

Soil texture is predominantly sandy loam to loam. Soil pH ranges from 7.0 to 8.5,
generally neutral to mildly alkaline. Fertility status is moderate to high in
irrigated areas but declining in rainfed tracts. Organic carbon content is
moderate (0.4-0.7%). Some areas show zinc and sulphur deficiency due to intensive
farming.

4. WATER RESOURCES
------------------
Annual rainfall: 750-850 mm (moderate, well-distributed)
Irrigation sources:
• Narmada canal system: Extensive coverage through Sabarmati branch and
  Dharoi dam command areas
• Sabarmati river: Provides surface water for some areas
• Groundwater: Tubewells and borewells; water table is relatively stable at
  15-40 metres in most talukas due to good recharge
• Check dams and farm ponds for supplementary irrigation

Water issues: The district is generally well-served with irrigation. Some areas
face minor groundwater depletion due to urban water demand. The peri-urban belt
has excellent irrigation security. Salinity is a minor issue in some low-lying
areas near the Sabarmati.

5. MAJOR CROPS
--------------
KHARIF (June-October):
• Cotton - 40,000 ha, varieties: G Cot-13, G Cot-21, Bt hybrids
• Bajra (Pearl millet) - 30,000 ha, varieties: GHB-558, GHB-732
• Paddy (Rice) - 20,000 ha in irrigated areas, varieties: GR-11, GR-12
• Castor - 15,000 ha, varieties: GCH-7, GCH-9
• Groundnut - 20,000 ha, varieties: GG-20, GG-22

RABI (November-March):
• Wheat - 50,000 ha, varieties: GW-366, GW-496, Lok-1
• Mustard - 10,000 ha, varieties: GJM-2, GJM-3
• Cumin - 8,000 ha, varieties: GC-1, GC-4
• Gram (Chickpea) - 10,000 ha, varieties: GG-5, GG-7
• Potato - 5,000 ha, varieties: Kufri Jyoti, Kufri Chandramukhi

SUMMER (March-May):
• Vegetables - 20,000 ha (peri-urban): Tomato, Brinjal, Okra, Chilli, Cucurbits
• Fodder crops: Hybrid Napier, Jowar, Lucerne for dairy

Productivity: Wheat 5.0-6.0 tonnes/ha; Cotton 3.0-4.0 quintals/ha lint;
Paddy 5.5-6.5 tonnes/ha; Groundnut 15-20 quintals/ha pods.

6. HORTICULTURE
---------------
• Vegetables: Extensive peri-urban vegetable cultivation around Gandhinagar city;
  tomato, brinjal, okra, chilli, cucumber, capsicum, beans
• Flowers: Rose, marigold, chrysanthemum for urban markets and government events
• Fruits: Mango, guava, papaya, banana in small orchards and home gardens
• Potato: Limited cultivation in irrigated areas
• The peri-urban belt supplies fresh produce to the capital city and Ahmedabad

7. LIVESTOCK
------------
• Cattle: Crossbred dairy cattle (Holstein-Friesian x Gir, Jersey x Gir) dominate
  in peri-urban areas; Gir breed maintained
• Buffalo: Mehsani and Surti breeds; dairy is important
• Poultry: Commercial broiler and layer farms, especially near Kalol and Mansa
• Goats and Sheep: Significant population in rural areas
• Fodder availability: Good in irrigated areas. Hybrid Napier, Jowar, Lucerne,
  and cowpea are grown. Dairy is well-developed with milk cooperatives.

9. COMMON PESTS & DISEASES
--------------------------
• Cotton: Pink bollworm, Aphids, Whitefly (CLCuV vector), Spodoptera
• Wheat: Rust, Karnal bunt, Aphids
• Paddy: Stem borer, Brown plant hopper (BPH), Leaf folder, Blast
• Groundnut: Late leaf spot (Tikka), Early leaf spot, Tobacco caterpillar
• Vegetables: Fruit borer, Whitefly, Aphids, Downy mildew, Root rot
• Castor: Semi-looper (Achaea janata), Capsule borer

10. KEY CHALLENGES
------------------
• Urbanization pressure: Farm land conversion to residential and industrial use
• Groundwater depletion in some areas due to urban water demand
• Soil salinity in low-lying areas near the Sabarmati
• Declining soil organic matter in intensive vegetable-growing areas
• Labour shortage during peak sowing and harvesting seasons
• Industrial pollution affecting peri-urban agriculture near GIDC areas
• Climate change affecting monsoon reliability and increasing temperature extremes
• Market competition from organized retailers affecting small farmers

11. GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
----------------------
• KVK: Krishi Vigyan Kendra, Gandhinagar (affiliated with Gujarat Agricultural
  University) - provides training and demonstrations
• ATMA: Very active in Gandhinagar with multiple farmer-interest groups
• Soil Testing Labs: District-level soil testing lab at Gandhinagar; mobile vans
• APMCs: Gandhinagar, Kalol, Mansa, Dehgam
• Cold Storages: Multiple cold storage units for potatoes and vegetables
• Dairy Support: Amul and Sabar dairy cooperatives; cattle breeding centres,
  AI services, veterinary hospitals
• Narmada Canal: Excellent irrigation infrastructure
• Government Schemes: Extensive implementation of central and state schemes
  due to proximity to the capital

12. FARMER ADVISORIES
---------------------
• Peri-urban farmers should focus on high-value vegetables and flowers for better
  returns; direct marketing to city markets or through APMC e-trading
• Adopt drip irrigation for cotton and vegetables to save water and increase
  yields by 20-30%
• In saline-alkali patches, apply gypsum @ 2 tonnes/ha and grow salt-tolerant
  crops like barley or sorghum initially
• Use soil test-based fertilization; address zinc deficiency with ZnSO4 @ 25 kg/ha
  in deficient soils
• Rotate cotton with wheat or chickpea to reduce soil-borne diseases and improve
  soil health
• Grow leguminous green manure (Sunnhemp, Dhaincha) during summer fallow to
  improve soil nitrogen and organic matter
• For dairy farmers, grow Azolla as a supplementary cattle feed to reduce feed
  costs by 15-20%
• Maintain soil health through regular organic manure application (FYM,
  vermicompost, biogas slurry) - aim for 10-15 tonnes FYM/ha/year
• Practice IPM (Integrated Pest Management) for vegetables to reduce pesticide
  residues and meet urban market standards
• Use mulching (paddy straw, wheat straw) for vegetables to conserve soil moisture
  and reduce weed growth
