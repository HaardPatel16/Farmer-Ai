================================================================================
FARMER SAHAYAK - AMRELI DISTRICT AGRO-CLIMATIC PROFILE
================================================================================

1. DISTRICT OVERVIEW
--------------------
Amreli is a prominent district in the Saurashtra region of Gujarat, located
on the southern coast of the peninsula. The district covers approximately
7,400 sq km with a population of around 1.5 million. It is divided into 11
talukas: Amreli, Babra, Bagasara, Dhari, Jafrabad, Khambha, Kunkavav-
Vadan, Lathi, Liliya, Rajula, and Savarkundla. The district is known for its
vibrant groundnut oil milling industry and the Gir forest ecosystem that
extends into its eastern talukas. Amreli has a strong agrarian economy with
agriculture supporting over 60% of the population directly or indirectly.

2. AGRO-CLIMATIC ZONE
---------------------
Amreli falls in the South Saurashtra Agro-Climatic Zone. The climate is
tropical monsoon with semi-arid tendencies. The district receives moderate
annual rainfall of 700-800 mm, with the monsoon concentrated in July and
August. The average maximum temperature ranges from 30°C in January to 42°C
in May, while minimum temperatures range from 8°C in winter to 27°C in summer.
Relative humidity peaks at 80-85% during the monsoon and drops to 25-30% in
summer. The coastal talukas (Rajula, Jafrabad) experience moderated
temperatures due to maritime influence.

3. SOIL TYPES
-------------
The dominant soil types in Amreli are:
• Medium to deep black soil (Vertisols) in central and eastern talukas
• Coastal alluvial sandy soils in Rajula and Jafrabad talukas
• Red and mixed red-black soils in hilly Gir border areas
• Gravelly soils in undulating terrain near the Gir foothills

Soil texture is predominantly clayey to clay-loam in the black soil belt.
Soil pH ranges from 7.5 to 8.5, generally neutral to mildly alkaline. The
fertility status is moderate to high in traditional groundnut-cotton areas
but declining in intensively cultivated zones. Organic carbon content is low
to medium (0.3-0.6%). Some areas show sulphur and zinc deficiency.

4. WATER RESOURCES
------------------
Annual rainfall: 700-800 mm (variable, with drought years occurring)
Irrigation sources:
• Shetrunji River and associated check dams serve parts of Lathi and Dhari
• Borewells are the dominant irrigation source; depths range 100-250 metres
• Check dams and farm ponds (Narmada canal has not reached most of Amreli)
• Traditional village ponds (talavs) provide limited summer irrigation

Water issues: Groundwater stress is severe in central talukas like Babra
and Bagasara. Water table has fallen 15-30 metres in the last two decades.
The coastal talukas face salinity ingress in shallow aquifers. The Gir
forest catchment helps recharge groundwater in eastern talukas.

5. MAJOR CROPS
--------------
KHARIF (June-October):
• Groundnut - 1,20,000 ha, varieties: GG-20, GG-22, GG-7, TMV-2
• Cotton - 70,000 ha, varieties: G Cot-13, G Cot-21, Bt cotton hybrids
• Sesame (Til) - 20,000 ha, varieties: G Til-3, G Til-10
• Bajra - 25,000 ha, varieties: GHB-558, GHB-732
• Castor - 15,000 ha, varieties: GCH-7, GCH-9

RABI (November-March):
• Wheat - 40,000 ha (irrigated pockets), varieties: GW-366, GW-496
• Gram (Chickpea) - 15,000 ha, varieties: GG-5, GG-7
• Cumin - 10,000 ha in irrigated areas
• Mustard - 8,000 ha

SUMMER (March-May):
• Vegetables (limited) - Onion, Chilli, Brinjal

Productivity: Groundnut yields 12-18 quintals/ha pods under rainfed
conditions; cotton 2.5-3.5 quintals/ha lint; sesame 5-7 quintals/ha.

6. HORTICULTURE
---------------
• Mango: Kesar and Alphonso varieties in Gir border talukas (Lathi, Dhari)
• Coconut: Coastal talukas (Rajula, Jafrabad) have coconut plantations
• Vegetables: Tomato, brinjal, okra, chilli grown in small pockets
• Fodder: Hybrid Napier and Jowar for dairy cattle

7. LIVESTOCK
------------
• Cattle: Gir breed is native to the region; crossbred dairy cattle increasing
• Buffalo: Surti and Mehsani breeds; dairy is important in irrigated villages
• Poultry: Backyard poultry and small commercial farms
• Sheep and Goats: Significant population in semi-arid talukas
• Fodder availability: Rainfed jowar and bajra stalks; irrigated fodder
  limited to well-irrigated villages. Fodder shortage is acute in summer.

9. COMMON PESTS & DISEASES
--------------------------
• Groundnut: Late leaf spot (Tikka), Early leaf spot, Tobacco caterpillar
  (Spodoptera litura), Jassids, Thrips
• Cotton: Pink bollworm, Spotted bollworm, Aphids, Whitefly
• Sesame: Gall midge, Shoot fly, Leaf roller, Phyllody (phytoplasma)
• Castor: Semi-looper (Achaea janata), Capsule borer, Leaf miner
• Wheat: Rust (Brown/Yellow), Karnal bunt, Aphids
• Bajra: Downy mildew (Green ear), Ergot, Stem borer

10. KEY CHALLENGES
------------------
• Groundwater depletion in central talukas (Babra, Bagasara, Kunkavav)
• Coastal salinity ingress in Rajula and Jafrabad
• Erratic monsoon leading to frequent droughts or delayed sowing
• Declining soil fertility in intensively cultivated groundnut areas
• Labour shortage during peak groundnut harvest (October-November)
• Limited cold storage facilities for horticultural produce
• Groundnut oil mill pollution affecting soil and water in some areas

11. GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
----------------------
• KVK: Krishi Vigyan Kendra, Amreli (affiliated with Junagadh Agricultural
  University) - provides training, seed production, and pest alerts
• ATMA: Active extension programmes in all talukas
• Soil Testing Labs: Mobile soil testing vans operate seasonally
• APMCs: Amreli (main), Rajula, Savarkundla, Bagasara, Lathi
• Check Dam Schemes: Government has built hundreds of check dams under
  Sujalam Sufalam and Jaldhara programmes
• Groundwater recharge schemes promoted by Gujarat Water Supply and
  Sewerage Board (GWSSB)

12. FARMER ADVISORIES
---------------------
• Groundnut farmers should rotate with cotton or bajra every 2-3 years to
  reduce soil-borne disease buildup and nematode pressure
• Adopt summer ploughing (May) to expose soil-borne pests to solar heat
• Use seed treatment with thiram + carbendazim for groundnut to prevent
  seed rot and early blight
• In water-scarce talukas, switch to low-water crops like cumin, castor,
  or sesame instead of water-intensive cotton
• For cotton, install pheromone traps from day 30 for pink bollworm
  monitoring; spray only at economic threshold levels
• Grow fodder crops (hybrid Napier, Lucerne) on 10-15% of land to support
  dairy; sell milk through Amul or Sabar dairy cooperatives
• Apply gypsum @ 500 kg/ha in groundnut at flowering stage to meet
  calcium requirement for pod development and prevent pod rot
• Use drip irrigation for cotton and castor to improve water use efficiency
  by 40-50%
