Kutch district major pest problems: Kutch has unique agroecological conditions — arid, saline, high wind, seasonal crops dependent on erratic monsoon. Major crops: cotton, bajra, castor, dates, cumin, fennel, jeera, and livestock fodder crops. Cotton: pink bollworm (Pectinophora gossypiella) is primary pest — Kutch cotton often planted in sandy soils with lower natural enemy pressure; strict pheromone monitoring from day 30; spray emamectin benzoate 0.4g/litre at threshold. Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci) is particularly severe in Kutch's dry, hot conditions — transmits CLCuV (Cotton Leaf Curl Virus); spray imidacloprid 0.3ml/litre at seedling stage, monitor with yellow sticky traps. Mites (Tetranychus urticae) in dry September — spray abamectin 1ml/litre. Bajra: migratory locust (Schistocerca gregaria) historically has affected Kutch in outbreak years — coordinate with NAPPO (National Authority for Pest Prevention and Control); district agriculture department issues locust alerts for Kutch and Banaskantha. Ergot (Claviceps fusiformis) on bajra is severe in Kutch varieties — seed treatment with thiram 2g/kg prevents seedling blight; scout for honeydew secretion from florets indicating ergot. Cumin and fennel (Rabi): stem gall (Protomyces macrosporus) caused by Protomyces — internal stem galling visible on cross-section; no effective cure; use disease-free seeds and crop rotation. Dates (Khajoor): Oryctes rhinoceros beetle bores into palm crown — use pheromone trap + naphthalene balls in crown. KVK Bhuj at 02832-250296 provides Kutch-specific alerts.
