================================================================================
FARMER SAHAYAK - BANASKANTHA PEST PROFILE
================================================================================

Banaskantha is North Gujarat's agricultural heartland and the state's premier
potato and spice-producing district. The unique combination of arid climate,
light sandy soils, and intensive irrigation creates a distinct pest and disease
profile that differs from other Gujarat regions. This profile details the major
pests, diseases, and their management for the district's primary crops.

1. POTATO PESTS & DISEASES
---------------------------
Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans):
This is the most destructive disease of potato in Banaskantha. The humid
November-December conditions, combined with irrigation mist, create ideal
conditions for late blight epidemics. The disease appears as water-soaked
dark lesions on leaves and stems, spreading rapidly under cool, humid conditions.
• Scout fields weekly from November 15 onwards
• At first sign of disease, spray metalaxyl-mancozeb 2.5g/litre or
  cymoxanil-mancozeb 2.5g/litre
• Ensure adequate plant spacing for air circulation
• Avoid overhead irrigation during late afternoon

Aphids (Myzus persicae, Aphis gossypii):
Aphids are serious vectors of Potato Virus Y (PVY) and Potato Leaf Roll Virus
(PLRV), which cause seed degeneration and yield loss.
• Spray imidacloprid 0.3ml/litre at first appearance
• Use yellow sticky traps (20 per hectare) for monitoring
• Remove and destroy volunteer potato plants that harbour aphids

Cutworm (Agrotis spp.):
Cutworms damage emerging potato shoots by cutting stems at soil level.
• Apply chlorpyriphos 2ml/litre in the evening near plant base
• Place bran bait (chlorpyriphos + jaggery + bran) in the evening

Tuber Moth (Phthorimaea operculella):
A major storage and field pest that bores into tubers.
• Spray neem oil 3ml/litre or spinosad 0.3ml/litre
• Ensure proper storage with good ventilation and use pheromone traps

2. CUMIN PESTS & DISEASES
-------------------------
Cumin Blight (Alternaria burnsii):
Endemic in Banaskantha, this fungal disease causes leaf spots, stem blight,
and seed discolouration. Spreads rapidly in humid, cloudy conditions.
• Spray carbendazim 0.5g/litre + mancozeb 2.5g/litre combination
• Apply first spray at 30 days after sowing, repeat every 15 days
• Use disease-free certified seed from KVK Dantiwada

Aphids on Cumin:
Aphids attack cumin umbels during flowering (January-February), causing major
seed set failure through direct feeding and honeydew secretion promoting sooty mould.
• Spray dimethoate 2ml/litre or acetamiprid 0.3g/litre
• Apply during morning or evening to avoid flowering disruption
• Monitor with yellow sticky traps from December onwards

Powdery Mildew (Erysiphe polygoni):
Appears as white powdery patches on leaves and stems, reducing photosynthesis.
• Spray wettable sulphur 3g/litre or dinocap 2ml/litre
• Ensure adequate plant spacing for air circulation

Wilt (Fusarium oxysporum):
Soil-borne disease causing yellowing and wilting of plants.
• Practice crop rotation with cereals or legumes
• Treat seeds with carbendazim + thiram (2g/kg seed)
• Apply Trichoderma viride @ 5kg/ha in soil at sowing

3. FENNEL PESTS & DISEASES
--------------------------
Alternaria Blight:
Similar to cumin blight, causes leaf spots and blight on fennel.
• Spray mancozeb 2.5g/litre + carbendazim 0.5g/litre
• Remove and destroy infected plant debris after harvest

Aphids and Thrips:
Feed on tender leaves and umbels, reducing seed quality and yield.
• Spray imidacloprid 0.3ml/litre or thiamethoxam 0.3g/litre
• Apply neem oil 3ml/litre as preventive spray

Powdery Mildew:
• Spray wettable sulphur 3g/litre or dinocap 2ml/litre at first appearance

4. BAJRA PESTS & DISEASES
-------------------------
Downy Mildew (Sclerospora graminicola) / "Green Ear":
A major Kharif problem in Banaskantha. Infected plants show chlorotic streaks
on leaves and green leafy structures replacing the grain ("green ear").
• Seed treatment with metalaxyl 6g/kg seed is mandatory
• Grow resistant varieties: GHB-558, GHB-732
• Avoid susceptible local varieties in high-risk areas
• Spray metalaxyl-mancozeb 2.5g/litre if symptoms appear

Ergot (Claviceps fusiformis):
Replaces grains with black sclerotia (honeydew stage visible first).
• Treat seeds with thiram 2g/kg to prevent seedling infection
• Remove and burn infected heads before sclerotia formation
• Practice crop rotation with non-cereal crops

5. CASTOR PESTS & DISEASES
--------------------------
Semi-looper Caterpillar (Achaea janata):
Causes mass defoliation in August-September. Larvae skeletonize leaves and
can strip plants completely in outbreak years.
• Spray chlorpyrifos 2ml/litre or emamectin benzoate 0.4g/litre
• Monitor from 45 days after sowing; spray at early larval stage
• Collect and destroy egg masses manually

Capsule Borer (Conogethes punctiferalis):
Bores into castor capsules, causing seed damage and shattering.
• Spray chlorpyrifos 2ml/litre or spinosad 0.3ml/litre
• Install pheromone traps (5 per hectare) for monitoring

6. COTTON PESTS
---------------
Pink Bollworm (Pectinophora gossypiella):
Pressure is lower than Saurashtra but still significant in northern talukas.
• Install pheromone traps from day 30 after sowing (8 traps per hectare)
• Economic threshold: 8 moths per trap per week triggers spray
• Spray emamectin benzoate 0.4g/litre or chlorantraniliprole 0.4ml/litre

Aphid Colonies:
Can be severe in October on cotton, especially in water-stressed fields.
• Spray imidacloprid 0.3ml/litre at first appearance
• Maintain adequate irrigation to reduce plant stress

7. GROUNDNUT PESTS
------------------
Thrips (Thrips palmi, Scirtothrips dorsalis):
Transmit Peanut Bud Necrosis Virus (PBNV/TSWV), causing severe yield loss.
• Early season (June-July) thrips control is critical
• Spray fipronil 1ml/litre at seedling stage (7-10 days after emergence)
• Monitor with yellow sticky traps from germination

Leaf Spot (Tikka Disease - Cercospora arachidicola + Phaeoisariopsis personata):
• Spray mancozeb 2.5g/litre or chlorothalonil 2g/litre starting at 30 days
  after sowing, repeat at 10-15 day intervals

8. MUSTARD PESTS & DISEASES
----------------------------
Aphids (Lipaphis erysimi):
Major pest causing honeydew secretion and virus transmission.
• Spray dimethoate 2ml/litre or imidacloprid 0.3ml/litre
• Release ladybird beetles (Coccinella septempunctata) for biological control

White Rust (Albugo candida):
Appears as white pustules on leaves, reducing yield and oil content.
• Spray mancozeb 2.5g/litre at first appearance
• Grow resistant varieties: GJM-2, GJM-3

Alternaria Blight:
• Spray carbendazim + mancozeb combination at first sign

9. DISTRICT-SPECIFIC ADVISORIES
-------------------------------
• KVK Dantiwada (Krishi Vigyan Kendra) issues weekly pest alerts from October
  to April for potato and spice crops. Contact: 02748-278219
• The arid climate of Banaskantha means pest pressure is generally lower than
  humid zones, but sprinkler and drip irrigation can create micro-humidity that
  favours fungal diseases - monitor fields closely after irrigation
• Apply preventive sprays for potato late blight from mid-November regardless
  of symptoms, as the disease can explode within 48 hours under favourable conditions
• Use disease-free certified seed for potato and spices to prevent introducing
  pathogens into clean fields
• Practice strict sanitation: remove and destroy crop residues after harvest to
  reduce overwintering inoculum for fungal diseases
