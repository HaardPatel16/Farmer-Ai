FARMER SAHAYAK - AHMEDABAD DISTRICT AGRO-CLIMATIC PROFILE
================================================================================

1. DISTRICT OVERVIEW
--------------------
Ahmedabad is the largest and most populous district in Gujarat, located in the
north-central part of the state. The district covers approximately 8,100 sq km
with a population of over 7 million. It serves as the economic and industrial
hub of Gujarat, with the city of Ahmedabad being the state's former capital.
Agriculturally, the district is significant for its peri-urban farming belt
that supplies fresh produce to one of India's largest urban markets. The
district has 11 talukas: Ahmedabad City, Bavla, Daskroi, Detroj-Rampura,
Dhandhuka, Dholka, Mandal, Sanand, Viramgam, Barwala, and Dholera.

2. AGRO-CLIMATIC ZONE
---------------------
Ahmedabad falls in the Middle Gujarat Agro-Climatic Zone. The climate is
semi-arid to sub-humid tropical. The district receives moderate annual rainfall
of 700-800 mm, with the southwest monsoon arriving in late June and retreating
by mid-September. About 80% of rainfall occurs during July-August. The mean
maximum temperature ranges from 28°C in January to 42°C in May, while minimum
temperatures range from 10°C in winter to 28°C in summer. Relative humidity
varies from 25% in summer (March-May) to 80% during the monsoon.

3. SOIL TYPES
-------------
The dominant soil types are:
• Medium black soil (Vertisols) in central and eastern talukas
• Sandy loam (Goradu) in western talukas like Dholka and Dhandhuka
• Alluvial soils along the Sabarmati river belt
• Saline-alkali patches in low-lying areas near the Gulf of Khambhat

Soil texture ranges from clayey to loamy. Soil pH is generally neutral to
slightly alkaline (7.2-8.5). Fertility status is moderate to high in
irrigated areas but declining in rainfed tracts due to continuous cotton
and cereal cropping. Some areas show zinc and sulphur deficiency.

4. WATER RESOURCES
------------------
Annual rainfall: 700-800 mm (moderately reliable)
Irrigation sources:
• Narmada main canal and branch canals serve Sanand, Bavla, Dholka talukas
• Sabarmati river system provides limited surface water
• Tubewell irrigation is extensive; borewell depths range 80-200 metres
• Peri-urban areas have good groundwater recharge from the Sabarmati basin

Water issues: The Dhandhuka and Viramgam belts face groundwater depletion.
The Little Rann border areas have saline groundwater. The Narmada canal
has improved irrigation security in the southern talukas significantly.

5. MAJOR CROPS
--------------
KHARIF (June-October):
• Cotton (Gossypium hirsutum) - 80,000+ ha, varieties: G Cot-13, G Cot-21
• Bajra (Pearl millet) - 60,000 ha, hybrids: GHB-558, GHB-732
• Paddy (Rice) - 30,000 ha in irrigated pockets, varieties: GR-11, GR-12
• Castor - 25,000 ha, hybrid: GCH-7, GCH-9
• Groundnut - 40,000 ha, varieties: GG-20, GG-22

RABI (November-March):
• Wheat - 90,000 ha, varieties: GW-366, GW-496, Lok-1
• Mustard - 15,000 ha
• Gram (Chickpea) - limited area
• Cumin - 10,000 ha in irrigated pockets

SUMMER (March-May):
• Vegetables - 25,000 ha (peri-urban)
• Fodder crops (Jowar, Maize) - dairy-linked

Productivity: Wheat yields 4.5-5.5 tonnes/ha under irrigation; cotton
yields 2.5-3.5 quintals/ha lint; groundnut 15-20 quintals/ha pods.

6. HORTICULTURE
---------------
Peri-urban horticulture is a major economic driver:
• Vegetables: Tomato, brinjal, okra, chilli, onion, garlic, potato
• Fruits: Mango (Kesar, Alphonso), banana, papaya, guava
• Flowers: Rose, marigold, chrysanthemum near urban markets
• The Sanand-Bavla belt has large-scale floriculture and vegetable farms
supplying the Ahmedabad city market and exports via the airport.

7. LIVESTOCK
------------
Ahmedabad has a significant dairy sector driven by the peri-urban demand:
• Cattle: Indigenous (Gir) and crossbred populations
• Buffalo: Mehsani and Surti breeds, important for milk production
• Poultry: Commercial broiler farms in Sanand and Dholka talukas
• Fodder availability: Good in irrigated tracts; peri-urban farmers grow
  hybrid Napier, Jowar, and Lucerne (alfalfa) for dairy feed.
• The district has numerous dairy cooperatives and private milk processors.

8. CROP CALENDAR
----------------
Month          | Major Activities
---------------|-----------------------------------------------
June           | Cotton sowing, Bajra sowing, Paddy nursery
July           | Cotton/bajra sowing continues, Castor sowing
August         | Groundnut sowing, Paddy transplanting

October        | Early Bajra harvest, Cotton pickings begin
November       | Cotton picking, Rabi wheat sowing begins
December       | Wheat sowing, Mustard/Cumin sowing
January        | Wheat irrigation, Mustard/Cumin maintenance
February       | Potato harvest, Cumin harvest begins
March          | Wheat harvest (mid-late), Rabi gram harvest
April          | Summer vegetable sowing, Fodder sowing
May            | Summer vegetables, Wheat threshing

9. COMMON PESTS & DISEASES
--------------------------
• Cotton: Pink bollworm (Pectinophora gossypiella), Aphids, Whitefly
  (transmits CLCuV), Spodoptera (Tobacco caterpillar)
• Groundnut: Leaf spot (Tikka disease), Tobacco caterpillar, Jassids
• Wheat: Rust (Brown/Yellow), Karnal bunt, Aphids
• Castor: Semi-looper (Achaea janata), Capsule borer
• Vegetables: Fruit borer, Whitefly, Aphids, Downy mildew

10. KEY CHALLENGES
------------------
• Urbanization pressure: Farm land conversion to industrial/residential use
• Groundwater depletion in Viramgam and Dhandhuka talukas
• Soil salinity near the Gulf of Khambhat border areas
• Labour shortage during peak sowing/harvesting seasons
• Industrial pollution affecting peri-urban agriculture near Ahmedabad city
• Declining soil organic matter in intensive vegetable-growing areas

11. GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
----------------------
• KVK (Krishi Vigyan Kendra): KVK Ahmedabad, located near Gujarat
  Agricultural University coordination
• ATMA (Agricultural Technology Management Agency): Active in all talukas
• Soil Testing Labs: District-level soil testing laboratory at Ahmedabad
• APMCs: Ahmedabad APMC (main), Dholka, Sanand, Viramgam sub-yards
• Cold Storages: Multiple cold storage units in Sanand and Ahmedabad city
  for potatoes and vegetables
• Narmada Canal Command: Provides reliable irrigation in southern talukas

12. FARMER ADVISORIES
---------------------
• Peri-urban farmers should focus on high-value vegetables and flowers for
  better returns; direct marketing to city markets or through APMC e-trading
• Adopt drip irrigation for cotton and vegetables to save water and increase
  yields by 20-30%
• In saline-alkali patches, apply gypsum @ 2 tonnes/ha and grow salt-tolerant
  crops like barley or sorghum initially
• Use summer ploughing and deep tillage to break hard pans in black soils
• Rotate cotton with wheat or chickpea to reduce soil-borne diseases and
  improve soil health
• Maintain soil test-based fertilization; address zinc deficiency with
  ZnSO4 @ 25 kg/ha in zinc-deficient soils
• For dairy farmers, grow Azolla as a supplementary cattle feed to reduce
  feed costs by 15-20%
