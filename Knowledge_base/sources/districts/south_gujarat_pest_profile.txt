South Gujarat (Surat, Navsari, Valsad, Tapi) major pest problems: South Gujarat has highest rainfall (1500-2000mm), humid conditions, and major horticultural crops — sugarcane, banana, sapota, mango, paddy, turmeric, ginger, and vegetables. Sugarcane: early shoot borer (Chilo infuscatellus) causes "dead heart" in young crop — spray chlorpyrifos 2.5ml/litre + sand (granular) in whorl of young plants. Top borer (Scirpophaga excerptalis) causes "dead top" — install Trichogramma cards (egg parasitoid) 50,000 per hectare at 30-day intervals from July. Pyrilla (sugarcane leafhopper) causes sooty mould — spray dimethoate. Paddy: leaf folder (Cnaphalocrocis medinalis) very common in South Gujarat rice — spray monocrotophos 1.5ml/litre at 25% leaf folding; gall midge causes "silver shoot" at tillering stage — spray carbofuran granules 1kg AI/ha in flood-water. Brown plant hopper (BPH) causes "hopper burn" — spray buprofezin 1.5ml/litre from base of plant. Banana: banana weevil (Cosmopolites sordidus) bores into corm — use pseudostem traps soaked in chlorpyrifos solution, place one trap per 25 plants and inspect weekly. Sigatoka leaf spot (Mycosphaerella musicola) on banana — spray mancozeb 2.5g/litre monthly. Ginger and turmeric: shoot borer (Conogethes punctiferalis) — spray chlorpyrifos 2.5ml/litre at plant base monthly from July.
