================================================================================
GUJARAT FARMING CALENDAR — JUNE (વૈશાખ / જેઠ (Vaishakh / Jeth))
================================================================================

OVERVIEW:
June is a critical month for Gujarat agriculture. The state's diverse agro-climatic zones — Saurashtra, North Gujarat, South Gujarat, and Kutch — each have specific crop activities during this period. This calendar provides detailed, week-by-week guidance for farmers.

CROPS IN FOCUS THIS MONTH:
  • Cotton (sowing)
  • Groundnut (sowing)
  • Bajra (sowing)
  • Paddy (nursery)
  • Monsoon onset

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION
================================================================================
  • Summer ploughing should be completed by now if not already done; deep ploughing (20-25 cm) improves water infiltration
  • For heavy black cotton soils, apply organic matter / FYM (Farm Yard Manure) 10-15 tonnes per hectare at least 2-3 weeks before sowing
  • Level the field using a leveller to ensure uniform irrigation and prevent waterlogging
  • In Saurashtra, apply gypsum 250 kg per hectare for sodic soil reclamation before sowing
  • Conduct soil testing: pH should be 6.5-7.5 for most crops; apply lime if pH < 6.0
  • Prepare raised beds (15-20 cm high) for vegetable crops to improve drainage during monsoon

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Cotton: sow with onset of monsoon in Saurashtra, North Gujarat, and South Gujarat — seed rate 1.5-2 kg/ha for Bt hybrids
  • Groundnut: sow after first good monsoon rain (50-75mm) in Saurashtra, Kutch, and North Gujarat; seed rate 100-125 kg/ha
  • Bajra: sow after first monsoon shower in Saurashtra and North Gujarat; seed rate 3-4 kg/ha
  • Paddy: nursery sowing in South Gujarat (Valsad, Navsari, Surat) and parts of North Gujarat; seed rate 30-40 kg/ha for nursery
  • Castor: sow in Saurashtra and North Gujarat rainfed tracts; seed rate 5-6 kg/ha for hybrids
  • Sesame: last sowing window in first week of June with monsoon rains in Saurashtra

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Cotton: first irrigation immediately after sowing if no rain within 3 days; maintain soil moisture for germination
  • Groundnut: if no rain after sowing, provide light irrigation (25mm) to ensure uniform germination
  • Bajra: generally rainfed; one protective irrigation if dry spell exceeds 15 days after sowing
  • Paddy nursery: maintain 2-3 cm water layer daily; drain and re-irrigate if water becomes stagnant
  • All Kharif crops: ensure drainage channels are open — June storms can cause waterlogging

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Cotton: apply basal dose — DAP 100 kg + potash 30 kg per hectare at sowing time
  • Groundnut: apply basal dose — DAP 200 kg + gypsum 250 kg per hectare; apply gypsum at flowering (not now)
  • Bajra: apply basal dose — DAP 40 kg + urea 20 kg per hectare at sowing
  • Paddy: apply basal dose in nursery — DAP 2 kg per 100 sq metres; main field dose applied at transplanting
  • Castor: apply basal dose — DAP 100 kg + potash 30 kg per hectare at sowing

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Cotton: monitor for jassids, aphids, thrips — spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml per litre at 20-30 days if needed
  • Groundnut: white grub risk — treat soil with Chlorpyriphos 2ml per litre drench if previous crop was pasture or fallow
  • Groundnut: leaf miner — monitor from 30 days; spray if damage exceeds 5% leaf area
  • Bajra: stem borer — monitor for dead heart at 20-30 days; apply Carbofuran 3G 20 kg/ha if >5% infection
  • Paddy: blast (Pyricularia oryzae) in nursery — spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre if spots appear
  • Castor: castor shoot and capsule borer — monitor for webbed shoots; remove and destroy infested shoots

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • Summer groundnut: harvest remaining late-sown crop; complete by first week of June
  • Summer bajra: harvest remaining crop; dry and store before monsoon humidity increases
  • Mango: harvest of late varieties (Neelam, Totapuri) continues; post-harvest handling critical in humid weather
  • Summer vegetables: harvest and market before monsoon rains reduce quality
  • Pre-monsoon sesame: harvest if mature; do not delay — rain can cause quality loss

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • Clean and dry harvested produce immediately; moisture content should be reduced to safe levels (8-10% for grains, 10-12% for oilseeds)
  • Use tarpaulins or cemented drying yards to avoid aflatoxin contamination in groundnut and cotton
  • Store in properly ventilated godowns; use neem leaves or safe storage pesticides
  • For cotton, ginning should be done within 15 days of picking to preserve fibre quality
  • Grade produce before selling: separate A, B, and C grades for better market prices
  • Maintain post-harvest records for traceability and insurance claims

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Southwest monsoon onset in Gujarat: typically arrives in second week of June (around 10-15 June) in South Gujarat
  • Monsoon progresses northward; reaches Saurashtra by third week; North Gujarat by last week of June
  • Pre-monsoon thunderstorms bring heavy but patchy rain; not sufficient for sowing alone
  • Day temperatures: 35-40°C before monsoon; drop to 30-35°C after monsoon onset
  • Cyclone risk in Arabian Sea — monitor IMD warnings for coastal districts (Valsad, Navsari, Surat, Bhavnagar, Amreli)

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PM-KISAN installment verification: ensure Aadhaar seeding is complete before 15th of the month
  • Soil Health Card scheme: collect updated cards from local Krushi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or ATMA office
  • PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana) enrollment: last date for Kharif crops is usually 31st July; for Rabi crops, 15th December
  • Gujarat State Farm Machinery Scheme: subsidy applications for tractor/power tiller due at district agriculture office
  • Drip irrigation subsidy under GGRC (Gujarat Green Revolution Company): submit estimates before month-end
  • Horticulture Mission for North East & Himalayan States (HMNEH) — nursery subsidy claims
  • Contact your nearest VLE (Village Level Entrepreneur) for eNAM registration and online mandi selling
