MONSOON ONSET ADVISORY FOR GUJARAT FARMERS
===========================================

OVERVIEW
The Southwest Monsoon is the lifeline of Gujarat agriculture. Its onset timing, distribution, 
and withdrawal determine the success of Kharif crops like groundnut, cotton, castor, and 
pearl millet. Understanding monsoon patterns helps farmers plan sowing and input use.

NORMAL MONSOON ONSET DATES IN GUJARAT
- South Gujarat (Valsad, Surat, Navsari): June 10-15
- Middle Gujarat (Vadodara, Anand, Kheda): June 15-20
- North Gujarat (Ahmedabad, Gandhinagar, Mehsana): June 20-25
- Saurashtra (Rajkot, Bhavnagar, Junagadh): June 25-30
- Kutch (Bhuj): July 1-10
- Gujarat overall average: ~June 20

Note: Onset dates have become increasingly variable (±2-3 weeks) due to climate change.

SIGNS OF APPROACHING MONSOON
- Sustained southwesterly winds increase over Arabian Sea
- Overcast skies with cloud cover from south
- Temperatures drop by 3-5°C from the pre-monsoon peak
- Sudden increase in humidity (>70%)
- IMD declares onset officially for Kerala around June 1, Gujarat follows

PRE-MONSOON PREPARATION CHECKLIST

Land Preparation
- Complete deep ploughing (before last week of May)
- Prepare field bunds to retain rainwater
- Desilt farm ponds before monsoon
- Check and repair irrigation channels and bunds

Input Procurement
- Buy certified seeds early (avoid last-minute shortage)
- Procure fertilisers (DAP, urea) and store in dry place
- Arrange pesticides for early pest management
- Ensure sprayer is serviced and functional

Soil Testing
- Complete soil testing before sowing
- Get soil health card recommendations from local agriculture office
- Adjust lime/gypsum application based on pH results

SOWING GUIDELINES BASED ON MONSOON TIMING

If Monsoon Arrives Normal (on time):
- Start sowing groundnut within 3-5 days of receiving 75-100 mm rain
- Sow cotton after 2-3 good rains (50-75 mm total)
- Sow pearl millet (bajra) with first rain (shallow rooted; quick to establish)

If Monsoon is Delayed (>15 days late):
- Do not dry sow cotton or groundnut hoping for rain
- Use early-maturing varieties if sowing is delayed beyond July 15
- For very late monsoon (after July 20 in North Gujarat), switch to shorter duration crops
- Consider pearl millet (GHB-719, 75 days) instead of groundnut

If Monsoon is Early or Very Heavy at Onset:
- Ensure drainage channels are open before sowing
- Do not sow in waterlogged conditions (use raised beds)
- Delay sowing until excess water drains (2-3 days after rain stops)

CRITICAL SOWING WINDOWS FOR GUJARAT KHARIF CROPS
- Groundnut (Saurashtra): June 25 – July 15 (optimal)
- Cotton (Saurashtra/N. Gujarat): June 15 – July 15
- Castor (N. Gujarat): June 25 – July 20
- Pearl Millet: June 15 – July 31
- Sesame (Til): June 25 – July 15
- Soybean (S. Gujarat): June 20 – July 15
- Pigeon Pea (Tur): June 15 – July 15

SOIL MOISTURE CHECK BEFORE SOWING
- Take a handful of soil from 8-10 cm depth
- Press firmly; if it forms a ball and cracks slightly = ideal for sowing
- If soil is too dry (crumbles): wait for more rain
- If soil is too wet (sticks, no cracks): wait for drainage
