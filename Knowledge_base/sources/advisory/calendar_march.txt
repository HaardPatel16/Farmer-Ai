================================================================================
GUJARAT FARMING CALENDAR — MARCH (મહા / ફાગણ (Maha / Phagun))
================================================================================

OVERVIEW:
March is a critical month for Gujarat agriculture. The state's diverse agro-climatic zones — Saurashtra, North Gujarat, South Gujarat, and Kutch — each have specific crop activities during this period. This calendar provides detailed, week-by-week guidance for farmers.

CROPS IN FOCUS THIS MONTH:
  • Wheat (harvest)
  • Gram (harvest)
  • Mustard (harvest)
  • Cumin (harvest)
  • Summer groundnut sowing
  • Bajra (summer)

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION
================================================================================
  • Summer ploughing should be completed by now if not already done; deep ploughing (20-25 cm) improves water infiltration
  • For heavy black cotton soils, apply organic matter / FYM (Farm Yard Manure) 10-15 tonnes per hectare at least 2-3 weeks before sowing
  • Level the field using a leveller to ensure uniform irrigation and prevent waterlogging
  • In Saurashtra, apply gypsum 250 kg per hectare for sodic soil reclamation before sowing
  • Conduct soil testing: pH should be 6.5-7.5 for most crops; apply lime if pH < 6.0
  • Prepare raised beds (15-20 cm high) for vegetable crops to improve drainage during monsoon

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Summer groundnut sowing begins in Saurashtra and South Gujarat — first fortnight of March is ideal
  • Summer bajra sowing in Saurashtra and Kutch with assured irrigation; seed rate 3-4 kg per hectare
  • Moong (green gram) summer sowing continues until mid-March in South Gujarat and Saurashtra
  • Sesame (til) summer sowing in North Gujarat and Saurashtra — requires warm temperatures and light soil
  • Cucumber, ridge gourd, and bitter gourd summer vegetable sowing in South Gujarat

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Wheat: stop all irrigation 10-15 days before harvest; allow field to dry for easy combine operation
  • Gram: no irrigation after pods turn yellow; dry spell before harvest helps uniform maturity and threshing
  • Summer groundnut: first irrigation immediately after sowing; second at 7 days; then every 7-10 days
  • Summer bajra: first irrigation at sowing; second at 15 days; then every 12-15 days depending on soil
  • Summer vegetables: frequent light irrigation (every 5-7 days) to maintain soil moisture; avoid waterlogging

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Summer groundnut: apply basal dose — 20 kg DAP + 20 kg potash per hectare at sowing; gypsum 250 kg at flowering
  • Summer bajra: apply 40 kg DAP + 20 kg urea as basal; first top dressing 40 kg urea at 25-30 days
  • Summer moong: apply 20 kg DAP as basal; no nitrogen — moong fixes its own nitrogen
  • Gram: no fertilizer application at harvest stage; basal dose should have been sufficient
  • Mustard: no fertilizer after flowering; focus on harvest and post-harvest management

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Wheat: monitor for black rust (Puccinia graminis) at late stage; spray if needed before harvest
  • Wheat: termite damage in standing crop — check field margins; apply Chlorpyriphos 2ml per litre if found
  • Gram: pulse beetle in stored grain risk — begin harvest when moisture is low; dry to 8% before storage
  • Summer groundnut: white grub risk — treat soil with Chlorpyriphos 2ml per litre drench if previous crop was pasture
  • Summer bajra: stem borer — monitor for dead heart at 20-30 days; apply Carbofuran 3G 20 kg/ha if >5% infection
  • Mango: powdery mildew and anthracnose — spray Sulphur 2g per litre or Carbendazim 1g per litre at flowering

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • Wheat: main harvest begins in Saurashtra (early varieties) and extends to North Gujarat by month-end
  • Wheat: combine harvest when grain moisture is 18-20%; dry to 12% before storage
  • Gram: harvest when 80% pods turn brown; use sickle or combine; thresh and clean same day
  • Mustard: harvest when 90% pods are yellow; cut and stack; thresh after 5-7 days of field drying
  • Cumin: harvest when seeds turn brown; uproot whole plant, dry in shade for 3-4 days, then thresh
  • Potato: harvest continues from February sowing; complete by month-end to avoid heat damage

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • Clean and dry harvested produce immediately; moisture content should be reduced to safe levels (8-10% for grains, 10-12% for oilseeds)
  • Use tarpaulins or cemented drying yards to avoid aflatoxin contamination in groundnut and cotton
  • Store in properly ventilated godowns; use neem leaves or safe storage pesticides
  • For cotton, ginning should be done within 15 days of picking to preserve fibre quality
  • Grade produce before selling: separate A, B, and C grades for better market prices
  • Maintain post-harvest records for traceability and insurance claims

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Day temperatures rise sharply to 35-40°C; nights 18-22°C; summer begins in Gujarat
  • Hot dry winds (loo) may begin in second fortnight — can cause wheat shrivelling; harvest early if predicted
  • Unseasonal rain in March is rare but can damage mature wheat; keep harvest equipment ready
  • Humidity drops significantly — good for harvest but increases irrigation demand for summer crops
  • IMD Heat Wave warnings: if day temperature forecast exceeds 40°C for 3+ days, activate heat protection measures

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PM-KISAN installment verification: ensure Aadhaar seeding is complete before 15th of the month
  • Soil Health Card scheme: collect updated cards from local Krushi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or ATMA office
  • PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana) enrollment: last date for Kharif crops is usually 31st July; for Rabi crops, 15th December
  • Gujarat State Farm Machinery Scheme: subsidy applications for tractor/power tiller due at district agriculture office
  • Drip irrigation subsidy under GGRC (Gujarat Green Revolution Company): submit estimates before month-end
  • Horticulture Mission for North East & Himalayan States (HMNEH) — nursery subsidy claims
  • Contact your nearest VLE (Village Level Entrepreneur) for eNAM registration and online mandi selling
