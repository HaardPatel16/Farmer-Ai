Post-harvest management and storage guide for vegetable crops Gujarat: Vegetables are highly perishable — post-harvest losses of 25-40% are common in Gujarat due to poor handling and lack of cold chain. Onion storage: most important storage vegetable in Gujarat. After field curing (15 days), store in well-ventilated bamboo or wooden storage structures called "palla" or "chaura"; spread in 2-3 layers only (not more, as heat build-up causes rot); allow airflow from all sides; avoid direct sunlight and rain; ideal storage temperature 20-25°C, humidity below 65%. Losses from neck rot (Botrytis), soft rot (bacterial), and fusarium basal rot are reduced by proper curing before storage. Storage life: well-cured onion stores 3-4 months at ambient conditions in Gujarat. Cold storage: onion cold storage available at Bhavnagar, Junagadh, Rajkot, Ahmedabad — extends life to 6-8 months. Garlic: store in hung bundles in well-ventilated shed; ambient storage 4-6 months if properly cured. Potato: most important cold storage crop in Gujarat. Commercial cold storage widely available in Banaskantha; charge Rs 200-300 per quintal for 4-6 months storage; stored at 2-4°C, 90-95% humidity. Potato straight from farm sells at Rs 5-8/kg at harvest; stored potato sells at Rs 12-20/kg in summer — profit after cold storage cost is substantial. Tomato, chilli, capsicum: no affordable rural cold storage exists for most Gujarat farmers; focus on rapid marketing. Ethylene management: store banana, mango, and tomato away from each other — ethylene from one crop accelerates ripening of others. Evaporative cooler (zerocooler/matka cooler): low-cost structure made of bamboo, sand, and burlap bags that uses evaporative cooling — keeps vegetables 10-15°C below ambient temperature; suitable for small quantities; MANAGE (National Institute of Agricultural Extension Management) has design plans.
