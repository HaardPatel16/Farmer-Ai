================================================================================
MANGO CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — FEBRUARY
================================================================================

CROP: Mango
MONTH: February
PRIMARY REGION: South Gujarat, Saurashtra
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR FEBRUARY
================================================================================
  • Orchard soil management: apply organic manure (FYM 25-50 kg per tree) in basin around tree trunk
  • Mulch the basin with dry grass or straw to conserve soil moisture and regulate temperature
  • In heavy soils, ensure drainage to prevent root rot during pre-monsoon showers
  • Soil pH should be 5.5-7.5; apply lime if pH < 5.5 (common in South Gujarat laterite soils)
  • Conduct soil testing every 2-3 years to monitor nutrient status and pH

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • NO field sowing in February for mango — mango is a perennial orchard crop
  • If planning new orchard, February is ideal for planting grafted seedlings in South Gujarat
  • Dig pits 1m x 1m x 1m one month before planting; fill with FYM + soil + 100g DAP per pit
  • Plant grafted seedlings (e.g., Kesar, Dasheri, Langda) at the same depth as in nursery bag
  • For existing orchards, focus on flower bud initiation and fruit set management

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Pre-flowering irrigation: provide one deep irrigation (100mm) in first week of February to induce flowering
  • In South Gujarat, mango orchards are often irrigated with drip or micro-sprinklers
  • Critical irrigation at flower bud break and fruit set stages; do NOT allow moisture stress
  • Avoid over-irrigation — waterlogging causes root rot and flower drop
  • In Saurashtra, some farmers use basin irrigation for mango orchards

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Apply organic manure (FYM 25-50 kg per tree) + 500g DAP + 250g potash per tree in February
  • Foliar spray of 2% urea + 0.5% potassium sulphate at pre-flowering for better flower bud initiation
  • Micronutrients: foliar spray of 0.5% zinc sulphate + 0.2% boron at flower bud break for better fruit set
  • If tree is too vigorous, reduce nitrogen and increase potash to promote flowering
  • For alternate bearing trees, apply paclobutrazol (cultar) 10g per metre canopy diameter in February to induce flowering

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Mango hopper: spray Imidacloprid 0.3ml per litre before flower bud break; hoppers cause flower drop and sooty mould
  • Mealy bug: if white cottony masses appear on trunk and branches, spray buprofezin 1.5ml per litre + bind sticker
  • Powdery mildew: if white powdery patches appear on young leaves and flowers, spray Sulphur 2g per litre or Carbendazim 1g per litre
  • Anthracnose: if black spots appear on leaves and twigs, spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre
  • Fruit fly: set up methyl eugenol traps (10 per hectare) at fruit set stage to monitor and control
  • Scale insects: if scales appear on branches, spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml per litre or use horticultural mineral oil

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO harvest in February for most mango varieties — this is flowering and fruit set stage
  • Early varieties (some Dasheri) may have very early harvest in South Gujarat by month-end
  • Continue monitoring flower bud initiation, fruit set, and fruit drop
  • Record keeping: document flowering date for harvest planning (May-July)

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations in February for most mango orchards
  • Early harvested Dasheri: handle carefully to avoid bruising; pack in corrugated boxes with cushioning
  • Grade mangoes by size, colour, and maturity; export-quality mangoes require hot water treatment (52°C for 5 minutes)
  • Store harvested mangoes at 12-14°C for ripening; do NOT store below 10°C — causes chilling injury
  • Contact exporters or APMC for price information and procurement schedules

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Day temperatures rise to 28-35°C; nights 12-18°C; transition from winter to pre-summer
  • This temperature range is ideal for mango flower bud initiation in most varieties
  • Unseasonal rain in February can cause flower drop and fungal diseases (anthracnose, powdery mildew)
  • Dew formation increases — favours fungal diseases; plan preventive sprays accordingly
  • Wind speeds increase in Saurashtra — can cause flower and fruit drop; provide windbreaks if needed

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • Mango orchard development scheme: contact district horticulture office for subsidy on new plantation
  • Drip irrigation subsidy under GGRC: apply for orchard drip system if not already installed
  • Soil Health Card: collect updated card from KVK or ATMA office before fertilizer application
  • PM-KISAN: verify installment eligibility and Aadhaar seeding status
  • Horticulture Mission for North East & Himalayan States (HMNEH) — nursery subsidy claims
