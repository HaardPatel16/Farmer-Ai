PEST BULLETIN 2025 - GUJARAT AGRICULTURAL ADVISORY
====================================================

OVERVIEW
This bulletin summarises major pest and disease threats for Gujarat's key crops in 2025, 
based on State Department of Agriculture, KVK reports, and ICAR-NCIPM surveillance data. 
It provides identification tips, economic thresholds, and management recommendations.

KHARIF 2025 PEST ALERTS

1. GROUNDNUT — SPODOPTERA (FALL ARMYWORM & TOBACCO CATERPILLAR)
   Status: HIGH ALERT (Saurashtra, Junagadh, Amreli, Rajkot)
   Identification:
   - Young larvae skeletonise leaves; older larvae cause ragged holes
   - Egg masses with hair-like coating on underside of leaves
   - Active at night; hide in soil at base of plant during day
   Economic Threshold: 2-3 larvae per plant or 10% leaf damage
   Management:
   - Install pheromone traps (10/ha) — Spodoptera litura lure
   - Spray Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC @ 0.4 ml/litre (Coragen)
   - OR Emamectin Benzoate 5% SG @ 0.5 g/litre
   - Spray during evening hours for best effect

2. COTTON — PINK BOLLWORM (Pectinophora gossypiella)
   Status: MODERATE TO HIGH (Saurashtra cotton belt)
   Identification:
   - Larvae bore into green bolls leaving a circular hole
   - Infested bolls show internal feeding and staining
   - Rosette flowers (no petal opening) are a key sign
   Economic Threshold: 10% rosette flowers or 10% damaged bolls
   Management:
   - Use Bt cotton varieties with bollworm resistance
   - Install Pink Bollworm pheromone traps (5/acre)
   - Spray Profenofos 50% EC @ 2 ml/litre
   - OR Indoxacarb 14.5% SC @ 0.75 ml/litre
   - Avoid repeated use of same insecticide class

3. COTTON — WHITEFLY (Bemisia tabaci)
   Status: HIGH ALERT (June-August — vector of Cotton Leaf Curl Virus)
   Identification:
   - Tiny white insects on underside of leaves
   - Yellowing, curling, and stunted growth if CLCuV transmitted
   - Honeydew deposits lead to black sooty mould
   Economic Threshold: 6-8 nymphs per leaf (average of 10 leaves)
   Management:
   - Use yellow sticky traps (10/acre)
   - Spray Spiromesifen 22.9% SC @ 1 ml/litre
   - OR Flonicamid 50% WG @ 0.3 g/litre
   - Do NOT use Neonicotinoids (resistance widespread in Gujarat)

4. PEARL MILLET — DOWNY MILDEW (Sclerospora graminicola)
   Status: WATCH (North Gujarat, Banaskantha, Patan)
   Identification:
   - Greenish-yellow streaks on leaves
   - White downy growth on lower surface of leaves
   - Systemic infection causes stunted, bushy plants ("green ear")
   Management:
   - Use resistant varieties: GHB-719, GHB-744
   - Seed treatment with Metalaxyl 35% WS @ 6 g/kg seed
   - Rogue out infected plants immediately
   - Avoid excessive irrigation near crop base

5. CASTOR — CAPSULE BORER (Conogethes punctiferalis)
   Status: MODERATE (North Gujarat)
   Identification:
   - Larvae bore into developing capsules
   - Webbing and frass on capsule surface
   Economic Threshold: 5% capsule damage
   Management:
   - Spray Quinalphos 25% EC @ 2 ml/litre
   - OR Lambda Cyhalothrin 5% EC @ 1 ml/litre
   - Apply during early morning when larvae are surface feeding

DISEASE ALERTS — RABI 2025 (ANTICIPATED)

1. CUMIN — POWDERY MILDEW AND BLIGHT
   - Expected surge in North Gujarat if rains occur November-December
   - Preventive spray of Mancozeb 75% WP @ 2.5 g/litre at 30 days after sowing

2. WHEAT — YELLOW RUST (Puccinia striiformis)
   - Monitor from January in Banaskantha, Patan, and Mehsana
   - Apply Propiconazole 25% EC @ 1 ml/litre at first symptom

INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM) PRINCIPLES
- Scout fields weekly; do not spray on calendar basis
- Always check economic threshold before spraying
- Rotate insecticide groups to prevent resistance
- Preserve natural enemies (ladybirds, spiders, parasitic wasps)
- Use pheromone traps for monitoring before deciding on chemical control
