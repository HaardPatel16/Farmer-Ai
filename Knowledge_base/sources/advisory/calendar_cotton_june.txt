================================================================================
COTTON CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — JUNE
================================================================================

CROP: Cotton
MONTH: June
PRIMARY REGION: Saurashtra, North Gujarat, South Gujarat
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR JUNE
================================================================================
  • Deep summer ploughing (20-25 cm) should have been completed by May; if not, do immediately
  • Apply FYM 10-15 tonnes per hectare 2-3 weeks before sowing
  • Level the field with a leveller; cotton requires uniform moisture distribution
  • In heavy black cotton soils, open drainage channels at 15-20 metre intervals to prevent waterlogging
  • Soil pH should be 6.5-7.5; apply lime if pH < 6.0
  • Prepare ridges and furrows in heavy soils for better drainage during monsoon

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Sow cotton with the onset of monsoon rains (first good shower of 25-50mm)
  • Ideal sowing window: 10-25 June in Saurashtra; 15-30 June in North Gujarat; 15-25 June in South Gujarat
  • Seed rate: 1.5-2.0 kg per hectare for Bt hybrid varieties (e.g., Gujarat Cotton Hybrid-1, GCH-2)
  • Spacing: 90-120 cm between rows; 45-60 cm between plants (depending on variety)
  • Sow 2-3 seeds per hill; thin to one plant per hill at 15-20 days after germination
  • Treat seeds with Carbendazim 2g + Streptocycline 1g per kg to prevent seedling diseases

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • If no rain within 3 days of sowing, provide light irrigation (25mm) to ensure uniform germination
  • First irrigation at 15-20 days if dry spell occurs during seedling stage
  • Maintain soil moisture at 60-70% field capacity during vegetative growth
  • In South Gujarat irrigated tracts, drip irrigation can be used with 4-6 litre per hour emitters
  • Ensure drainage channels are functional before sowing — June storms can cause flash flooding

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Basal dose at sowing: DAP 100 kg + potash 30 kg per hectare
  • Apply all phosphorus and potash as basal; nitrogen is split into 2-3 top dressings
  • First nitrogen top dressing (40-50 kg urea per hectare) at 30-35 days (knee-high stage)
  • Second nitrogen top dressing (50 kg urea per hectare) at boll formation stage (60-65 days)
  • No nitrogen after boll opening — excess N delays maturity and reduces fibre quality
  • Micronutrients: apply zinc sulphate 25 kg per hectare as basal if soil is deficient

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Seedling diseases: damping off — treat seed before sowing; drench with Carbendazim if damping off observed
  • Sucking pests (jassids, aphids, thrips): monitor from 15 days; spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml/litre if damage exceeds 5%
  • Whitefly (Bemisia tabaci): use yellow sticky traps; spray Buprofezin 1.5ml/litre if threshold crossed
  • Set up pheromone traps for pink bollworm (Pectinophora gossypiella) at 45 days — 5 traps per hectare
  • Alternaria leaf spot: spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre if circular brown spots appear after rain
  • Termite: apply Chlorpyriphos 2ml per litre drench along rows if termite mounds observed

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO harvest in June — cotton is in germination/seedling stage
  • Prepare harvesting equipment and storage space for later months
  • Arrange for pickers or labour for manual picking (October-March)
  • Contact ginning mills for October-November processing slots

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations in June
  • Prepare drying yards and storage godowns for later harvest
  • Clean and repair picking bags, storage bins, and transport vehicles
  • Arrange for market information and price tracking via eNAM or local mandi

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Monsoon onset month: typically 10-15 June in South Gujarat; 15-25 June in Saurashtra
  • Pre-monsoon heat: 40-45°C before monsoon; hot dry winds can desiccate seedlings
  • Heavy thunderstorms with strong winds — can damage young seedlings; provide support if needed
  • Monitor IMD monsoon onset forecasts and daily weather bulletins
  • Cyclone risk in Arabian Sea — coastal districts (Bhavnagar, Amreli, Valsad, Navsari) should be alert

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY enrollment: complete by 31st July for Kharif cotton; visit PACS or bank branch
  • Bt cotton seed subsidy: purchase certified seeds from authorized dealers with bill
  • Drip irrigation subsidy under GGRC: apply before sowing for best results
  • Gujarat State Cotton Mission: contact district agriculture office for technical support and subsidies
  • Kisan Credit Card (KCC): renew or apply for fresh credit before sowing season
