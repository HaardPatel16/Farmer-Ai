MONSOON WITHDRAWAL ADVISORY FOR GUJARAT FARMERS
================================================

OVERVIEW
The withdrawal of the Southwest Monsoon from Gujarat marks the start of the Rabi season. 
Understanding withdrawal timing helps farmers plan Kharif harvesting, residue management, 
and timely Rabi sowing to ensure good yields.

NORMAL MONSOON WITHDRAWAL DATES IN GUJARAT
- Kutch and North Gujarat: September 15-20
- Saurashtra: September 20-25
- Middle Gujarat: September 25-30
- South Gujarat (Surat, Navsari, Valsad): October 1-10

Signs of Monsoon Withdrawal:
- Dry northwest winds replace moist southwesterly flow
- Clear skies and decrease in cloudiness
- Humidity drops significantly
- No rainfall for 5+ consecutive days in most districts
- IMD officially announces withdrawal date

POST-MONSOON PERIOD: OCTOBER-NOVEMBER
After monsoon withdrawal, Gujarat experiences warm days (32-36°C) and cool nights — 
ideal conditions for many Rabi crops. Northeast monsoon occasionally brings rains 
to South Gujarat in October-November.

KHARIF CROP HARVESTING GUIDANCE

Groundnut (Saurashtra)
- Harvest when 75-80% pods show dark inner surface (mature)
- Dig test: cut 10 pods, check internal colour
- Harvest window: September 15 – October 15 (for June-sown crop)
- Delay causes aflatoxin risk if rains occur after maturity
- Dry peanuts to <10% moisture before storage

Cotton
- First picking at 45 days after boll opening
- Harvest in multiple rounds every 15-20 days
- Avoid picking wet cotton after rain (quality loss)
- Final harvest by December for Bt varieties

Pearl Millet (Bajra)
- Harvest when grains become hard and cannot be dented by thumbnail
- Thresh within 2-3 days of cutting to avoid bird damage
- Dry grain to <12% moisture for safe storage

Castor
- Harvest primary spike when 2/3 of capsules turn brown
- Dry spikes in sun for 3-4 days before threshing
- Avoid delay — ripe capsules shatter and cause field losses

POST-HARVEST FIELD PREPARATION FOR RABI

Residue Management
- Remove or incorporate crop residue promptly after harvest
- Chopped residue incorporated into soil improves organic matter
- Avoid burning residue — reduces soil microbes and pollutes air
- Use residue as mulch for Rabi nurseries

Deep Ploughing
- One deep ploughing (20-25 cm) after Kharif crop
- Breaks hardpan and improves water infiltration
- Best done in October before soil becomes too dry

Soil Testing
- Collect soil samples from 0-20 cm depth post-harvest
- Test at nearest soil testing lab (free at agriculture office)
- Apply lime/gypsum if pH >8.0 or if recommended on card

RABI SOWING CALENDAR AFTER MONSOON WITHDRAWAL
- Wheat (Middle and South Gujarat): October 25 – November 15
- Cumin (North Gujarat, Saurashtra): October 15 – November 10
- Mustard (North Gujarat): October 1-15
- Chickpea (Gram): October 15 – November 15
- Coriander: October 15 – November 15
- Fennel (Saunf): September 25 – October 25
- Potato (Saurashtra, Middle Gujarat): October 1 – November 15

MOISTURE CONSERVATION AFTER MONSOON
- Use conserved soil moisture for early Rabi crops (cumin, mustard)
- Apply pre-sowing irrigation if soil moisture is insufficient at 20-25 cm depth
- Sow Rabi crops on stored moisture where possible to reduce irrigation cost

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT PROGRAMS (RABI SEASON)
- Beej Mahotsav: Gujarat Govt distributes certified Rabi seeds at subsidised rates (October)
- Soil Health Card: Fertiliser recommendations valid for 2 seasons
- PM Fasal Bima Yojana: Rabi enrollment window opens October-November
- KVK demonstrations for high-yield Rabi varieties

CONTACT
- District Agriculture Office for Rabi seed availability
- Gujarat Agro Industries Corporation (GAIC): seeds and inputs
- KVK in your district for crop-specific Rabi advice
