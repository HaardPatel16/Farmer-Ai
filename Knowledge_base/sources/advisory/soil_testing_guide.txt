Soil testing guide for Gujarat farmers: Soil test before every crop season helps save money on unnecessary fertilizer and fix real deficiencies. What soil test measures: pH (acidity/alkalinity), EC (salinity), organic carbon (OC%), available nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium (NPK), micronutrients (zinc, iron, boron, manganese). How to collect soil sample: take soil from 0-15cm depth from at least 10-15 spots in each field in a W-pattern, mix together, take 500g from the mix. Label bag with farmer name, field location, crop to be grown. Send to soil testing lab. Labs: Soil Testing Lab in each district headquarters (government); Anand Agricultural University soil lab (best in Gujarat); ICAR-CAZRI lab for micronutrients; many private labs in major towns. Cost: government lab Rs 150-200 per sample (all parameters); Soil Health Card scheme — free soil testing once in 3 years through government. Results interpretation: pH 6.5-7.5 = ideal for most crops; below 6 = too acidic (add lime); above 8.5 = alkaline (add gypsum). OC above 0.75% = good; below 0.5% = apply FYM urgently. Zinc below 0.6 ppm = apply zinc sulphate 25kg/ha. Gujarat soil facts: most Gujarat soils are alkaline (pH 7.5-8.5), nitrogen deficient, and increasingly zinc deficient due to continuous cropping without zinc supplementation.
