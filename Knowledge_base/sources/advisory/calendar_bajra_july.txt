================================================================================
BAJRA (PEARL MILLET) CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — JULY
================================================================================

CROP: Bajra (Pearl Millet)
MONTH: July
PRIMARY REGION: Saurashtra, Kutch, North Gujarat
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR JULY
================================================================================
  • Summer ploughing should be completed by June; if not, do immediately with one ploughing
  • Apply FYM 5-10 tonnes per hectare before sowing (bajra requires less organic matter than cotton)
  • Level the field for uniform moisture; bajra tolerates poor soils but responds to good preparation
  • Soil pH should be 6.0-7.5; apply lime if pH < 5.5
  • Bajra is drought-tolerant and grows well in light sandy loam soils of Saurashtra and Kutch

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Sow after first good monsoon rain (50-75mm) in Saurashtra, Kutch, and North Gujarat
  • Ideal sowing window: 15-30 July in Saurashtra; 20-30 July in North Gujarat; 15-25 July in Kutch
  • Seed rate: 3-4 kg per hectare for hybrid varieties; 4-5 kg for local varieties
  • Spacing: 45-60 cm between rows; 15-20 cm between plants
  • Sow 2-3 seeds per hill; thin to one plant per hill at 15-20 days
  • First weeding critical within first 25 days as bajra is a poor competitor against weeds early on

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Generally rainfed; one protective irrigation if dry spell exceeds 15 days after sowing
  • First irrigation at 15-20 days if no rain for 15+ days during seedling stage
  • Critical irrigation at tillering (35-45 days) and heading (50-60 days) stages
  • In North Gujarat, some farmers use check-basin irrigation for bajra
  • Ensure drainage after heavy rain — bajra is somewhat tolerant of waterlogging but prolonged flooding damages roots

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Basal dose at sowing: DAP 40 kg + urea 20 kg per hectare
  • First nitrogen top dressing (40 kg urea per hectare) at 25-30 days after sowing
  • Second nitrogen top dressing (30-40 kg urea per hectare) at tillering stage (35-45 days)
  • No potash needed for bajra in most Gujarat soils
  • Micronutrients: apply zinc sulphate 25 kg per hectare as basal if soil is deficient (common in Kutch)

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Stem borer (Coniesta ignefusalis): monitor for dead heart (dead central shoot that pulls out easily and smells sour)
  • Apply Carbofuran 3G granules 20 kg per hectare in leaf whorl if >5% plants show dead heart
  • Downy mildew (green ear): if pale green streaks and stunted green ear appear, rogue out and destroy affected plants
  • Ergot (Claviceps fusiformis): honeydew stage — black sticky fluid on ears at flowering; spray Mancozeb 2.5g/litre
  • Aphids: if colonies exceed 10 per plant, spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml per litre
  • Termite: apply Chlorpyriphos 2ml per litre drench along rows if termite mounds observed

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO harvest in July — bajra is in seedling to early vegetative stage (20-40 days)
  • Continue monitoring crop growth and pest populations
  • Record keeping: document sowing date and variety for harvest planning (75-90 days after sowing)

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations in July
  • Prepare harvesting equipment (sickle, thresher) and storage space for later months
  • Arrange for labour during peak harvest season (September-October)
  • Contact fodder traders or grain market for September-October price information

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Peak monsoon month: heavy rainfall in Saurashtra; 250-350mm average in July
  • Waterlogging risk; check drainage channels after every rain event
  • Humidity 70-90%; favourable for fungal diseases — increase scouting frequency
  • Day temperatures 28-32°C; nights 24-26°C; warm and humid
  • IMD warnings: watch for heavy rainfall alerts and flood warnings

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY enrollment: complete by 31st July for Kharif bajra
  • Bajra seed subsidy: purchase certified seeds from authorized dealers with bill
  • Kisan Credit Card (KCC): renew or apply for fresh credit before sowing season
  • Fodder development scheme: contact district animal husbandry department for fodder subsidy
  • Contact KVK for improved varieties and cultivation techniques
