PESTICIDE SPRAY SAFETY GUIDE FOR GUJARAT FARMERS
==================================================

OVERVIEW
Pesticide poisoning causes hundreds of deaths and thousands of cases of illness among 
Gujarat farm workers each year. Safe handling, application, and disposal of pesticides 
protects farmers, their families, the environment, and consumers.

BEFORE SPRAYING — READING THE LABEL
- Always read the pesticide label before use
- Check: registration number, crop, pest, dosage, PHI (Pre-Harvest Interval)
- Note the HAZARD symbols: RED TRIANGLE = highly toxic, YELLOW = moderately toxic, BLUE = slightly toxic, GREEN = relatively safe
- Check the PHI (Pre-Harvest Interval) — do not harvest before this period

PRE-SPRAY PREPARATION

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
MANDATORY for all pesticide spraying:
- Chemical-resistant gloves (rubber or nitrile, NOT cotton)
- Long-sleeved shirt and full-length trousers
- Closed-toe shoes (not sandals/chappals)
- Hat or cap

RECOMMENDED for organophosphates, carbamates, and highly toxic chemicals:
- Face shield or goggles
- Nose-mouth mask (N95 respirator, not cloth mask)
- Rubber apron

Spray Equipment Check
- Inspect nozzle for clogs or damage before spraying
- Test spray with plain water to check for leaks
- Use flat-fan nozzles for field crops; cone nozzles for trees
- Calibrate sprayer to ensure correct dose per acre

Mixing Pesticides
- Mix in a well-ventilated area — never inside a closed room
- Add pesticide to water, not water to pesticide
- Never mix with bare hands — use a stick to stir
- Do not use eating utensils for measuring
- Prepare only the quantity needed for that day

DURING SPRAYING

Weather Conditions — When NOT to Spray
- Wind speed above 15 km/h (causes drift to non-target areas and farmer)
- Temperature above 35°C (increases volatilisation and skin absorption)
- Rain expected within 4 hours
- Early morning with heavy dew
Best time: Early morning (7-10 AM) or late afternoon (4-6 PM)

Spraying Posture
- Always spray downwind — never walk through sprayed area
- Walk backwards while spraying field crops
- Do not spray upward (liquid will fall on face/skin)
- Take breaks every 30-45 minutes in shade

Do NOT Do These
- Do not eat, drink, or smoke while spraying
- Do not clean nozzle by blowing with mouth
- Do not carry pesticides in food containers
- Do not spray near water bodies or wells
- Do not let children or pregnant women near spray area

AFTER SPRAYING

Cleaning Up
- Wash all equipment thoroughly with soap and water
- Wash hands, face, and all exposed skin with soap and water immediately after
- Shower and change clothing after each spray session
- Wash spray clothing separately from family clothes
- Triple-rinse empty pesticide containers before disposal

Pesticide Container Disposal
- Puncture containers so they cannot be reused
- Do NOT burn empty containers — releases toxic fumes
- Do NOT use for storing water, food, or animal feed
- Bury crushed containers in a designated pit away from water sources

SYMPTOMS OF PESTICIDE POISONING
Mild: Headache, dizziness, nausea, excessive sweating, blurred vision
Moderate: Vomiting, muscle twitching, difficulty breathing, salivation
Severe: Seizures, unconsciousness, pinpoint pupils, severe respiratory distress

FIRST AID FOR PESTICIDE POISONING
1. Remove the person from the exposure area immediately
2. Remove contaminated clothing
3. Wash skin/eyes with large amounts of clean water for 15-20 minutes
4. Keep patient calm and lying down
5. Call 108 (ambulance) immediately
6. Take the pesticide label/bottle to the hospital
7. Do NOT give milk, oil, or water if person is unconscious

For Organophosphate poisoning (very common in Gujarat): 
- Atropine is the antidote — available at district hospitals

PROHIBITED PESTICIDES IN INDIA (DO NOT USE)
- Monocrotophos (banned on vegetables since 2005)
- Methyl Parathion (banned)
- Endosulfan (banned since 2011)
- Chlorpyrifos on vegetables (restricted)

STORAGE OF PESTICIDES
- Store in original containers, tightly sealed
- Keep in cool, dry, locked storage away from food and feed
- Keep away from children — store above 1.5 m height
- Never store near heat or flame
- Dispose of old/expired pesticides through agriculture department

LEGAL REQUIREMENTS
- Pesticides Act 1968 governs sale and use in India
- Only buy pesticides from licensed dealers
- Keep purchase receipts for insurance claims
- Report fake/spurious pesticides to District Agriculture Officer
