HEAT WAVE ADVISORY FOR GUJARAT FARMERS
=======================================

OVERVIEW
Gujarat is one of India's most heat-prone states. Summer temperatures from April to June 
regularly exceed 42-45°C in Saurashtra, North Gujarat, and Kutch. Heat waves cause crop 
damage, livestock stress, and serious health risks for farm workers.

HEAT WAVE DEFINITION (IMD)
- Plains: Maximum temperature ≥40°C AND 4.5°C above normal = Heat Wave
- Severe Heat Wave: ≥47°C or 6.5°C above normal
- Gujarat typically experiences peak heat waves in May-June

IMPACT ON CROPS
- Cereals: Pollen sterility in wheat, grain shriveling at temperatures above 35°C
- Vegetables: Flower drop in tomato, brinjal, chilli above 38°C
- Cotton: Boll shedding during flowering under heat stress
- Groundnut: Pod filling affected if heat coincides with peg formation
- Mango: Pre-harvest fruit drop during heat waves

IMPACT ON LIVESTOCK
- Reduced feed intake and milk production (10-25% drop in peak heat)
- Heat exhaustion in buffaloes and cows
- Increased mortality risk in poultry
- Reduced reproduction rates

PROTECTIVE MEASURES FOR CROPS

Field Management
- Irrigate fields during evening or early morning to cool soil
- Apply light irrigation (sprinkler) to cool crop canopy
- Mulch with dry grass, straw, or sugarcane trash (5-8 cm layer)
- Erect shade nets (50% shade) over nursery beds and high-value vegetables
- Avoid transplanting on peak heat days; do it in evening

Nutritional Support
- Spray kaolin (white kaolin clay, 5%) on leaves to reflect sunlight
- Foliar spray of potassium nitrate (KNO3 1%) or 1% KCl to reduce heat stress
- Apply boron (0.2%) and zinc (0.5% ZnSO4) during heat stress period
- Avoid top dressing nitrogen fertiliser during extreme heat

Scheduling
- Postpone pesticide and fertiliser application during heat wave days
- Schedule irrigation during cooler hours to reduce evaporation losses
- Delay sowing of summer crops until temperatures drop

LIVESTOCK PROTECTION
- Provide shade using tarpaulins or tree shelter
- Ensure unlimited cool drinking water (buffaloes need 80-100 litres/day in summer)
- Reduce fodder intake and increase watery feed (green fodder, fruits)
- Apply wet gunny sacks on body or use sprinklers near shed
- Avoid working cattle between 11 AM and 4 PM
- Increase ventilation in poultry sheds; use foggers or coolers

FARM WORKER SAFETY
- Avoid working in fields between 12 PM and 3 PM during heat wave
- Drink ORS (oral rehydration solution) or buttermilk (chaas) regularly
- Wear light-coloured, loose cotton clothing and head cover
- Symptoms of heat stroke: confusion, no sweating, very high body temp — call 108 immediately

DISTRICT-WISE HEAT RISK (April-June)
- Extreme Risk: Kutch, Banaskantha, Patan, Surendranagar
- High Risk: Rajkot, Amreli, Bhavnagar, Ahmedabad, Gandhinagar
- Moderate Risk: Vadodara, Anand, Kheda
- Lower Risk: South Gujarat (Surat, Navsari, Valsad) due to sea breeze
