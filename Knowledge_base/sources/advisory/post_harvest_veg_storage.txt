POST-HARVEST VEGETABLE STORAGE FOR GUJARAT FARMERS
====================================================

OVERVIEW
Post-harvest losses of vegetables in Gujarat range from 25-40% due to improper handling, 
storage, and transport. Reducing these losses increases farmer income without increasing 
production costs. This guide covers storage methods, temperature requirements, and 
value addition options.

CAUSES OF POST-HARVEST LOSSES
- Mechanical damage during harvesting (bruising, cuts)
- Exposure to high temperatures after harvest (>35°C accelerates spoilage)
- Excessive moisture loss (wilting, shrinkage)
- Fungal and bacterial infections in storage
- Delayed transport to market
- Poor packaging (lack of ventilation in jute bags)

CRITICAL HARVESTING GUIDELINES
- Harvest early morning (5-9 AM) to avoid heat stress
- Use sharp, clean knives or clippers — never pull or tear
- Handle produce carefully to avoid bruising
- Do not harvest wet produce (after rain/dew) — increases rot risk
- Harvest at correct maturity stage (not over-ripe)

FIELD HEAT REMOVAL (PRECOOLING)
- Shade produce immediately after harvest
- Spread on gunny sacks in ventilated shade
- Avoid placing directly on hot ground or in sunlight
- Water cooling: dip crates in cold water for 10-15 minutes for leafy vegetables
- Even simple shade reduces temperature by 6-8°C compared to sun exposure

STORAGE REQUIREMENTS FOR MAJOR GUJARAT VEGETABLES

Tomato
- Pre-ripe (green-turning): 12-15°C, 90-95% RH, up to 4-6 weeks
- Ripe: 8-10°C, 90-95% RH, up to 1-2 weeks
- Field storage (no cold room): keep in shade, spread in single layer

Onion
- Cured properly at 30-35°C for 2-3 weeks after harvest
- Storage: 0-2°C or at ambient (25-30°C) with good ventilation
- Never store in plastic bags (moisture causes rotting)
- Traditional method: hang in jali (mesh) bags in dry, airy room

Potato
- Curing: 15-20°C for 10-14 days to heal skin cuts
- Long term: 3-5°C, 90-95% RH, in cold storage
- Avoid light exposure (causes greening and solanine formation)

Brinjal (Eggplant)
- Store at 10-12°C, 90-95% RH
- Shelf life: 2-3 weeks in cold room, 3-5 days at ambient
- Very susceptible to chilling injury below 10°C

Chilli (Green)
- Store at 7-10°C, 90-95% RH
- Shelf life: 2-3 weeks cold, 5-7 days ambient
- Dry red chilli: moisture below 10%; store in cool, dry room

Leafy Vegetables (Spinach, Fenugreek, Coriander)
- Highly perishable; shelf life 1-3 days at ambient
- Pre-cool and store at 0-5°C to extend to 7-10 days
- Bundle loosely; do not compress into bags
- Sprinkle with cold water to maintain turgidity

Okra (Bhindi)
- Very sensitive; harvest daily at correct size
- Store at 7-10°C for 7-10 days
- Chilling injury occurs below 7°C

ZERO-ENERGY COOL CHAMBER (ZECC) — LOW COST OPTION
- Simple evaporative cooling structure; no electricity needed
- Double brick wall with wet sand between layers
- Reduces temperature by 10-15°C compared to outside
- Maintains high humidity (90-95%)
- Cost: approximately ₹3,000-5,000 for small chamber
- Suitable for: tomato, brinjal, leafy vegetables, green chilli
- Construction details available at local KVK

COLD STORAGE FACILITIES IN GUJARAT
- Gujarat Agro Industries Corporation (GAIC) operates subsidised cold storage
- Private cold storage available in Anand, Nadiad, Surat, Rajkot, Junagadh
- Horticultural Produce Market Committee (HPMC) has pre-cooling facilities
- MIDH subsidy: 35-50% for setting up cold storage (apply via Hortnet portal)

VALUE ADDITION OPTIONS
- Sun-dried chilli: High value, 4-5x price of fresh (Gondal, Unjha markets)
- Dehydrated onion flakes: Export demand; units in Bhavnagar and Mahuva
- Tomato puree: Small-scale processing units at block level
- Pickle making: Self-help groups (SHGs) across Gujarat active in mango/chilli pickle
- Turmeric powder: Polishing and grinding units in Anand, Nadiad

PACKAGING FOR MARKET
- Use ventilated plastic crates instead of jute bags for tomato and brinjal
- Bamboo baskets lined with leaves for short-distance transport
- Avoid overfilling containers — bottom layer damage reduces quality
- Use foam nets for individual wrapping of premium produce (capsicum, cucumber)
