Kharif 2025 pest situation summary Gujarat: Based on reports from district agriculture offices and KVKs across Gujarat during June-October 2025 season. Cotton: Pink bollworm (PBW) pressure was moderate in Saurashtra (Amreli, Bhavnagar) and above normal in Kutch and North Gujarat (Banaskantha). Whitefly population was high in July-August in North Gujarat — CLCuV (Cotton Leaf Curl Virus) incidence was above normal in Banaskantha and some Patan areas. Thrips were high in early cotton (June-July) across Saurashtra. Groundnut: Late leaf spot was severe in Rajkot and Amreli in August-September due to intermittent rainfall and high humidity; tobacco caterpillar (Spodoptera litura) pressure was above average in Saurashtra in July-August. Thrips (PBNV vector) pressure was moderate. Bajra: Downy mildew ("green ear") incidence was above normal in Banaskantha; seed treatment compliance helped control but some susceptible varieties had 15-20% infection. Paddy (South Gujarat): Brown plant hopper pressure was high in Navsari and Valsad in September. Leaf folder was moderate. Castor (Saurashtra): Semi-looper outbreak in Amreli and Bhavnagar districts in September; some fields reported 60-70% defoliation in outbreak pockets. Vegetable crops: YVMV in okra was high wherever susceptible varieties were planted in Kharif — confirms need for YVMV-resistant hybrids only in Kharif. Chilli (Kharif): Mite infestation was severe in late Kharif chilli in Kheda and Vadodara — abamectin applications were heavily used. Outlook for Rabi 2025-26: termite pressure likely to be high in fields with poor soil health — use chlorpyrifos soil treatment at sowing; aphid pressure on mustard and wheat expected to be moderate given warm December forecasts.
