================================================================================
POTATO CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — NOVEMBER
================================================================================

CROP: Potato
MONTH: November
PRIMARY REGION: Saurashtra, North Gujarat
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR NOVEMBER
================================================================================
  • Well-drained sandy loam soils with pH 5.5-6.5 ideal for potato
  • Apply FYM 15-20 tonnes per hectare 2-3 weeks before sowing
  • Prepare ridges (20-25 cm high) for better drainage and tuber development
  • In Saurashtra, apply gypsum 250 kg per hectare for sodic soil reclamation before sowing
  • Soil should be loose and friable for tuber expansion; avoid compacted soils

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Rabi potato sowing in Saurashtra and North Gujarat from mid-October to mid-November
  • Seed rate: 2,000-2,500 kg per hectare of certified seed tubers (size 35-50g)
  • Spacing: 60 cm between rows; 20-25 cm between plants
  • Plant at 8-10 cm depth on top of ridges; cover with soil and light irrigation
  • Treat seed tubers with Carbendazim 2g + Streptocycline 1g per kg OR Mancozeb 3g per kg to prevent seed-borne diseases
  • Use certified varieties: Kufri Jyoti, Kufri Sindhuri, Gujarat Potato-1 for Gujarat conditions

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • First irrigation immediately after sowing; then every 7-10 days depending on soil type
  • Maintain soil moisture at 80% field capacity for optimal tuber development
  • Critical irrigation at stolon formation (30 days), tuber initiation (45 days), and tuber bulking (60 days)
  • Reduce irrigation 2 weeks before harvest to harden skin and improve storage life
  • In North Gujarat, sprinkler irrigation can be used for uniform moisture distribution

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Basal dose at sowing: DAP 100 kg + potash 50 kg per hectare
  • First top dressing (50 kg urea per hectare) at stolon formation (30 days after sowing)
  • Second top dressing (50 kg urea per hectare) at tuber initiation (45 days after sowing)
  • Third top dressing (20 kg urea per hectare) at tuber bulking stage (60 days after sowing)
  • Micronutrients: foliar spray of 0.5% zinc sulphate + 0.2% boron at tuber initiation for better tuber size

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Early blight (Alternaria solani): if brown spots with concentric rings appear, spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre
  • Late blight (Phytophthora infestans): if water-soaked lesions appear on leaves, spray Metalaxyl+Mancozeb 2.5g per litre immediately
  • Potato tuber moth: if larvae found in tubers, spray Indoxacarb 0.5ml per litre or use pheromone traps
  • Aphids: if colonies exceed 10 per plant, spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml per litre
  • White grubs: if damage observed, drench with Chlorpyriphos 2ml per litre
  • Monitor every 7-10 days; early blight and late blight can cause total crop loss if not controlled

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO harvest in November — potato is in germination/early vegetative stage
  • Continue monitoring germination and seedling establishment
  • Record keeping: document sowing date and variety for harvest planning (February-March)
  • Prepare storage facilities and grading equipment for February-March harvest

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations in November
  • Prepare cold storage facilities for February-March harvest
  • Contact potato traders, chip manufacturers, or market yards for price information
  • Potato is a high-value crop; maintain quality standards for seed, table, and processing markets

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Day temperatures: 28-32°C; nights 12-16°C; pleasant weather ideal for potato germination
  • November dew is heavy — favours fungal diseases (early blight, late blight); increase scouting frequency
  • First western disturbances may bring light rain to North Gujarat; beneficial for establishment
  • Cold wave risk in North Gujarat by month-end; frost can damage young potato plants — protect with mulch
  • IMD forecast: monitor for western disturbance predictions and cold wave warnings

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY enrollment: complete by 15th December for Rabi potato; visit PACS or bank branch
  • Potato seed subsidy: purchase certified seed tubers from authorized dealers with bill and Aadhaar
  • Cold storage subsidy: contact district horticulture office for cold storage construction subsidy
  • Soil Health Card: collect updated card from KVK or ATMA office before fertilizer application
  • Kisan Credit Card (KCC): renew or apply for fresh credit before sowing season
  • PM-KISAN: verify installment eligibility and Aadhaar seeding status
