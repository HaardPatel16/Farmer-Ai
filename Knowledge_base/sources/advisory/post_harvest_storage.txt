Post-harvest storage guidance for Gujarat farm produce: Proper storage prevents losses of 20-30% that occur between harvest and market. Grain storage: dry grain to below 12-13% moisture before storage (use moisture meter or bite test — grain should not dent with nail). Treat storage bags with Malathion 50EC — spray inside and outside gunny bag before filling. Airtight storage (metal bins, HDPE bins with seal) protects against weevils and rodents without chemicals. For large quantities: use PUSA bins (hermetic storage) — CO2 builds up inside and kills insects without any pesticide. Groundnut storage: critical to store below 8% moisture to prevent aflatoxin contamination — aflatoxin is a cancer-causing mold toxin and causes rejection in export. Never store groundnut in contact with wall or floor directly — use pallets. Onion storage: cure harvested onions 10-12 days in shade with airflow; store in open bamboo or mesh crates in ventilated shed; never store in sealed bags; temperature 25-30°C with low humidity. Potato storage: cool dark storage 10-15°C ideal; if no cold storage available, store in ventilated shed and check for sprouting/rot weekly. Cold storage facility: available in major towns, charges Rs 300-500 per quintal per month; negotiate early-season booking. Government warehouse: WDRA (Warehouse Development and Regulatory Authority) registered warehouses in each taluka issue warehouse receipts that can be pledged for bank loans.
