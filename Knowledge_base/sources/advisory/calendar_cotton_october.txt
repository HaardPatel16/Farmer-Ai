================================================================================
COTTON CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — OCTOBER
================================================================================

CROP: Cotton
MONTH: October
PRIMARY REGION: Saurashtra, North Gujarat, South Gujarat
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR OCTOBER
================================================================================
  • NO soil preparation in October for cotton — crop is in harvest stage
  • For fields where cotton harvest is complete, begin summer ploughing for next Kharif
  • Deep ploughing (20-25 cm) after harvest improves soil structure and exposes pupae to sunlight
  • Apply FYM 10-15 tonnes per hectare for fields being prepared for next season
  • Collect and destroy cotton stalks after final picking to reduce pink bollworm overwintering

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • NO sowing in October for cotton — too late for Kharif and too early for summer cotton
  • If planning summer cotton (irrigated), prepare nurseries in February-March
  • For next Kharif, plan seed procurement and treat seeds with fungicide before sowing in June
  • Focus on harvest and post-harvest management of standing crop

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • NO irrigation in October for cotton — allow field to dry for picking
  • Excess moisture at boll opening delays maturity and causes boll rot
  • If unseasonal rain occurs, provide drainage immediately to prevent boll staining
  • For fields where harvest is complete, irrigate only if planning Rabi intercrop or fodder
  • In South Gujarat, some farmers take Rabi gram or wheat as intercrop after cotton

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • NO fertilizer application after boll opening — excess nitrogen delays maturity and reduces fibre quality
  • If crop shows severe deficiency, foliar spray of 2% urea can be applied early in October (before boll opening)
  • For next season, plan soil testing and balanced fertilizer application
  • In Rabi intercrop fields, apply basal dose for Rabi crop at sowing
  • Record fertilizer usage for PM-KISAN and soil health card documentation

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Pink bollworm: late season infestations damage open bolls; remove and destroy infested bolls
  • Avoid spraying close to harvest; observe pre-harvest intervals (PHI) for all chemicals
  • Aphid and whitefly: if heavy infestation, use safe chemicals with short PHI (e.g., acetamiprid)
  • Boll rot: if humid conditions persist, remove affected bolls and improve field aeration
  • After final picking, collect and burn or bury cotton stalks to destroy overwintering pink bollworm larvae
  • Monitor for storage pests (pink bollworm, red cotton bug) in ginned cotton and seeds

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • First picking begins in early-sown crop (May-June sown); pick every 15-20 days
  • Pick only fully open bolls (white fluffed cotton); avoid green and immature bolls
  • Pick in dry conditions; avoid picking during rain or heavy dew to prevent moisture damage
  • Store picked cotton in dry, ventilated area; do not heap wet cotton — causes heating and quality loss
  • Grade cotton before selling: separate good quality (long staple, clean) from stained/damaged cotton
  • Second and third pickings continue through November-December for late-sown crop

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • Dry picked cotton to 8-10% moisture before storing or transporting to ginning mill
  • Use clean gunnies or plastic-lined bags for storage; avoid direct contact with ground
  • Transport to ginning mill within 7-10 days of picking to preserve fibre quality
  • Ginning should be done within 15 days of picking; delayed ginning reduces fibre length and strength
  • Store ginned cotton (lint) in cool, dry godowns with proper ventilation
  • Maintain records of quantity, grade, and sale price for PMFBY and income documentation

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Post-monsoon clear skies; day temperatures 32-36°C; nights 18-22°C; ideal for harvest and drying
  • Northeast monsoon (retreating monsoon) may bring scattered rain to South Gujarat; monitor forecasts
  • Humidity drops to 50-70%; favourable for cotton drying and storage
  • Unseasonal rain in October can stain cotton and reduce market price; cover stored cotton with tarpaulin
  • First cold wave signals in North Gujarat by month-end; does not affect cotton harvest significantly

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY: if crop damage occurred, file claim with required documentation (photos, FIR, survey report)
  • Cotton price support: sell through eNAM or registered mandi for transparent pricing
  • Gujarat State Cotton Mission: contact for market information and price stabilization support
  • PM-KISAN: verify 4th installment eligibility and Aadhaar seeding status
  • Kisan Credit Card: repay crop loan or renew for next season
