WEATHER-BASED IRRIGATION ADVISORY FOR GUJARAT FARMERS
=======================================================

OVERVIEW
Irrigation accounts for 70-80% of total water use in Gujarat. Over-irrigation wastes water 
and increases costs, while under-irrigation reduces yields. Weather-based irrigation 
scheduling helps farmers apply the right amount of water at the right time, saving 
water and improving crop productivity.

KEY CONCEPTS

Evapotranspiration (ET)
- ET is the combination of soil evaporation and plant transpiration
- It represents how much water crops actually use
- Affected by temperature, humidity, wind speed, and solar radiation
- Higher temperatures = higher ET = more irrigation needed

Reference ET (ETo)
- Standard ET calculated for a reference grass crop
- Available from IMD agromet stations for Gujarat districts
- Used to calculate actual crop water needs (ETc = ETo × Kc)

Crop Coefficient (Kc)
- Ratio of crop ET to reference ET
- Changes through crop growth stages
- Example for groundnut: Kc = 0.4 (early growth), 1.15 (peak), 0.6 (late season)

DETERMINING WHEN TO IRRIGATE

Feel Method (Simple, No Instruments)
- Take soil from root zone depth (20-30 cm)
- Press firmly in hand:
  * Soil forms ball that breaks on slight pressure = RIGHT MOISTURE
  * Soil doesn't form ball, feels dry = IRRIGATE NOW
  * Soil is wet, sticks together, water drips = TOO WET, delay irrigation

Tensiometer Method
- Device measures soil water tension (suction)
- Reading <20 centibars = sufficient moisture
- Reading 20-40 centibars = irrigate soon
- Reading >40-50 centibars = crop under stress, irrigate immediately
- Cost: ₹800-1500 per set; available at KVK

IMD-Based Approach
- Use weekly ET data from IMD agromet advisory
- If weekly rainfall < 60% of weekly ETc, apply irrigation
- Available from Meghdoot app and KVK

CROP-SPECIFIC IRRIGATION GUIDELINES FOR GUJARAT

Groundnut (Kharif, Saurashtra)
- Critical stages: Pegging (45-55 DAS), pod filling (60-80 DAS)
- Skip irrigation in early rains if 50+ mm received in a week
- Total water requirement: 450-550 mm in 120-day crop
- Drip: 4-5 litres/plant/day during pod fill; 2-3 litres/plant at other stages
- Avoid waterlogging during pod formation (causes pod rot)

Cotton
- Critical stages: Squaring (40-60 DAS), boll development (70-120 DAS)
- Water requirement: 700-900 mm for entire season
- Drip: 2.5-3 litres/plant/day at boll stage
- Withhold irrigation 15 days before final harvest to force boll opening

Castor (Kharif, North Gujarat)
- Drought tolerant but responds well to 2-3 supplemental irrigations
- Critical stage: Inflorescence emergence and capsule formation
- Apply 40-50 mm at each critical stage if no rain

Pearl Millet (Bajra)
- Mostly rainfed in Gujarat; 1-2 supplemental irrigations increase yield 30%
- Critical stages: Boot stage (panicle formation) and grain filling
- Apply 30-40 mm at each stage if rainfall is inadequate

Wheat (Rabi, Middle and South Gujarat)
- Critical stages: Crown root initiation (20-25 DAS), tillering (40-45 DAS), 
  jointing (55-60 DAS), flowering (75-80 DAS), grain filling (95-100 DAS)
- 5-6 irrigations of 50-60 mm each = optimal
- First irrigation most critical — never skip CRI stage

Cumin (North Gujarat, Saurashtra Rabi)
- Very sensitive to excess water — well-drained sandy loam required
- 3-4 light irrigations (25-30 mm each)
- Do not irrigate within 3 days of predicted rain
- Withhold last irrigation 10 days before harvest

DRIP IRRIGATION — SCHEDULING TIPS
- Run drip for shorter, more frequent cycles rather than long cycles
- Soil type matters: sandy soils need more frequent, shorter runs
- Check tensiometer before running drip
- Fertigate (inject fertilizer through drip) during active growth stages
- Flush drip lines monthly to prevent clogging

WATER SAVING TIPS
- Irrigate in early morning or evening to reduce evaporation
- Mulching reduces water need by 20-30%
- Land levelling reduces run-off and improves water distribution
- Use soil moisture sensors (tensiometers) to avoid guesswork
- Avoid flood irrigation on sandy soils (high percolation loss)

SUBSIDY FOR MICRO-IRRIGATION IN GUJARAT
- Drip/Sprinkler irrigation subsidy: 55-80% of cost (SC/ST/small farmers: higher)
- Apply through: iKhedut Portal (ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in)
- Component: Gujarat Micro Irrigation Mission (GMIM) under PM Krishi Sinchayee Yojana
- Process: Apply online → site inspection → installation by approved vendor → subsidy
