================================================================================
COTTON CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — AUGUST
================================================================================

CROP: Cotton
MONTH: August
PRIMARY REGION: Saurashtra, North Gujarat, South Gujarat
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR AUGUST
================================================================================
  • If waterlogging occurred in July, apply gypsum 250 kg per hectare to reclaim sodic patches
  • In waterlogged fields, aerate soil by shallow cultivation between rows
  • Maintain drainage channels; August floods are common and can cause sudden wilt
  • Avoid deep tillage — cotton roots are shallow and easily damaged at this stage
  • If soil is compacted, light inter-culture with hand hoe improves aeration

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • NO sowing in August — too late for profitable cotton crop in Gujarat
  • Focus on crop management: weeding, pest scouting, and fertilizer application
  • If gaps are severe (>20% missing plants), consider intercropping with short-duration moong
  • Remove waterlogged and dead plants; do NOT replant at this stage

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Generally rainfed; if dry spell exceeds 10 days during boll formation, provide 25mm irrigation
  • Critical water requirement at flowering and boll formation — do not allow moisture stress
  • In irrigated tracts, schedule irrigation every 7-10 days during boll formation
  • Avoid waterlogging — August floods cause root rot and sudden wilt
  • Remove excess water within 24 hours of flooding; apply Copper oxychloride drench to surrounding healthy plants

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Second nitrogen top dressing (50 kg urea per hectare) at boll formation stage (60-65 days)
  • If crop is vigorous and dark green, reduce nitrogen to 30 kg urea per hectare
  • If boll shedding is high, spray 2% urea + 0.5% potassium sulphate at flowering
  • No fertilizer application during heavy rain — nutrients will leach; wait for rain to stop
  • Plan for final nitrogen management in September (reduce/stop after boll opening)

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Pink bollworm (Pectinophora gossypiella): pheromone trap catches peak in August
  • If trap catch exceeds 8 moths per trap per week for 3 consecutive weeks, spray Emamectin benzoate 0.4g/litre
  • American bollworm (Helicoverpa armigera): scout for larvae and eggs on leaves and squares
  • Spray Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis) 1g/litre for early instar larvae; HaNPV at dusk for larva control
  • Mirid bug (Harpedona marginella): causes leaf crumpling; spray acetamiprid if secondary pest emergence observed
  • Alternaria and Cercospora leaf spot: spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre as preventive after rain periods
  • Whitefly: if resurgence observed, spray Buprofezin 1.5ml/litre; avoid repeated use of same chemical group
  • Weekly scouting: minimum 2 times per week in August for bollworms, aphids, and disease

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO harvest in August — cotton is in peak flowering and boll formation stage
  • Remove and destroy fallen flowers and bolls to reduce pink bollworm habitat
  • Continue recording pest counts and weather data for advisory updates

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations in August
  • Prepare pickers and harvesting bags for September-October first picking
  • Arrange transport to ginning mill or market yard
  • Clean and repair storage godowns; fumigate to prevent pest infestation

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • August is the wettest month in Gujarat: 300-450mm average rainfall
  • Waterlogging and flood risk remains high; prioritize drainage over irrigation
  • Humidity 75-95%; ideal for fungal diseases — increase scouting to twice per week
  • Day temperatures 28-32°C; nights 24-26°C; warm and humid
  • IMD warnings: heavy rainfall alerts (100mm+ in 24 hours) and flood warnings for coastal districts

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY: if crop damage from waterlogging or pest, notify within 72 hours and file claim
  • Crop damage survey: contact local agriculture officer ( taluka development officer) for assessment
  • Bt cotton seed subsidy: no new claims in August; focus on crop protection
  • Kisan Credit Card: avail credit for pesticide and labour costs
  • Contact KVK for pest identification and spray recommendations
