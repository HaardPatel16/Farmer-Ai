Pesticide spray safety guide for Gujarat farmers: Pesticide poisoning causes 1,000+ hospitalisations in Gujarat annually — most are preventable. Personal protective equipment (PPE): wear before every spray — full-sleeve shirt and full-length trousers (cotton, not synthetic), rubber gloves (NOT cotton gloves — they absorb chemical), rubber boots (not sandals or flip flops), wide-brim hat, and eye protection (safety glasses or full face shield for mixing). Mixing safety: mix pesticides outdoors or in well-ventilated shed; never mix with bare hands; read label completely before use; use measuring cup — never use bare-hand measurement. Spray conditions: spray only when wind is below 10-15 km/hr (leaves swaying gently); do not spray in rain or if rain expected in 2 hours; avoid spraying during 10am-3pm in summer (heat causes rapid evaporation and chemical degradation, and increases heat stress on applicator); best time is early morning (6-9am) or evening (4-6pm). Direction: always spray with wind at your back, not into wind. Duration: do not spray continuously for more than 2-3 hours — take break, remove gloves and wash hands, drink clean water; organophosphate poisoning symptoms (dizziness, nausea, excessive sweating, small pupils) — stop immediately and go to hospital. Post-spray: wash all clothes, gloves, and equipment separately from household items; take shower before eating or drinking; store pesticides in original containers only — never in soft drink bottles that children may consume. Disposal: empty pesticide containers — puncture and bury 50+ metres from water source; never rinse and sell empty containers. Emergency: Gujarat Poison Control Centre — Ahmedabad 079-22681111; Surat 0261-2457251; village level contact: ASHA worker or PHC (Primary Health Centre). Post-harvest interval (PHI): observe waiting period between last spray and harvest as on label — minimum 7-14 days for most pesticides; never spray on the day of harvest.
