================================================================================
GROUNDNUT CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — JULY
================================================================================

CROP: Groundnut
MONTH: July
PRIMARY REGION: Saurashtra, Kutch, North Gujarat
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR JULY
================================================================================
  • If waterlogging occurred in June-July, apply gypsum 250 kg per hectare to improve soil structure
  • In waterlogged fields, open drainage channels and aerate soil by shallow inter-culture
  • Light hoeing between rows improves aeration and controls weeds
  • Do NOT deep cultivate — groundnut pegs are shallow and easily damaged
  • Maintain soil pH at 6.0-7.0; acidic soils require lime application

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Gap filling within 10 days if germination is poor; maintain plant population of 15-20 plants per sq metre
  • If gaps are severe (>20%), consider re-sowing only in small patches
  • Weeding: first manual weeding at 20-25 days; second at 40-45 days
  • In Saurashtra, some farmers use pre-emergence herbicide (Pendimethalin 1.0 kg/ha) for weed control
  • Maintain weed-free field until canopy closure (45-50 days)

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Generally rainfed in July; one irrigation if dry spell exceeds 10 days during vegetative stage
  • First irrigation at 20-25 days if no rain for 10+ days
  • Maintain soil moisture at 60-70% field capacity during vegetative growth
  • In Saurashtra, sprinkler irrigation can be used for uniform moisture distribution
  • Ensure drainage after heavy rain — waterlogging causes collar rot and aflatoxin risk

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • NO nitrogen top dressing — groundnut fixes atmospheric nitrogen through root nodules
  • If crop shows yellowing, apply 20 kg DAP per hectare at 30 days (rarely needed)
  • Micronutrient spray: 0.5% zinc sulphate + 0.2% boron at 30 days if deficiency symptoms appear
  • Plan for gypsum application in August at flowering stage (250-400 kg/ha)
  • In boron-deficient soils, foliar spray of 0.2% borax at flowering improves pod filling

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • White grub: monitor for adult beetle activity at night; if severe, drench with Chlorpyriphos 2ml/litre
  • Leaf miner (Aproaerema modicella): monitor from 30 days; spray if damage exceeds 5% leaf area
  • Aphids: if colonies exceed 10 per plant, spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml per litre
  • Collar rot (Aspergillus niger): if seedlings wilt, drench with Carbendazim 1g per litre
  • Tikka leaf spot (Cercospora): if early infection appears, spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre as preventive
  • Stem rot (Sclerotium rolfsii): if white fungal growth appears at base, drench with Carbendazim 1g/litre + Metalaxyl 2.5g/litre

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO harvest in July — groundnut is in vegetative stage (30-50 days after sowing)
  • Continue monitoring crop growth and pest populations
  • Record keeping: document sowing date, variety, fertilizer, and pest control measures for traceability

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations in July
  • Prepare harvesting equipment and storage facilities for September-October harvest
  • Purchase required pesticides and store safely away from rain and sunlight
  • Arrange for labour during peak digging season (September-October)

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Peak monsoon month: heavy rainfall in Saurashtra; 250-350mm average in July
  • Waterlogging risk highest in July; check drainage channels after every rain event
  • Humidity 70-90%; favourable for fungal diseases — increase scouting frequency
  • Day temperatures 28-32°C; nights 24-26°C; warm and humid
  • IMD warnings: watch for heavy rainfall alerts (100mm+ in 24 hours) and flood warnings

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY enrollment: complete by 31st July for Kharif groundnut
  • Groundnut seed subsidy: claim through authorized dealer with GST bill and Aadhaar
  • Drip/sprinkler irrigation subsidy: submit proposal to GGRC if not already done
  • Kisan Credit Card: avail fresh credit for pesticide and fertilizer purchases
  • Contact KVK for pest identification and spray recommendations
