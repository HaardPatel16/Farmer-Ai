IMD seasonal weather forecast guide for Gujarat farmers: Indian Meteorological Department (IMD) issues seasonal forecasts that help farmers make key planting and management decisions. How to access forecasts: IMD official website imd.gov.in; Meghdoot app (free, Play Store) — farmer-specific weather advisory; Kisan Mausam (SMS weather alert service) — register at mausam.imd.gov.in or through KVK; Damini app (lightning alert — important for safety during monsoon fieldwork). Seasonal outlooks: IMD issues Long Range Forecast (LRF) for SW monsoon (June-September) in April and updated in June. Key parameters: All-India rainfall probability; Gujarat regional forecast; district-level forecast not available in seasonal prediction. How to use LRF: Below normal forecast (below 90% of LIM): water conservation critical, avoid water-intensive crops (sugarcane, banana, long-duration cotton), sow short-duration drought-tolerant varieties (JL-24 groundnut, short bajra hybrids). Normal to above normal: full Kharif cropping safe. District-level daily forecast: IMD Agrimet (Agricultural Meteorology) division issues 5-day block forecast for each district of Gujarat — available at gramin.imd.gov.in; key parameters include maximum-minimum temperature, rainfall probability, wind speed (important for spray timing), humidity. Cyclone alerts: Gujarat coast (Saurashtra and Kutch) is vulnerable to Arabian Sea cyclones (May-June and October-November); INCOIS (incois.gov.in) and IMD issue 5-day cyclone track forecasts; Krishi Vigyan Kendras issue crop-specific advisories during cyclone approach. Practical use: check 5-day forecast before any spray operation (avoid spray if rain in 2 days, wind above 15 km/hr or temperature above 35°C); check forecast before irrigation to avoid rain arriving immediately after; plan harvest dates using 10-day forecast.
