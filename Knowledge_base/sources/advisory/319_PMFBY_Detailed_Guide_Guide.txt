1. INTRODUCTION
Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) is India's flagship crop insurance scheme, providing comprehensive risk coverage to farmers at nominal premium rates.

2. KEY FEATURES
- Comprehensive risk coverage from sowing to post-harvest
- Lowest premium rates globally
- Use of technology (satellite, drones, mobile apps)
- Timely claim settlement
- Coverage for prevented sowing
- Post-harvest losses covered
- Localized calamity coverage

3. PREMIUM RATES
Farmer's Share:
- Kharif crops: 2% of sum insured
- Rabi crops: 1.5% of sum insured
- Annual commercial/horticultural: 5%

Government Subsidy:
- Central + State government pays remaining premium
- No upper limit on subsidy
- Premium subsidy shared 50:50 (Center:State)

4. COVERAGE LEVELS
Sum Insured Options:
- Compulsory component: Based on scale of finance
- Add-on coverage: Up to 150% of average yield
- Cover for post-harvest losses: Up to 14 days

Yield-based Coverage:
- Indemnity level: 70% (default), 80% or 90%
- Threshold yield: Average of last 7 years x indemnity level
- Loss calculation: (Threshold yield - Actual yield) / Threshold yield x Sum insured

5. ENROLLMENT PROCESS
Step 1: Visit bank at loan disbursement (compulsory for loanee)
- Fill PMFBY proposal form
- Submit land records
- Pay premium

Step 2: For non-loanee farmers
- Visit CSC (Common Service Center)
- Online enrollment through portal
- Contact insurance company
- Pay premium before deadline

Deadlines:
- Kharif: July 31
- Rabi: December 31
- Commercial crops: Varies by crop

6. CLAIM PROCESS
Step 1: Crop Cutting Experiments (CCE)
- Conducted by state government
- Random plot selection
- Yield measurement
- Technology-aided validation

Step 2: Yield Data Processing
- CCE data compilation
- Yield comparison with threshold
- Loss calculation

Step 3: Claim Settlement
- Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) to bank account
- Timeline: Within 2 months of harvest
- Notification via SMS

7. LOCALIZED CALAMITY CLAIMS
Covered Calamities:
- Hailstorm
- Landslide
- Cyclone
- Unseasonal rains
- Flood
- Drought

Process:
- Report to agriculture office within 72 hours
- Panchayat verification
- Insurance company assessment
- Individual claim settlement

8. POST-HARVEST LOSSES
Coverage:
- Up to 14 days after harvest
- Named perils only (cyclone, flood, hail)
- Coverage for crop lying in field
- Coverage during drying in cut and spread condition

9. EXCLUSIONS
- Losses due to war/nuclear risks
- Malicious damage
- Preventable risks
- Losses covered under other insurance
- Losses after harvesting (except specified)

10. IMPORTANT CONTACTS
