================================================================================
GUJARAT FARMING CALENDAR — NOVEMBER (આસો / કારતક (Aso / Kartak))
================================================================================

OVERVIEW:
November is a critical month for Gujarat agriculture. The state's diverse agro-climatic zones — Saurashtra, North Gujarat, South Gujarat, and Kutch — each have specific crop activities during this period. This calendar provides detailed, week-by-week guidance for farmers.

CROPS IN FOCUS THIS MONTH:
  • Wheat (early growth)
  • Mustard (vegetative)
  • Gram (vegetative)
  • Cumin (sowing/germination)
  • Potato (sowing)
  • Cotton (late picking)

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION
================================================================================
  • Summer ploughing should be completed by now if not already done; deep ploughing (20-25 cm) improves water infiltration
  • For heavy black cotton soils, apply organic matter / FYM (Farm Yard Manure) 10-15 tonnes per hectare at least 2-3 weeks before sowing
  • Level the field using a leveller to ensure uniform irrigation and prevent waterlogging
  • In Saurashtra, apply gypsum 250 kg per hectare for sodic soil reclamation before sowing
  • Conduct soil testing: pH should be 6.5-7.5 for most crops; apply lime if pH < 6.0
  • Prepare raised beds (15-20 cm high) for vegetable crops to improve drainage during monsoon

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Wheat: continue sowing in Saurashtra and North Gujarat until 15th November; after 15th, yield declines significantly
  • Mustard: sowing window until 15th November; use certified varieties suitable for Gujarat (Gujarat Mustard-1, GM-2)
  • Gram: sowing continues until mid-November; use high-yielding varieties (Gujarat Gram-1, GG-2, JG-11)
  • Cumin: sow from mid-November to early December; cool, dry weather essential for good germination
  • Potato: sowing window until mid-November; use certified seed tubers (Gujarat Potato-1, Kufri Jyoti)
  • Chilli (Rabi): transplant nursery-raised seedlings (40-45 days old) in well-prepared fields
  • Onion (Rabi): sow nursery in first fortnight for transplanting in January

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Wheat: first irrigation at CRI stage (20-25 days after sowing) — apply 50mm water; critical for tillering
  • Mustard: generally rainfed; one irrigation at flowering if no rain for 15+ days
  • Gram: generally rainfed; one protective irrigation during pod filling if dry spell exceeds 20 days
  • Cumin: light irrigation immediately after sowing; then every 10-12 days; avoid waterlogging
  • Potato: first irrigation immediately after sowing; maintain 80% field capacity; irrigate every 7-10 days
  • Cotton (late sown): final picking; no irrigation needed
  • All Rabi crops: November dew favours fungal diseases; avoid overhead irrigation in evening

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Wheat: first top dressing (40-50 kg urea per hectare) at CRI stage (20-25 days after sowing)
  • Mustard: apply 30 kg urea per hectare at flowering if crop looks pale
  • Gram: if basal DAP was insufficient, apply 20 kg DAP per hectare at early branching stage (30 days)
  • Cumin: apply 20 kg urea per hectare at 30 days after sowing; second dose at 50 days if needed
  • Potato: apply 50 kg urea per hectare at stolon formation (30 days); second dose at tuber initiation (45 days)
  • Chilli: apply 50 kg urea + 25 kg potash per hectare at 30 days after transplanting

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Wheat: yellow rust monitoring begins in North Gujarat; scout every 7-10 days
  • Wheat: aphids — if colonies exceed 10 per tiller, spray Dimethoate 2ml per litre
  • Mustard: sawfly and aphids — spray Dimethoate 2ml per litre if aphids exceed 10 per plant
  • Gram: pod borer (Helicoverpa) — begin monitoring at flowering; spray NSKE 5% or HaNPV if eggs found
  • Cumin: wilt and blight complex — drench with Carbendazim 1g + Mancozeb 2.5g per litre if wilting observed
  • Potato: early blight (Alternaria solani) — spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre at first sign of brown spots
  • Chilli: thrips and mites — spray Fipronil 1.5ml per litre if thrips exceed 5 per flower

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • Cotton: late picking continues; complete by month-end; grade and store properly
  • Groundnut: harvest of late-sown crop continues; complete by mid-November
  • Paddy: harvest of late-transplanted crop continues; dry and store before winter humidity
  • Bajra: post-harvest processing; thresh, clean, and store at 8% moisture
  • Moong: harvest remaining summer moong; complete by first week of November
  • Sugarcane: harvest of early varieties in South Gujarat begins

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • Clean and dry harvested produce immediately; moisture content should be reduced to safe levels (8-10% for grains, 10-12% for oilseeds)
  • Use tarpaulins or cemented drying yards to avoid aflatoxin contamination in groundnut and cotton
  • Store in properly ventilated godowns; use neem leaves or safe storage pesticides
  • For cotton, ginning should be done within 15 days of picking to preserve fibre quality
  • Grade produce before selling: separate A, B, and C grades for better market prices
  • Maintain post-harvest records for traceability and insurance claims

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Day temperatures: 28-32°C; nights 12-16°C; pleasant weather ideal for Rabi crop establishment
  • November dew is heavy — favours yellow rust in wheat and fungal diseases in potato; increase scouting
  • First western disturbances may bring light rain to North Gujarat; beneficial for mustard and gram
  • Cold wave risk in North Gujarat and Kutch by month-end; minimum temperatures may drop to 8-10°C
  • IMD forecast: monitor for western disturbance predictions and cold wave warnings for frost protection

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PM-KISAN installment verification: ensure Aadhaar seeding is complete before 15th of the month
  • Soil Health Card scheme: collect updated cards from local Krushi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or ATMA office
  • PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana) enrollment: last date for Kharif crops is usually 31st July; for Rabi crops, 15th December
  • Gujarat State Farm Machinery Scheme: subsidy applications for tractor/power tiller due at district agriculture office
  • Drip irrigation subsidy under GGRC (Gujarat Green Revolution Company): submit estimates before month-end
  • Horticulture Mission for North East & Himalayan States (HMNEH) — nursery subsidy claims
  • Contact your nearest VLE (Village Level Entrepreneur) for eNAM registration and online mandi selling
