COLD WAVE AND FROST PROTECTION FOR GUJARAT FARMERS
====================================================

OVERVIEW
Gujarat experiences cold waves primarily from December to February, especially in 
North Gujarat (Banaskantha, Patan, Mehsana) and Saurashtra. Night temperatures can 
drop to 4-8°C, causing frost damage to vegetables, spices, and winter crops.

CROPS MOST VULNERABLE TO FROST IN GUJARAT
- Vegetables: tomato, brinjal, chilli, capsicum, potato
- Spices: cumin (jeera), fennel (saunf), coriander
- Fruits: papaya, banana, guava (young plants)
- Flowers: marigold, chrysanthemum
- Pulses: chickpea at flowering stage

COLD WAVE WARNING SIGNS
- IMD issues yellow/orange/red alerts for cold wave conditions
- Temperature 4.5°C or more below normal = cold wave
- Temperature below 10°C in plains = cold wave for crops
- Clear skies + low wind + low humidity at night = frost risk

PROTECTIVE MEASURES

Before Cold Wave
- Monitor IMD daily forecasts (mausam.imd.gov.in or Meghdoot app)
- Avoid irrigation just before predicted cold night (wet soil holds more heat)
- Apply potassium-rich fertiliser (SOP) 2 weeks before winter to harden plants
- Do not apply nitrogen just before cold wave (soft growth more vulnerable)

During Cold Wave
1. Overhead Irrigation (Sprinkling)
   - Light irrigation at night creates a thin ice layer that protects plant tissue
   - Start sprinklers when temperature drops near 2°C
   - Effective for cumin, coriander, vegetable nurseries

2. Smoke/Dhuan Method
   - Burn crop residue, dry leaves, or biomass on windward side of field
   - Creates smoke canopy trapping ground heat
   - Start 2-3 hours before sunrise (coldest period)
   - Cost-effective for large fields

3. Covering Small Plants
   - Use dry grass, straw, or agro-net to cover nursery beds
   - Polythene tunnels for high-value vegetables
   - Remove covers after sunrise to prevent overheating

4. Wind Barriers
   - Erect temporary windbreaks using dry grass bundles or agro-shade nets
   - Reduces wind chill effect on crops

5. Mulching
   - Apply 5-8 cm straw or sugarcane trash as mulch
   - Insulates soil and root zone

Post Frost Damage Management
- Do not prune frost-damaged parts immediately; wait for new growth to assess damage
- Apply 0.5% potassium nitrate spray to help recovery
- Apply urea spray (2%) after recovery begins to stimulate new growth
- Damaged cumin/coriander: re-sowing may be needed if >50% plants affected

DISTRICT-WISE RISK ZONES IN GUJARAT
- High Risk: Banaskantha, Patan, Mehsana, Surendranagar, Rajkot (Jan-Feb)
- Medium Risk: Gandhinagar, Sabarkantha, Ahmedabad, Amreli
- Low Risk: South Gujarat (Surat, Navsari, Valsad), coastal areas

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
- Crop damage due to cold wave eligible under PM Fasal Bima Yojana
- Report crop loss to Talati or local agriculture officer within 72 hours
- Gujarat Kisan Sahay Yojana provides input subsidy for unseasonal damage
