================================================================================
GUJARAT FARMING CALENDAR — FEBRUARY (પોષ / મહા (Posh / Maha))
================================================================================

OVERVIEW:
February is a critical month for Gujarat agriculture. The state's diverse agro-climatic zones — Saurashtra, North Gujarat, South Gujarat, and Kutch — each have specific crop activities during this period. This calendar provides detailed, week-by-week guidance for farmers.

CROPS IN FOCUS THIS MONTH:
  • Wheat (grain filling)
  • Gram (podding)
  • Mustard (ripening)
  • Potato (harvest)
  • Cumin (mid-season)
  • Mango (pre-flowering)

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION
================================================================================
  • Summer ploughing should be completed by now if not already done; deep ploughing (20-25 cm) improves water infiltration
  • For heavy black cotton soils, apply organic matter / FYM (Farm Yard Manure) 10-15 tonnes per hectare at least 2-3 weeks before sowing
  • Level the field using a leveller to ensure uniform irrigation and prevent waterlogging
  • In Saurashtra, apply gypsum 250 kg per hectare for sodic soil reclamation before sowing
  • Conduct soil testing: pH should be 6.5-7.5 for most crops; apply lime if pH < 6.0
  • Prepare raised beds (15-20 cm high) for vegetable crops to improve drainage during monsoon

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • NO main field sowing in February for most crops — mid-to-late Rabi season
  • Nursery sowing for summer vegetables (tomato, brinjal, chilli) in South Gujarat and Saurashtra
  • Late moong (green gram) summer sowing possible in South Gujarat until mid-February with assured irrigation
  • Mango orchards: no sowing; focus on orchard management and flower induction

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Wheat: critical irrigation at boot leaf/flag leaf stage (60-70 days); another at grain filling (80-90 days)
  • Wheat: final irrigation at dough stage (100-105 days) — do NOT irrigate after milk stage to avoid lodging
  • Gram: generally rainfed; one irrigation at pod filling if soil moisture is below 50% field capacity
  • Mustard: stop irrigation once 50% pods turn yellow; excess water at this stage causes black rot
  • Potato: irrigation every 7-10 days; reduce 2 weeks before harvest to harden skin and improve storage life
  • Cumin: light irrigation every 12-15 days; reduce irrigation frequency as crop approaches maturity

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Wheat: third nitrogen top dressing (if needed) at boot leaf stage — 30 kg urea per hectare only if crop is deficient
  • Wheat: foliar spray of 2% urea + 0.5% zinc sulphate at grain filling for better grain weight
  • Gram: apply 20 kg DAP per hectare at pod filling stage if leaves show yellowing
  • Mustard: no nitrogen application after flowering; excess N delays maturity and reduces oil content
  • Potato: final fertilizer application (20 kg urea) at tuber bulking stage (60 days); then stop all fertilization
  • Cumin: final top dressing of 15 kg urea at 70 days after sowing; no fertilizer after flowering begins

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Wheat: yellow rust peak month in Gujarat; scout every 5-7 days; spray Propiconazole if lesions appear
  • Wheat: Karnal bunt (Tilletia indica) — remove and destroy infected heads; apply Tebuconazole 1ml per litre at boot stage
  • Gram: pod borer (Helicoverpa) — spray NSKE 5% or Bacillus thuringiensis (Bt) 1g per litre; chemical: Indoxacarb 0.5ml/litre
  • Mustard: aphids and painted bug — spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml per litre if aphids exceed 5 per terminal shoot
  • Potato: late blight (Phytophthora infestans) — spray Metalaxyl+Mancozeb 2.5g per litre if humid conditions persist
  • Mango: hopper and mealy bug monitoring — spray Imidacloprid 0.3ml per litre before flower bud break

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • Potato: main harvest season in Saurashtra and North Gujarat; harvest when tops turn yellow and begin to dry
  • Mustard: harvest when 75% pods turn yellow-brown; cut with sickle and stack for 5-7 days before threshing
  • Early wheat varieties (e.g., GW-273) may be ready for harvest in last week of February in South Gujarat
  • Green gram (moong): harvest when 80% pods turn black; do not delay — shattering losses increase after maturity
  • Mango: begin harvesting fallen or early ripening fruits from Hapus orchards in South Gujarat

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • Clean and dry harvested produce immediately; moisture content should be reduced to safe levels (8-10% for grains, 10-12% for oilseeds)
  • Use tarpaulins or cemented drying yards to avoid aflatoxin contamination in groundnut and cotton
  • Store in properly ventilated godowns; use neem leaves or safe storage pesticides
  • For cotton, ginning should be done within 15 days of picking to preserve fibre quality
  • Grade produce before selling: separate A, B, and C grades for better market prices
  • Maintain post-harvest records for traceability and insurance claims

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Day temperatures rise to 28-35°C; night temperatures 12-18°C; transition from winter to pre-summer
  • Unseasonal rain and hail in February can cause severe damage to standing wheat, mustard, and gram crops
  • Dew formation increases — favours fungal diseases in wheat and potato; plan preventive sprays accordingly
  • Wind speeds increase in Saurashtra — can cause lodging in tall wheat varieties; avoid excess irrigation
  • IMD forecast: watch for western disturbance predictions — rain and hailstorm warnings require emergency measures

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PM-KISAN installment verification: ensure Aadhaar seeding is complete before 15th of the month
  • Soil Health Card scheme: collect updated cards from local Krushi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or ATMA office
  • PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana) enrollment: last date for Kharif crops is usually 31st July; for Rabi crops, 15th December
  • Gujarat State Farm Machinery Scheme: subsidy applications for tractor/power tiller due at district agriculture office
  • Drip irrigation subsidy under GGRC (Gujarat Green Revolution Company): submit estimates before month-end
  • Horticulture Mission for North East & Himalayan States (HMNEH) — nursery subsidy claims
  • Contact your nearest VLE (Village Level Entrepreneur) for eNAM registration and online mandi selling
