================================================================================
WHEAT CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — JANUARY
================================================================================

CROP: Wheat
MONTH: January
PRIMARY REGION: North Gujarat, Saurashtra, Kutch
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR JANUARY
================================================================================
  • Light hoeing between rows improves aeration and controls late-emerging weeds
  • Do NOT deep cultivate — wheat roots are shallow and easily damaged at this stage
  • In waterlogged fields, open drainage channels and aerate soil by shallow cultivation
  • Maintain soil pH at 6.5-7.5; apply lime if pH < 6.0
  • Ensure good drainage — waterlogging at tillering stage reduces tiller survival

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • NO sowing in January — too late for Rabi wheat; yield potential is severely reduced
  • Focus on crop management: irrigation, fertilizer, and pest control for established crop
  • If gaps are severe, no replanting is recommended at this stage
  • Remove weeds and waterlogged plants; maintain healthy plant population

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Second irrigation at tillering stage (40-45 days after sowing); apply 50-60mm water
  • Third irrigation at boot leaf/flag leaf stage (60-70 days); apply 60mm water
  • In North Gujarat, check-basin or flood irrigation is common
  • Avoid irrigation during cold wave/frost events; irrigate during daytime in winter
  • If frost is predicted, light irrigation in evening can provide some protection (latent heat release)

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Second nitrogen top dressing (40 kg urea per hectare) at tillering (40-45 days after sowing)
  • Third nitrogen top dressing (30 kg urea per hectare) at boot leaf stage (60-70 days) if needed
  • Foliar spray of 2% urea + 0.5% zinc sulphate at tillering if crop shows deficiency symptoms
  • If crop is too vigorous and dark green, reduce nitrogen dose to avoid lodging
  • No potash top dressing needed if basal potash was applied
  • Plan for grain filling stage irrigation and fertilizer in February

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Yellow rust (Puccinia striiformis): peak risk month in North Gujarat; scout every 5-7 days
  • If yellow rust appears (yellow-orange streaks on leaves), spray Propiconazole 1ml per litre immediately
  • Alert agriculture department if yellow rust is detected — it is a quarantine disease in Gujarat
  • Aphids: if colonies exceed 10 per tiller, spray Dimethoate 2ml per litre
  • Karnal bunt (Tilletia indica): remove and destroy infected heads; apply Tebuconazole 1ml per litre at boot stage
  • Termite: if damage observed, drench with Chlorpyriphos 2ml per litre along affected rows

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO harvest in January — wheat is in tillering to boot leaf stage (40-70 days)
  • Early sown bajra (fodder) may be harvested for green fodder in South Gujarat
  • Continue monitoring crop growth and pest populations
  • Record keeping: document tillering and boot leaf dates for harvest planning (March-April)

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations in January
  • Prepare storage facilities and threshing equipment for March-April harvest
  • Contact grain market or FCI procurement centre for price information
  • Arrange for combine harvester or labour in advance for March-April peak season

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • January is the coldest month in Gujarat; minimum temperatures 5-10°C in North Gujarat and Kutch
  • Frost risk in North Gujarat (Banaskantha, Patan, Mehsana) and Kutch — protect wheat with light irrigation or smoke
  • Day temperatures: 25-30°C; nights 8-15°C; ideal for wheat growth
  • Watch for unseasonal rain — January rain can cause lodging in wheat and fungal diseases
  • IMD Cold Wave warnings: if minimum temperature forecast is 4°C or below for 2+ days, activate frost protection measures

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY: if crop damage from frost or pest, notify within 72 hours and file claim
  • Crop damage survey: contact local agriculture officer for assessment
  • Wheat seed subsidy: no new claims in January; focus on crop protection
  • Kisan Credit Card: avail credit for fertilizer and irrigation costs
  • PM-KISAN: verify installment eligibility and Aadhaar seeding status
