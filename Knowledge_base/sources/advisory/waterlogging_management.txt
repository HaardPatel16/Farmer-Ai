Waterlogging management in Gujarat farms: Waterlogging (excess water standing in fields) is a major problem during heavy monsoon in Gujarat especially in black cotton soils, low-lying areas, and canal command areas. Damage: root asphyxiation begins within 24-48 hours; cotton is most sensitive (3-4 days causes significant damage); groundnut, chilli, potato cannot tolerate any standing water. Prevention: (1) Raise field bunds (do not make water-tight bunds — water needs to drain somewhere). (2) Open drainage channels along field borders sloping to outlet. (3) Provide field drains every 30-40 metres for large fields. (4) Land leveling so water does not pond in low spots. (5) Deep tillage (subsoil plowing) breaks hardpan and improves drainage in black soils. Emergency action when waterlogged: (1) Open outlets and drain water as fast as possible. (2) After draining, do not enter field with heavy machinery — will create compaction. (3) If plants are wilting even after drainage, check for root damage; apply potassium 50kg per hectare as top dressing to boost recovery. (4) Spray potassium nitrate 1% foliar if severe stress. Waterlogging-tolerant crops: rice, sugarcane can withstand 7+ days; maize tolerates 3-4 days; wheat 2-3 days; all others very sensitive.
