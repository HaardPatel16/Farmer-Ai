================================================================================
COTTON CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — JULY
================================================================================

CROP: Cotton
MONTH: July
PRIMARY REGION: Saurashtra, North Gujarat, South Gujarat
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR JULY
================================================================================
  • If sowing was delayed to early July, complete soil preparation immediately
  • For fields with waterlogging in June, apply gypsum 250 kg per hectare to improve soil structure
  • In waterlogged fields, open additional drainage channels and aerate soil by shallow cultivation
  • Apply organic matter / FYM if not applied before sowing
  • Maintain soil pH at 6.5-7.5; acidic soils require lime application

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Gap filling within first 15 days using extra seeds kept at sowing time
  • Thinning to one plant per hill at 15-20 days where germination is excess
  • First weeding at 20-25 days — critical as competition in first 30 days heavily impacts final yield
  • Second weeding at 40-45 days; maintain weed-free field until canopy closure
  • If sowing is very late (after 15 July), consider short-duration varieties (e.g., GCH-7)

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Generally rainfed in July; one irrigation if dry spell exceeds 10-15 days during vegetative stage
  • First irrigation at 15-20 days if no rain after sowing
  • Maintain soil moisture at 60-70% field capacity
  • In South Gujarat irrigated tracts, schedule drip irrigation every 3-4 days (8-10 mm)
  • Ensure drainage after heavy rain — waterlogging for >24 hours causes root rot and sudden wilt

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • First nitrogen top dressing (40-50 kg urea per hectare) at 30-35 days when plants are knee-high
  • If basal DAP was not applied, apply 50 kg DAP per hectare at first weeding (20-25 days)
  • Micronutrient spray: 0.5% zinc sulphate + 0.2% boron at 30 days if deficiency symptoms appear
  • No potassium top dressing needed if basal potash was applied
  • Plan for second nitrogen top dressing in August at boll formation stage

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Sucking pests: jassids, aphids, thrips — spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml per litre if damage exceeds 5% leaf area
  • Whitefly: monitor using yellow sticky traps; spray Buprofezin 1.5ml/litre if >5 whiteflies per leaf
  • Alternaria leaf spot: spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre if circular brown spots appear after rain
  • Set up pheromone traps for pink bollworm at 45 days — 5 traps per hectare
  • Monitor for American bollworm (Helicoverpa armigera) eggs on leaves and squares from 40 days
  • Root rot: if waterlogging occurs, drench affected plants with Copper oxychloride 2.5g per litre + Metalaxyl 2.5g/litre

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO harvest in July — cotton is in vegetative/early reproductive stage
  • Continue monitoring crop growth and pest populations
  • Record keeping: document sowing date, variety, fertilizer, and pest control measures for traceability

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations in July
  • Prepare spray equipment and calibrate nozzles for August peak pest season
  • Purchase required pesticides and store safely away from rain and sunlight
  • Arrange for labour during peak picking season (October-December)

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Peak monsoon month: heavy rainfall in Saurashtra and South Gujarat; 250-350mm average
  • Waterlogging risk highest in July; check drainage channels after every rain event
  • Humidity 70-90%; favourable for fungal diseases — increase scouting frequency
  • Day temperatures 28-32°C; nights 24-26°C; warm and humid
  • IMD warnings: watch for heavy rainfall alerts (100mm+ in 24 hours) and flood warnings

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY enrollment: complete by 31st July for Kharif cotton
  • Bt cotton seed subsidy: claim through authorized dealer with GST bill and Aadhaar
  • Drip irrigation subsidy: submit proposal to GGRC if not already done
  • Kisan Credit Card: avail fresh credit for pesticide and fertilizer purchases
  • Contact VLE for eNAM registration and online mandi selling
