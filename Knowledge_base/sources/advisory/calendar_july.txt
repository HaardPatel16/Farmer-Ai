================================================================================
GUJARAT FARMING CALENDAR — JULY (જેઠ / આષાઢ (Jeth / Ashadh))
================================================================================

OVERVIEW:
July is a critical month for Gujarat agriculture. The state's diverse agro-climatic zones — Saurashtra, North Gujarat, South Gujarat, and Kutch — each have specific crop activities during this period. This calendar provides detailed, week-by-week guidance for farmers.

CROPS IN FOCUS THIS MONTH:
  • Cotton (vegetative)
  • Groundnut (vegetative)
  • Bajra (tillering)
  • Paddy (transplanting)
  • Castor (early growth)
  • Monsoon active

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION
================================================================================
  • Summer ploughing should be completed by now if not already done; deep ploughing (20-25 cm) improves water infiltration
  • For heavy black cotton soils, apply organic matter / FYM (Farm Yard Manure) 10-15 tonnes per hectare at least 2-3 weeks before sowing
  • Level the field using a leveller to ensure uniform irrigation and prevent waterlogging
  • In Saurashtra, apply gypsum 250 kg per hectare for sodic soil reclamation before sowing
  • Conduct soil testing: pH should be 6.5-7.5 for most crops; apply lime if pH < 6.0
  • Prepare raised beds (15-20 cm high) for vegetable crops to improve drainage during monsoon

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Cotton: gap filling within first 15 days using extra seeds kept at sowing; thin to one plant per hill at 15-20 days
  • Groundnut: gap filling within 10 days if germination is poor; maintain plant population of 15-20 plants per sq metre
  • Bajra: gap filling at 10-15 days; thin to 45cm x 15cm spacing 15-20 days after germination
  • Paddy: transplanting in South Gujarat and parts of North Gujarat; transplant 25-30 day old seedlings
  • Castor: gap filling at 15 days; maintain spacing 120cm x 60cm for hybrid varieties
  • Late sowing of moong (green gram) as intercrop in cotton or standalone in Saurashtra until mid-July

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Cotton: generally rainfed; one irrigation if dry spell exceeds 10-15 days during vegetative stage
  • Groundnut: rainfed; if no rain for 10 days, provide 25mm irrigation at pegging initiation (30-40 days)
  • Bajra: rainfed; one protective irrigation if dry spell exceeds 15 days at tillering stage
  • Paddy: maintain 5 cm water layer after transplanting; increase to 10 cm at tillering stage
  • Castor: rainfed; one irrigation at 30-40 days if dry spell occurs during vegetative growth
  • All crops: ensure drainage channels are functional — July receives heaviest rainfall in Gujarat

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Cotton: first nitrogen top dressing (40-50 kg urea per hectare) at 30-35 days when plants are knee-high
  • Groundnut: no nitrogen top dressing — groundnut fixes nitrogen; apply gypsum 250 kg/ha at flowering (not yet)
  • Bajra: first nitrogen top dressing (40 kg urea per hectare) at 25-30 days after sowing
  • Paddy: apply basal dose in main field at transplanting — DAP 100 kg + urea 50 kg per hectare
  • Paddy: first top dressing (50 kg urea per hectare) at tillering stage (20-25 days after transplanting)
  • Castor: first nitrogen top dressing (40 kg urea per hectare) at 30 days after sowing

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Cotton: jassids, aphids, thrips — spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml per litre if sucking pest damage exceeds economic threshold
  • Cotton: Alternaria leaf spot — spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre if circular brown spots appear
  • Groundnut: leaf miner — monitor from 30 days; spray if damage exceeds 5% leaf area
  • Groundnut: collar rot (Aspergillus niger) — drench with Carbendazim 1g per litre if seedlings wilt
  • Bajra: downy mildew (green ear) — if pale green streaks appear, rogue out affected plants immediately
  • Paddy: stem borer (dead heart) — apply Carbofuran 3G 20 kg/ha if >5% dead heart observed
  • Paddy: leaf blast — spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre if diamond-shaped lesions appear on leaves

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO main crop harvest in July — all Kharif crops are in vegetative or early reproductive stage
  • Pre-monsoon summer crops: complete any remaining harvest and storage before fungal contamination
  • Mango: harvest of late varieties (Banganapalli) continues in South Gujarat
  • Vegetables: harvest of early-planted Kharif vegetables (okra, cucumber) may begin in South Gujarat
  • Green fodder: harvest and store surplus fodder for dry season; silage preparation can begin

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • Clean and dry harvested produce immediately; moisture content should be reduced to safe levels (8-10% for grains, 10-12% for oilseeds)
  • Use tarpaulins or cemented drying yards to avoid aflatoxin contamination in groundnut and cotton
  • Store in properly ventilated godowns; use neem leaves or safe storage pesticides
  • For cotton, ginning should be done within 15 days of picking to preserve fibre quality
  • Grade produce before selling: separate A, B, and C grades for better market prices
  • Maintain post-harvest records for traceability and insurance claims

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Peak monsoon month in Gujarat: average rainfall 250-400mm; heavy rainfall in South Gujarat and Saurashtra
  • Waterlogging risk is highest in July; ensure all drainage channels are functional
  • Day temperatures: 28-32°C; high humidity (70-90%); favourable for fungal diseases
  • Flood risk in low-lying areas (Bharuch, Narmada, Surat, Valsad); monitor flood warnings
  • IMD Heavy Rainfall warnings: if 24-hour rainfall exceeds 100mm, check for waterlogging and crop damage

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PM-KISAN installment verification: ensure Aadhaar seeding is complete before 15th of the month
  • Soil Health Card scheme: collect updated cards from local Krushi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or ATMA office
  • PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana) enrollment: last date for Kharif crops is usually 31st July; for Rabi crops, 15th December
  • Gujarat State Farm Machinery Scheme: subsidy applications for tractor/power tiller due at district agriculture office
  • Drip irrigation subsidy under GGRC (Gujarat Green Revolution Company): submit estimates before month-end
  • Horticulture Mission for North East & Himalayan States (HMNEH) — nursery subsidy claims
  • Contact your nearest VLE (Village Level Entrepreneur) for eNAM registration and online mandi selling
