================================================================================
BAJRA (PEARL MILLET) CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — AUGUST
================================================================================

CROP: Bajra (Pearl Millet)
MONTH: August
PRIMARY REGION: Saurashtra, Kutch, North Gujarat
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR AUGUST
================================================================================
  • Light hoeing between rows improves aeration and controls late-emerging weeds
  • Do NOT deep cultivate — roots are established and shallow cultivation is sufficient
  • In waterlogged fields, open drainage channels and aerate soil by shallow inter-culture
  • Maintain soil pH at 6.0-7.5; apply lime if pH < 5.5
  • Bajra tolerates poor soils but responds to good drainage and aeration

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • NO sowing in August — too late for profitable bajra crop in Gujarat
  • Focus on crop management: weed control, pest scouting, and fertilizer application
  • If gaps are severe, no replanting is recommended at this stage
  • Remove waterlogged and dead plants; maintain healthy plant population

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Generally rainfed; one irrigation at heading stage (50-60 days) if dry spell exceeds 15 days
  • Critical irrigation at grain filling stage (60-75 days) if no rain for 10+ days
  • In North Gujarat, check-basin irrigation can be used for bajra at heading stage
  • Ensure drainage after heavy rain — prolonged flooding causes root rot and lodging
  • Remove excess water within 24 hours of flooding

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Second nitrogen top dressing (30-40 kg urea per hectare) at tillering stage (35-45 days)
  • If first top dressing was missed, apply 50 kg urea per hectare at 40-45 days (compensatory dose)
  • No potash needed for bajra in most Gujarat soils
  • If crop shows yellowing, foliar spray of 2% urea at heading stage
  • No fertilizer application during heavy rain — nutrients will leach; wait for rain to stop

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Stem borer: monitor for dead heart at 20-30 days; if missed earlier, check now and apply Carbofuran 3G if >5%
  • Downy mildew (green ear): if pale green streaks appear, rogue out and destroy affected plants immediately
  • Ergot (Claviceps fusiformis): at heading stage, watch for black sticky honeydew; spray Mancozeb 2.5g/litre immediately
  • At heading stage, watch for ergot hard sclerotia replacing grains — these are toxic; do NOT keep seed from affected fields
  • Aphids: if colonies exceed 10 per plant, spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml per litre
  • Rust (Puccinia penniseti): if orange pustules appear on leaves, spray Propiconazole 1ml per litre

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • Early bajra (sown in May) may be ready for harvest in South Gujarat by month-end
  • Main Kharif bajra (sown in June-July) is at tillering to heading stage (40-70 days)
  • Continue monitoring crop growth and pest populations
  • Record keeping: document heading date for harvest planning (75-90 days after sowing)

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations for main Kharif bajra in August
  • Early bajra: if harvested, cut and stack for field drying; thresh when moisture is 15-18%
  • Prepare storage facilities and threshing equipment for September-October harvest
  • Contact fodder traders or grain market for price information

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • August is the wettest month in Gujarat: 300-450mm average rainfall
  • Waterlogging and flood risk remains high; prioritize drainage over irrigation
  • Humidity 75-95%; favourable for fungal diseases — increase scouting to twice per week
  • Day temperatures 28-32°C; nights 24-26°C; warm and humid
  • IMD warnings: heavy rainfall alerts (100mm+ in 24 hours) and flood warnings

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY: if crop damage from waterlogging or pest, notify within 72 hours and file claim
  • Crop damage survey: contact local agriculture officer for assessment
  • Kisan Credit Card: avail credit for pesticide and labour costs
  • Fodder development scheme: contact district animal husbandry department
  • Contact KVK for pest identification and spray recommendations
