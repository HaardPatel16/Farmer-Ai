IMD WEATHER FORECAST GUIDE FOR GUJARAT FARMERS
================================================

OVERVIEW
The India Meteorological Department (IMD) provides weather forecasts that are crucial for 
farm planning in Gujarat. Understanding how to read and act on IMD forecasts helps farmers 
make timely decisions on sowing, irrigation, spraying, and harvesting.

IMD FORECAST TYPES

1. Short Range Forecast (1-3 days)
   - Most accurate; used for immediate farm operations
   - Available daily on mausam.imd.gov.in
   - Includes: temperature, rainfall, wind speed, humidity

2. Medium Range Forecast (4-7 days)
   - Good for planning irrigation, harvesting, and spraying schedules
   - Updated daily; accuracy reduces after day 5
   - Source: MFRS (Medium Range Forecast System), IMD Pune

3. Extended Range Forecast (8-30 days)
   - Useful for crop calendar planning
   - Released weekly by IMD
   - Less accurate but guides Kharif/Rabi sowing decisions

4. Seasonal Forecast (3-6 months)
   - Released before monsoon (April-May) and before Rabi (September)
   - Indicates whether monsoon will be normal, above normal, or deficient
   - Critical for crop selection and insurance planning

IMD WARNING COLOR CODES
- Green (No warning): Normal weather; no precautions needed
- Yellow (Watch): Potentially bad weather in 24-48 hours; stay updated
- Orange (Alert): Severe weather likely; prepare precautions
- Red (Warning): Extremely severe weather; take immediate action

RAINFALL CATEGORY TERMINOLOGY
- No Rain: 0 mm
- Light Rain: 0.1 to 15.5 mm
- Moderate Rain: 15.6 to 64.4 mm
- Heavy Rain: 64.5 to 115.5 mm
- Very Heavy Rain: 115.6 to 204.4 mm
- Extremely Heavy Rain: Above 204.4 mm

HOW TO ACCESS IMD FORECASTS

Online
- mausam.imd.gov.in — main portal
- Internal.imd.gov.in/pages/fctmap.php — district forecasts
- IMD Ahmedabad regional center: ahmedabad.imd.gov.in

Mobile Apps (Free, Android)
- Meghdoot App — dedicated agromet advisory; gives crop-specific advice
- Damini App — real-time lightning alerts with map (very useful during monsoon)
- mKisan App (by Govt of India) — weather + crop advisory combined
- IFFCO Kisan App — includes IMD weather for farmers

SMS/Toll-Free Services
- mKisan portal: farmers can register for SMS alerts
- Dial 1800-180-1551 (toll-free) for recorded weather updates

AGROMET ADVISORY SERVICES (AAS)
- IMD issues Agromet advisories twice a week (Tuesday and Friday)
- Covers: crop-specific advice based on current and forecast weather
- Available for all 33 districts of Gujarat
- Distributed through KVKs, agriculture department, and mobile apps

HOW TO READ AN AGROMET ADVISORY
Example Advisory Format:
  District: Rajkot | Date: 15 June 2025
  Forecast: Heavy rain expected on 17-18 June
  Advisory: 
  - Postpone irrigation for groundnut for next 4 days
  - Do not spray pesticides on 17-18 June
  - Harvest mature groundnut before 17 June if possible
  - Drain waterlogged fields immediately after rain

PRACTICAL TIPS FOR FARMERS
- Check forecast every morning before starting field work
- Never spray pesticides if rain is forecast within 24 hours
- Harvest ripe crops before predicted heavy rain
- Schedule chemical fertiliser application on clear days only
- Use monsoon onset forecast to plan nursery preparation and sowing
- In North Gujarat, check cold wave alerts from November to February
