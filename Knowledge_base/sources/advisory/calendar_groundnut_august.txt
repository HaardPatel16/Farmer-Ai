================================================================================
GROUNDNUT CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — AUGUST
================================================================================

CROP: Groundnut
MONTH: August
PRIMARY REGION: Saurashtra, Kutch, North Gujarat
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR AUGUST
================================================================================
  • If waterlogging occurred in July, apply gypsum 250 kg per hectare to reclaim sodic patches
  • Light inter-culture with hand hoe between rows improves aeration and soil temperature
  • Do NOT deep cultivate — pegs are entering soil and pods are forming; any disturbance reduces yield
  • Maintain drainage channels; August floods can cause aflatoxin contamination
  • In waterlogged fields, remove excess water within 24 hours to prevent pod rot

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • NO sowing in August — too late for profitable groundnut crop in Gujarat
  • Focus on crop management: weed control, pest scouting, and gypsum application
  • If gaps are severe, no replanting is recommended at this stage
  • Remove waterlogged and dead plants; maintain healthy plant population

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Critical irrigation at flowering and pegging stage (40-60 days); if no rain for 10 days, apply 25mm
  • Maintain soil moisture at 60-70% field capacity during pod formation
  • In Saurashtra, sprinkler irrigation is ideal for groundnut at this stage
  • Avoid waterlogging — August floods cause pod rot and aflatoxin contamination
  • Remove excess water within 24 hours of flooding; aerate soil if possible

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Apply gypsum 250-400 kg per hectare at flowering stage (40-50 days) — THIS IS THE CRITICAL APPLICATION
  • Gypsum provides calcium for pod development and improves pod filling
  • Spread gypsum between rows when soil is moist; do NOT apply during heavy rain
  • NO nitrogen application — groundnut fixes its own nitrogen
  • If micronutrient deficiency was observed earlier, apply 0.2% borax foliar spray at flowering

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Leaf spot (Cercospora and Alternaria): spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre if spots appear after rain
  • White grub: if damage is observed, drench with Chlorpyriphos 2ml per litre around affected plants
  • Aphids: if colonies exceed 10 per plant, spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml per litre
  • Stem rot (Sclerotium rolfsii): if white fungal growth appears at base, drench with Carbendazim 1g/litre
  • Tikka leaf spot: if infection is severe, spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre + Carbendazim 1g per litre
  • Rust (Puccinia arachidis): if orange pustules appear, spray Propiconazole 1ml per litre immediately

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO harvest in August — groundnut is in flowering and pod formation stage (40-80 days)
  • Continue monitoring crop growth and pest populations
  • Record keeping: document flowering date for harvest planning (90-110 days after sowing)
  • Prepare harvesting equipment and storage facilities for September-October

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations in August
  • Prepare drying yards, threshers, and storage godowns for September-October harvest
  • Clean and repair digging equipment; arrange for transport
  • Contact oil mills or market yards for price information and procurement schedules

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • August is the wettest month in Gujarat: 300-450mm average rainfall
  • Waterlogging and flood risk remains high; prioritize drainage over irrigation
  • Humidity 75-95%; favourable for fungal diseases — increase scouting to twice per week
  • Day temperatures 28-32°C; nights 24-26°C; warm and humid
  • IMD warnings: heavy rainfall alerts (100mm+ in 24 hours) and flood warnings for Saurashtra

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY: if crop damage from waterlogging or pest, notify within 72 hours and file claim
  • Crop damage survey: contact local agriculture officer for assessment
  • Groundnut seed subsidy: no new claims in August; focus on crop protection
  • Kisan Credit Card: avail credit for pesticide and labour costs
  • Contact KVK for pest identification and spray recommendations
