================================================================================
WHEAT CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — MARCH
================================================================================

CROP: Wheat
MONTH: March
PRIMARY REGION: North Gujarat, Saurashtra, Kutch
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR MARCH
================================================================================
  • Light hoeing between rows NOT recommended at this stage — roots are deep and plants are tall
  • If lodging occurs due to wind or rain, prop up plants with stakes or support if possible
  • In waterlogged fields, provide drainage immediately to prevent root rot and lodging
  • Maintain soil pH at 6.5-7.5; apply lime if pH < 6.0
  • Ensure good drainage — waterlogging at grain filling stage causes grain discolouration and quality loss

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • NO sowing in March — too late for Rabi wheat and too early for summer wheat
  • Focus on harvest preparation and management of standing crop
  • If gaps are severe, no replanting is recommended at this stage
  • Remove weeds and lodged plants; maintain healthy plant population

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Fourth irrigation at grain filling stage (80-90 days after sowing); apply 50mm water
  • Fifth irrigation at dough stage (100-105 days) if needed; do NOT irrigate after milk stage
  • Stop all irrigation 10-15 days before harvest to allow field to dry for combine operation
  • In North Gujarat, check-basin or flood irrigation is common
  • Avoid irrigation during hot dry winds (loo); irrigate in evening if possible

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • NO nitrogen application after boot leaf stage — excess nitrogen causes lodging and delays maturity
  • Foliar spray of 2% urea + 0.5% potassium sulphate at grain filling for better grain weight and quality
  • If crop shows severe deficiency, apply 20 kg urea per hectare at grain filling stage only
  • No potash top dressing needed if basal potash was applied
  • Plan for harvest and post-harvest management; record fertilizer usage for documentation

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Black rust (Puccinia graminis): if black pustules appear on leaves and stems, spray Propiconazole 1ml per litre
  • Termite: check field margins for termite damage; apply Chlorpyriphos 2ml per litre if found
  • Aphids: if colonies exceed 10 per tiller, spray Dimethoate 2ml per litre (observe PHI before harvest)
  • Karnal bunt (Tilletia indica): if partially infected grains observed, remove and destroy infected heads
  • Flag smut: if black streaks appear on flag leaf, remove and destroy affected plants
  • Do NOT spray chemicals close to harvest; observe pre-harvest intervals (PHI) for all chemicals

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • Main harvest begins in Saurashtra (early varieties) by mid-March and extends to North Gujarat by month-end
  • Harvest when grain moisture is 18-20% (dough stage); use combine harvester or manual sickle cutting
  • If using combine, ensure field is dry and firm to prevent machine damage and soil compaction
  • Stack cut wheat in field for 3-5 days for field drying; thresh when moisture is 15-16%
  • Dry threshed grain to 12% moisture before storage; use cemented drying yards or mechanical dryers
  • Store in clean, dry, ventilated godowns; use safe storage pesticides or neem leaves

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • Dry grain to 12% moisture before storage; moisture above 14% causes fungal growth and aflatoxin
  • Clean grain by winnowing and grading; separate broken, shrivelled, and damaged grains
  • Store in gunny bags or metal bins; place on pallets (not directly on floor) to prevent moisture absorption
  • Fumigate storage area with aluminium phosphide if insect infestation is suspected
  • Maintain records of quantity, grade, and sale price for PMFBY and income documentation
  • Sell through eNAM or registered mandi for transparent pricing; contact FCI for MSP procurement

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Day temperatures rise sharply to 35-40°C; nights 18-22°C; summer begins in Gujarat
  • Hot dry winds (loo) may begin in second fortnight — can cause grain shrivelling; harvest early if predicted
  • Unseasonal rain in March is rare but can damage mature wheat; keep harvest equipment ready
  • Humidity drops significantly — good for harvest but increases irrigation demand for summer crops
  • IMD Heat Wave warnings: if day temperature forecast exceeds 40°C for 3+ days, activate heat protection measures

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY: if crop damage occurred, file claim with required documentation (photos, FIR, survey report)
  • Wheat MSP procurement: contact FCI or state procurement centre for MSP operations (usually April-May)
  • PM-KISAN: verify installment eligibility and Aadhaar seeding status
  • Kisan Credit Card: repay crop loan or renew for next season
  • Gujarat State Foodgrain Scheme: contact district supply office for market information
