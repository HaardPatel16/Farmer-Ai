================================================================================
WHEAT CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — DECEMBER
================================================================================

CROP: Wheat
MONTH: December
PRIMARY REGION: North Gujarat, Saurashtra, Kutch
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR DECEMBER
================================================================================
  • If sowing was delayed to early December, complete soil preparation immediately
  • In waterlogged fields, open drainage channels and aerate soil by shallow cultivation
  • Apply FYM 10-15 tonnes per hectare if not applied before sowing
  • Maintain soil pH at 6.5-7.5; apply lime if pH < 6.0
  • Ensure good drainage — wheat is sensitive to waterlogging; check drainage channels

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Late sowing possible until 10-15 December in South Gujarat; yield declines after 15 December
  • If sowing after 15 December, increase seed rate to 125-150 kg per hectare and use short-duration varieties
  • For normal sowing (November), crop is in germination/early vegetative stage
  • Gap filling NOT recommended for wheat; if germination is poor, consider re-sowing in small patches only
  • Focus on irrigation and pest management for established crop

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • First irrigation at CRI stage (20-25 days after sowing) — apply 50mm water; this is THE MOST CRITICAL irrigation
  • Second irrigation at tillering stage (40-45 days); apply 50-60mm water
  • In North Gujarat, check-basin or flood irrigation is common
  • Avoid irrigation during cold wave/frost events; irrigate during daytime in winter
  • If frost is predicted, light irrigation in evening can provide some protection (latent heat release)

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • First nitrogen top dressing (40-50 kg urea per hectare) at CRI stage (20-25 days after sowing)
  • If crop was sown late (December), increase first nitrogen dose to 50 kg urea per hectare
  • Second nitrogen top dressing (40 kg urea per hectare) at tillering (40-45 days)
  • Foliar spray of 2% urea + 0.5% zinc sulphate at CRI stage if crop shows deficiency symptoms
  • No potash top dressing needed if basal potash was applied
  • Plan for third nitrogen top dressing in January at boot leaf stage

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Yellow rust (Puccinia striiformis): peak risk month in North Gujarat; scout every 5-7 days
  • If yellow rust appears (yellow-orange streaks on leaves), spray Propiconazole 1ml per litre immediately
  • Alert agriculture department if yellow rust is detected — it is a quarantine disease in Gujarat
  • Aphids: if colonies exceed 10 per tiller, spray Dimethoate 2ml per litre
  • Termite: if damage observed, drench with Chlorpyriphos 2ml per litre along affected rows
  • Pink stem borer: if dead heart observed, remove and destroy affected plants; apply Carbofuran 3G if >5%

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO harvest in December — wheat is in early vegetative stage (CRI to tillering)
  • Continue monitoring crop growth and pest populations
  • Record keeping: document CRI and tillering dates for harvest planning (March-April)

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations in December
  • Prepare storage facilities and threshing equipment for March-April harvest
  • Contact grain market or FCI procurement centre for price information
  • Arrange for combine harvester or labour in advance for March-April peak season

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • December is the second coldest month in Gujarat; minimum temperatures 5-10°C in North Gujarat and Kutch
  • Frost risk in North Gujarat (Banaskantha, Patan, Mehsana) and Kutch — protect wheat with light irrigation or smoke
  • Day temperatures: 25-30°C; nights 8-14°C; ideal for wheat growth
  • Western disturbances may bring light rain and cloud cover to North Gujarat; beneficial for Rabi crops
  • IMD Cold Wave warnings: if minimum temperature forecast is 4°C or below for 2+ days, activate frost protection measures

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY enrollment: complete by 15th December for Rabi wheat; do NOT delay
  • Wheat seed subsidy: claim through authorized dealer with GST bill and Aadhaar
  • Soil Health Card: collect updated card from KVK or ATMA office
  • Kisan Credit Card: avail fresh credit for fertilizer and irrigation costs
  • PM-KISAN: verify installment eligibility and Aadhaar seeding status
