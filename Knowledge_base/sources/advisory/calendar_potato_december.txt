================================================================================
POTATO CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — DECEMBER
================================================================================

CROP: Potato
MONTH: December
PRIMARY REGION: Saurashtra, North Gujarat
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR DECEMBER
================================================================================
  • Light hoeing between ridges improves aeration and controls weeds
  • Do NOT deep cultivate — potato tubers are shallow and easily damaged at this stage
  • In waterlogged fields, open drainage channels and aerate soil by shallow inter-culture
  • Maintain soil pH at 5.5-6.5; apply lime if pH < 5.5
  • Ensure good drainage — waterlogging at tuber formation stage causes tuber rot and quality loss

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Late sowing possible until mid-December in South Gujarat; yield declines after 15 December
  • If sowing after 15 December, increase seed rate to 2,500-3,000 kg per hectare and use short-duration varieties
  • For normal sowing (November), crop is in germination/early vegetative stage
  • Gap filling NOT recommended for potato; if germination is poor, consider re-sowing in small patches only
  • Focus on irrigation, fertilizer, and pest management for established crop

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • First irrigation at stolon formation (30 days after sowing); apply 50mm water
  • Second irrigation at tuber initiation (45 days after sowing); apply 50mm water
  • Third irrigation at tuber bulking stage (60 days after sowing); apply 50mm water
  • Maintain soil moisture at 80% field capacity for optimal tuber development
  • Avoid over-irrigation — waterlogging causes tuber rot and hollow heart
  • In North Gujarat, sprinkler irrigation can be used for uniform moisture distribution

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • First top dressing (50 kg urea per hectare) at stolon formation (30 days after sowing)
  • Second top dressing (50 kg urea per hectare) at tuber initiation (45 days after sowing)
  • Third top dressing (20 kg urea per hectare) at tuber bulking stage (60 days after sowing)
  • If crop shows yellowing, foliar spray of 2% urea + 0.5% zinc sulphate at tuber initiation
  • No potash top dressing needed if basal potash was applied
  • Plan for harvest and post-harvest management; record fertilizer usage for documentation

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Early blight (Alternaria solani): if brown spots with concentric rings appear, spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre
  • Late blight (Phytophthora infestans): if water-soaked lesions appear on leaves, spray Metalaxyl+Mancozeb 2.5g per litre immediately
  • Potato tuber moth: if larvae found in tubers, spray Indoxacarb 0.5ml per litre or use pheromone traps
  • Aphids: if colonies exceed 10 per plant, spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml per litre
  • White grubs: if damage observed, drench with Chlorpyriphos 2ml per litre
  • Monitor every 7-10 days; early blight and late blight can cause total crop loss if not controlled

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO harvest in December — potato is in stolon formation to tuber initiation stage (30-60 days)
  • Continue monitoring crop growth and pest populations
  • Record keeping: document stolon formation and tuber initiation dates for harvest planning (February-March)
  • Prepare storage facilities and grading equipment for February-March harvest

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations in December
  • Prepare cold storage facilities for February-March harvest
  • Contact potato traders, chip manufacturers, or market yards for price information
  • Potato is a high-value crop; maintain quality standards for seed, table, and processing markets

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • December is the second coldest month in Gujarat; minimum temperatures 5-10°C in North Gujarat and Kutch
  • Frost risk in North Gujarat (Banaskantha, Patan, Mehsana) and Kutch — protect potato with light irrigation or mulch
  • Day temperatures: 25-30°C; nights 8-14°C; ideal for potato tuber development
  • Western disturbances may bring light rain and cloud cover to North Gujarat; beneficial for potato growth
  • IMD Cold Wave warnings: if minimum temperature forecast is 4°C or below for 2+ days, activate frost protection measures

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY enrollment: complete by 15th December for Rabi potato; do NOT delay
  • Potato seed subsidy: claim through authorized dealer with GST bill and Aadhaar
  • Cold storage subsidy: contact district horticulture office for cold storage construction subsidy
  • Soil Health Card: collect updated card from KVK or ATMA office
  • Kisan Credit Card: avail fresh credit for fertilizer and irrigation costs
  • PM-KISAN: verify installment eligibility and Aadhaar seeding status
