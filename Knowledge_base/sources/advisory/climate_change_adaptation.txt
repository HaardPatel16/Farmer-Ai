CLIMATE CHANGE ADAPTATION FOR GUJARAT FARMERS
==============================================

OVERVIEW
Gujarat farmers face increasing climate risks including erratic monsoons, rising temperatures, 
prolonged dry spells, and extreme weather events. Adapting farming practices helps protect 
yields and livelihoods.

KEY CHALLENGES IN GUJARAT
- Monsoon onset delayed by 1-2 weeks compared to 1990s averages
- Summer temperatures in Saurashtra and North Gujarat crossing 45°C
- Unseasonal rains during Rabi harvest damaging wheat and cumin
- Coastal Saurashtra facing increased salinity due to sea intrusion
- Groundwater depletion in Mehsana, Banaskantha, and Patan districts

ADAPTATION STRATEGIES

1. Crop Diversification
   - Shift from single water-intensive crops to mixed cropping
   - Include drought-tolerant varieties: GG-20 groundnut, GHB-719 pearl millet
   - Introduce short-duration varieties to avoid terminal drought
   - Intercrop cotton with moong to reduce risk

2. Water Management
   - Adopt drip and sprinkler irrigation (50-70% water saving)
   - Harvest rainwater through farm ponds (Gujarat govt subsidy available)
   - Use mulching (straw or plastic) to reduce soil moisture loss
   - Shift irrigation to early morning or evening hours

3. Soil Health
   - Increase organic matter to improve water holding capacity
   - Use compost, vermicompost, and green manure crops like dhaincha
   - Avoid deep tillage during dry conditions to prevent moisture loss
   - Practice minimum tillage in moisture-stressed zones

4. Crop Calendar Adjustments
   - Delay Kharif sowing by 10-15 days if monsoon onset is late
   - Advance Rabi sowing to avoid heat stress during grain filling
   - Track IMD Agromet advisories for district-level guidance

5. Heat Stress Management (Livestock)
   - Provide shade structures and adequate drinking water
   - Reduce stocking density in peak summer
   - Supplement with mineral mixtures during heat waves

CLIMATE-SMART VARIETIES RECOMMENDED FOR GUJARAT
- Groundnut: GG-20, TG-37A (drought tolerant)
- Cotton: Bt hybrids with early maturity (<160 days)
- Wheat: GW-496 (heat tolerant for South Gujarat)
- Pearl Millet: GHB-719, GHB-744 (drought tolerant)
- Castor: GAUCH-1, GCH-7 (drought tolerant)

GOVERNMENT SUPPORT
- PM Fasal Bima Yojana (crop insurance against climate risks)
- Gujarat Kisan Sahay Yojana (compensation for unseasonal rain losses)
- Mukhyamantri Bagayat Vikas Mission (horticulture support)
- Soil Health Card scheme for adaptive fertiliser management
