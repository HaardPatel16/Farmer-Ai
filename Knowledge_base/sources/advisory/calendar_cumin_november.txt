================================================================================
CUMIN CROP CALENDAR — GUJARAT — NOVEMBER
================================================================================

CROP: Cumin
MONTH: November
PRIMARY REGION: North Gujarat, Saurashtra
DOCUMENT TYPE: Month-wise Activity Calendar

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION FOR NOVEMBER
================================================================================
  • Light to medium loamy soils ideal for cumin; avoid heavy clay soils
  • Soil pH should be 6.5-7.5; apply lime if pH < 6.0
  • Apply FYM 5-10 tonnes per hectare 2-3 weeks before sowing
  • Ensure excellent drainage — cumin is highly sensitive to waterlogging and root rot
  • In North Gujarat, raised beds (15-20 cm high) are recommended for cumin to improve drainage

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Sow mid-November to early December in North Gujarat and Saurashtra
  • Cool, dry weather essential for good germination; avoid sowing during humid or cloudy conditions
  • Seed rate: 10-12 kg per hectare; treat seeds with Carbendazim 2g + Streptocycline 1g per kg
  • Spacing: 25-30 cm between rows; 10-15 cm between plants
  • Sow at 2-3 cm depth; light soil coverage
  • Use certified varieties: Gujarat Cumin-1, GC-2, GC-3 for high yield and oil content

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Light irrigation immediately after sowing to ensure germination
  • Then irrigate every 10-12 days through the season; cumin needs consistent but light moisture
  • Avoid over-irrigation — waterlogging causes wilt and blight complex
  • In North Gujarat, drip irrigation is ideal for cumin; use 2-4 litre/hour emitters
  • Reduce irrigation frequency as crop approaches maturity (February-March)

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Basal dose at sowing: DAP 50 kg + potash 20 kg per hectare
  • First nitrogen top dressing (20 kg urea per hectare) at 30 days after sowing
  • Second nitrogen top dressing (15 kg urea per hectare) at 50 days if crop looks pale
  • Total nitrogen: 40-60 kg per hectare depending on soil fertility
  • Micronutrients: apply zinc sulphate 25 kg per hectare as basal if soil is deficient

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Wilt and blight complex (Fusarium + Alternaria): drench with Carbendazim 1g + Mancozeb 2.5g per litre if wilting observed
  • Powdery mildew (Erysiphe cichoracearum): if white powdery patches appear on leaves, spray Sulphur 2g per litre or Carbendazim 1g per litre
  • Aphids: if colonies exceed 10 per plant, spray Imidacloprid 0.5ml per litre
  • Thrips: if damage exceeds 5% leaf area, spray Fipronil 1.5ml per litre
  • Root rot: if waterlogging occurs, drench with Metalaxyl 2.5g + Mancozeb 2.5g per litre
  • Monitor every 7-10 days; cumin is a high-value crop and early pest detection is critical

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • NO harvest in November — cumin is just germinated or in very early growth stage
  • Continue monitoring germination and seedling establishment
  • Record keeping: document sowing date and variety for harvest planning (February-March)

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • NO post-harvest operations in November
  • Prepare storage facilities and drying yards for February-March harvest
  • Contact spice traders or market yards for price information and procurement schedules
  • Cumin is a high-value export crop; maintain quality standards for export certification

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Day temperatures: 28-32°C; nights 12-16°C; pleasant weather ideal for cumin germination
  • November dew is heavy — favours fungal diseases; increase scouting frequency
  • First western disturbances may bring light rain to North Gujarat; beneficial for establishment
  • Cold wave risk in North Gujarat by month-end; minimum temperatures may drop to 8-10°C
  • IMD forecast: monitor for western disturbance predictions and cold wave warnings

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PMFBY enrollment: complete by 15th December for Rabi cumin; visit PACS or bank branch
  • Cumin seed subsidy: purchase certified seeds from authorized dealers with bill and Aadhaar
  • Soil Health Card: collect updated card from KVK or ATMA office before fertilizer application
  • Kisan Credit Card (KCC): renew or apply for fresh credit before sowing season
  • PM-KISAN: verify installment eligibility and Aadhaar seeding status
