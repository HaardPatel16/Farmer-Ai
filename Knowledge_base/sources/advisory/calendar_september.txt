================================================================================
GUJARAT FARMING CALENDAR — SEPTEMBER (શ્રાવણ / ભાદરવો (Shravan / Bhadarvo))
================================================================================

OVERVIEW:
September is a critical month for Gujarat agriculture. The state's diverse agro-climatic zones — Saurashtra, North Gujarat, South Gujarat, and Kutch — each have specific crop activities during this period. This calendar provides detailed, week-by-week guidance for farmers.

CROPS IN FOCUS THIS MONTH:
  • Cotton (boll opening)
  • Groundnut (pod filling)
  • Bajra (harvest)
  • Paddy (panicle)
  • Monsoon withdrawal

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION
================================================================================
  • Summer ploughing should be completed by now if not already done; deep ploughing (20-25 cm) improves water infiltration
  • For heavy black cotton soils, apply organic matter / FYM (Farm Yard Manure) 10-15 tonnes per hectare at least 2-3 weeks before sowing
  • Level the field using a leveller to ensure uniform irrigation and prevent waterlogging
  • In Saurashtra, apply gypsum 250 kg per hectare for sodic soil reclamation before sowing
  • Conduct soil testing: pH should be 6.5-7.5 for most crops; apply lime if pH < 6.0
  • Prepare raised beds (15-20 cm high) for vegetable crops to improve drainage during monsoon

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • NO main field sowing in September — too late for Kharif and too early for Rabi
  • Rabi crop nursery preparation (wheat, mustard, gram) can begin in last week in South Gujarat
  • Vegetable nursery for Rabi vegetables (tomato, brinjal, chilli) — start raised beds in last week
  • Fodder jowar/maize for late green fodder — possible in South Gujarat until mid-September
  • Focus shifts from sowing to crop protection and harvest preparation

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Cotton: critical irrigation at boll opening stage if dry spell occurs; every 10-12 days if no rain
  • Groundnut: final irrigation at pod filling stage (70-80 days) if rain decreases; do NOT irrigate after 90 days
  • Bajra: no irrigation needed; rainfed crop approaching maturity
  • Paddy: maintain 5-7 cm water layer at panicle initiation and boot stage; critical for grain setting
  • Paddy: if rain decreases, provide 50mm irrigation at flowering stage to prevent spikelet sterility
  • All crops: as monsoon withdraws, transition from rainfed to protective irrigation

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Cotton: reduce nitrogen after boll opening; excess N delays maturity and reduces fibre quality
  • Groundnut: no more fertilizer after gypsum application; focus on harvest timing
  • Bajra: no more fertilizer; basal and top dressings should be complete by now
  • Paddy: third top dressing (30 kg urea per hectare) at panicle initiation stage (50-60 days after transplanting)
  • Paddy: apply 25 kg potash per hectare at flowering for better grain filling
  • Rabi preparation: apply FYM 10-15 tonnes per hectare for fields being prepared for wheat/mustard/gram

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Cotton: pink bollworm — peak trap catches; spray Emamectin benzoate 0.4g/litre or Spinosad 0.3ml/litre if threshold crossed
  • Cotton: American bollworm — late instar larvae management; spray Indoxacarb 0.5ml/litre if Bt sprays fail
  • Cotton: aphid and whitefly resurgence — spray Buprofezin 1.5ml/litre if whitefly exceeds 5 per leaf
  • Groundnut: Tikka leaf spot — spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre if circular spots appear on leaves
  • Groundnut: white grub — final control; if damage is severe, mark affected areas and plan for next season
  • Paddy: stem borer (white ear) — if white empty panicles appear, it is too late for chemical control; remove affected plants
  • Paddy: bacterial leaf blight — spray Streptocycline 1g + Copper oxychloride 2.5g per litre if lesions appear

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • Early bajra (sown in June) ready for harvest in Saurashtra and North Gujarat; harvest when ears turn brown
  • Early cotton (sown in May) — first picking may begin in South Gujarat by month-end
  • Groundnut: NOT ready for harvest yet; will be ready in October
  • Paddy: NOT ready for harvest yet; in flowering/panicle stage
  • Fodder crops: final cut for green fodder before winter demand increases

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • Clean and dry harvested produce immediately; moisture content should be reduced to safe levels (8-10% for grains, 10-12% for oilseeds)
  • Use tarpaulins or cemented drying yards to avoid aflatoxin contamination in groundnut and cotton
  • Store in properly ventilated godowns; use neem leaves or safe storage pesticides
  • For cotton, ginning should be done within 15 days of picking to preserve fibre quality
  • Grade produce before selling: separate A, B, and C grades for better market prices
  • Maintain post-harvest records for traceability and insurance claims

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • Southwest monsoon begins withdrawing from North Gujarat by mid-September; from South Gujarat by end of month
  • Day temperatures: 30-34°C; nights 22-26°C; humidity begins to decrease as monsoon withdraws
  • Post-monsoon thunderstorms (retreating monsoon) can bring heavy rain; watch for crop lodging in cotton
  • Clear skies after withdrawal favour harvest operations; but night temperatures may drop suddenly
  • IMD Monsoon Withdrawal Advisory: monitor withdrawal dates; plan irrigation and Rabi preparations accordingly

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PM-KISAN installment verification: ensure Aadhaar seeding is complete before 15th of the month
  • Soil Health Card scheme: collect updated cards from local Krushi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or ATMA office
  • PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana) enrollment: last date for Kharif crops is usually 31st July; for Rabi crops, 15th December
  • Gujarat State Farm Machinery Scheme: subsidy applications for tractor/power tiller due at district agriculture office
  • Drip irrigation subsidy under GGRC (Gujarat Green Revolution Company): submit estimates before month-end
  • Horticulture Mission for North East & Himalayan States (HMNEH) — nursery subsidy claims
  • Contact your nearest VLE (Village Level Entrepreneur) for eNAM registration and online mandi selling
