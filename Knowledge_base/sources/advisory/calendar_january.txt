GUJARAT FARMING CALENDAR — JANUARY (માગશર / પોષ (Magshar / Posh))
================================================================================

OVERVIEW:
January is a critical month for Gujarat agriculture. The state's diverse agro-climatic zones — Saurashtra, North Gujarat, South Gujarat, and Kutch — each have specific crop activities during this period. This calendar provides detailed, week-by-week guidance for farmers.

CROPS IN FOCUS THIS MONTH:
  • Wheat (Rabi)
  • Mustard / Rapeseed
  • Gram (Chana)
  • Potato
  • Cumin

================================================================================
1. SOIL PREPARATION
================================================================================
  • Summer ploughing should be completed by now if not already done; deep ploughing (20-25 cm) improves water infiltration
  • For heavy black cotton soils, apply organic matter / FYM (Farm Yard Manure) 10-15 tonnes per hectare at least 2-3 weeks before sowing
  • Level the field using a leveller to ensure uniform irrigation and prevent waterlogging
  • In Saurashtra, apply gypsum 250 kg per hectare for sodic soil reclamation before sowing
  • Conduct soil testing: pH should be 6.5-7.5 for most crops; apply lime if pH < 6.0
  • Prepare raised beds (15-20 cm high) for vegetable crops to improve drainage during monsoon

================================================================================
2. SOWING / PLANTING DATES
================================================================================
  • NO sowing for main Kharif or Rabi field crops in January — this is mid-season for Rabi
  • Late sowing of potato (second crop) possible in South Gujarat until 15th January with irrigation
  • Summer vegetable nursery preparation (tomato, brinjal, chilli) can begin in last week of January in South Gujarat
  • Green gram (moong) summer sowing nursery raised beds preparation in Saurashtra

================================================================================
3. IRRIGATION SCHEDULE
================================================================================
  • Wheat: critical irrigation at CRI (Crown Root Initiation) stage — 20-25 days after sowing; apply 50mm water
  • Wheat: second irrigation at tillering stage (40-45 days); third at boot leaf/flag leaf stage (60-70 days)
  • Mustard: one irrigation at flowering stage (50-60 days) if no rain; avoid excess water
  • Gram: generally rainfed; one protective irrigation during pod filling if dry spell exceeds 15 days
  • Potato: maintain soil moisture at 80% field capacity; irrigation every 7-10 days depending on soil type
  • Cumin: light irrigation every 10-12 days; avoid waterlogging — cumin is highly sensitive to root rot

================================================================================
4. FERTILIZER TIMING & DOSES
================================================================================
  • Wheat: first top dressing of nitrogen (40-50 kg urea per hectare) at CRI stage (20-25 days)
  • Wheat: second nitrogen top dressing (40 kg urea per hectare) at tillering (40-45 days)
  • Mustard: apply 30 kg urea per hectare at flowering stage if crop looks pale
  • Gram: if basal dose was insufficient, apply 20 kg DAP per hectare at early branching stage
  • Potato: apply 50 kg urea per hectare at stolon formation (30 days); second dose at tuber initiation (45 days)
  • Cumin: apply 20 kg urea per hectare at 30 days after sowing; second dose at 50 days if needed

================================================================================
5. PEST SCOUTING DATES
================================================================================
  • Wheat: monitor for aphids and yellow rust (Puccinia striiformis); yellow rust appears as yellow-orange streaks
  • Wheat: if yellow rust appears, spray Propiconazole 1ml per litre immediately; alert agriculture department
  • Mustard: watch for sawfly and aphids; spray Dimethoate 2ml per litre if aphids exceed 10 per plant
  • Gram: pod borer (Helicoverpa armigera) monitoring; spray HaNPV or NSKE 5% if eggs found
  • Potato: early blight (Alternaria solani) — spray Mancozeb 2.5g per litre at first sign of brown spots
  • Cumin: wilt and blight complex — drench with Carbendazim 1g + Mancozeb 2.5g per litre if symptoms appear

================================================================================
6. HARVESTING WINDOWS
================================================================================
  • Early sown bajra (fodder) may be harvested for green fodder in South Gujarat
  • Green gram (moong) sown in October-November reaches maturity in late January in South Gujarat
  • Mustard: early varieties may be ready for harvest in last week of January in South Gujarat
  • Pre-harvest desiccation of moong: spray 20% common salt solution 3 days before harvest to aid uniform drying

================================================================================
7. POST-HARVEST OPERATIONS
================================================================================
  • Clean and dry harvested produce immediately; moisture content should be reduced to safe levels (8-10% for grains, 10-12% for oilseeds)
  • Use tarpaulins or cemented drying yards to avoid aflatoxin contamination in groundnut and cotton
  • Store in properly ventilated godowns; use neem leaves or safe storage pesticides
  • For cotton, ginning should be done within 15 days of picking to preserve fibre quality
  • Grade produce before selling: separate A, B, and C grades for better market prices
  • Maintain post-harvest records for traceability and insurance claims

================================================================================
8. WEATHER WATCH
================================================================================
  • January is the coldest month in Gujarat; minimum temperatures can drop to 5-10°C in North Gujarat and Kutch
  • Frost risk in North Gujarat (Banaskantha, Patan, Mehsana) and Kutch — protect sensitive crops with light irrigation or smoke
  • Day temperatures: 25-30°C; Night: 8-15°C; ideal for wheat, mustard, gram, and cumin
  • Watch for unseasonal rain — January rain can cause lodging in mustard and fungal diseases in gram
  • IMD Cold Wave warnings: if minimum temperature forecast is 4°C or below for 2+ days, activate frost protection

================================================================================
9. GOVERNMENT SCHEME DEADLINES
================================================================================
  • PM-KISAN installment verification: ensure Aadhaar seeding is complete before 15th of the month
  • Soil Health Card scheme: collect updated cards from local Krushi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or ATMA office
  • PMFBY (Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana) enrollment: last date for Kharif crops is usually 31st July; for Rabi crops, 15th December
  • Gujarat State Farm Machinery Scheme: subsidy applications for tractor/power tiller due at district agriculture office
  • Drip irrigation subsidy under GGRC (Gujarat Green Revolution Company): submit estimates before month-end
  • Horticulture Mission for North East & Himalayan States (HMNEH) — nursery subsidy claims
  • Contact your nearest VLE (Village Level Entrepreneur) for eNAM registration and online mandi selling
